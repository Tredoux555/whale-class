3. Image Generation Workflow

This is your production line for generating consistent, high-quality illustrations for all Montessori materials.

The Master Prompt (paste this first in every new ChatGPT session)

ChatGPTI am a Montessori teacher creating educational materials for 4-year-old ESL learners. I need you to generate images with these consistent rules for EVERY image: - Simple cartoon illustration style - Bold, bright, saturated colours - Pure white background (#FFFFFF) - No text, no letters, no words anywhere on the image - No shadows or 3D effects - Clean black outlines - Cute and friendly style appropriate for young children - Each object shown in isolation (not part of a scene) - Realistic proportions (a cup should look like a cup, not abstract) - Consistent art style across all images in this session Please confirm you understand, then I will request images one at a time.

Generating a Complete Set

Once ChatGPT confirms, request each image individually. Be specific about what you need:

Example PromptsGenerate a cartoon image of a red fish for a children's flashcard Generate a cartoon image of a wooden ship sailing on blue water for a children's flashcard Generate a cartoon image of a bright yellow sun with rays of light and a friendly smile for a children's flashcard Generate a cartoon image of a modern white wind turbine with three blades on a green hill for a children's flashcard

Quality Control Checklist

  • White background — no gradients, no colour bleed
  • No text anywhere on the image
  • Object is centred and fills most of the frame
  • Style matches the rest of the set
  • Object is clearly identifiable — a 4-year-old could name it
  • Colours are bright and saturated, not pastel or muted
Pro Tip

If an image comes out slightly different in style, say: "Regenerate this in the exact same style as the previous images — same line weight, same colour saturation, same level of detail." ChatGPT will adjust.

From Image to Flashcard (Canva Workflow)

1
Save all images from ChatGPT to a folder on your computer called "Montree Materials"
2
Open Canva → Create a design → Custom size → 4 x 6 inches (standard flashcard)
3
Upload your image → drag it to fill the top two-thirds of the card
4
Add text below: English word in large bold font (e.g. "shell"), Chinese word underneath in smaller font (e.g. "贝壳")
5
Add a coloured border: Green for the current series, pink for Pink Series, blue for Blue Series
6
Duplicate the page for each new card — just swap the image and text. Keeps everything consistent.
7
Download as PDF (Print) → high quality → ready to laminate

The Formula for Catchy Children's Songs

The Science

Research shows hit children's songs share these elements: upbeat mood, simple catchy melodies, heavy repetition, call-and-response sections, physical actions tied to words, and the ABABCB structure (verse, chorus, verse, chorus, bridge, chorus).

5. Montessori Material Types

Every material type you need to produce, with specifications for AI generation.

Three-Part Cards (Classified Cards)

AMI Standard

Three-part cards consist of: (1) a control card with picture + label, (2) a picture card (image only), and (3) a label card (text only). The child matches the picture to the label using the control card to self-correct.

Production spec: Generate the image in ChatGPT. Create three versions in Canva: image+text, image only, text only. Print on cardstock, laminate, cut. Colour-code the border by curriculum area.

Object-to-Picture Matching Cards

For Phonics

A card showing the picture that matches a physical miniature object. Used in sound games and three-period lessons. The child places the miniature on the matching picture card.

Production spec: Generate images that closely match your actual 1688 miniature objects. If your miniature shell is a spiral seashell, prompt ChatGPT for "spiral seashell" not just "shell."

Phonogram Cards

For Digraphs

Cards showing the digraph letters (sh, ch, th, etc.) in Montessori pink/blue/green with 4-6 picture examples on the back or as a separate matching set.

Production spec: Use Canva for the letter side (consistent Montessori font, red for consonants, blue for vowels). Use ChatGPT images for the picture side.

Booklet Pages

For Reading Practice

Simple folded booklets with one image per page and a word or simple sentence to read. The child reads the word/sentence and checks against the picture.

Production spec: Generate 6-8 images per booklet. Layout in Canva as an A4 page that folds into a booklet. Include dotted lines for the child to trace the word.

Cultural Classified Cards

For Weekly Themes

Sets of cards showing vocabulary related to the week's cultural theme. Example: Green Energy week needs cards for sun, wind, water, solar panel, windmill, dam, coal, oil, gas, pollution.

Production spec: Generate all images in one ChatGPT session for style consistency. Add English label + Chinese label + pinyin in Canva.

Sorting Cards

For Classification Work

Two or more category headers plus picture cards to sort. Example: "Renewable Energy" header and "Non-Renewable Energy" header, with picture cards the child sorts between them.

Production spec: Generate category headers as distinct cards. Use green border for one category, red for the other. Picture cards have no border — the child decides where they go.

8. Weekly Production Workflow

How to integrate material production into your weekly planning without losing your mind.

The Golden Rule

Don't try to build everything at once. Produce materials one week ahead. Every Sunday evening, generate next week's materials. It takes 1-2 hours once you're in the rhythm.

Sunday Evening (1-2 hours)

1
Check next week's theme in the Montree weekly plan
2
Open ChatGPT — paste the master prompt — generate all flashcard images for the week (usually 8-12 images, takes ~20 minutes)
3
Open Canva — drop images into flashcard templates — add English + Chinese labels — export as PDF (takes ~20 minutes)
4
Open Suno — generate the week's theme song and/or phonics song (takes ~20 minutes including revisions)
5
Ask Claude — write the song lyrics, booklet text, and any worksheet content. Claude handles the curriculum thinking; you handle the production.
6
Print and laminate Monday morning before class

How this content feeds into the Montree platform and drives school adoption.

The Funnel

Layer 1 — Free downloads: All material sets available as free PDFs on Montree. No login required. Teachers discover them through Google, WeChat shares, Montessori Facebook groups, and word of mouth.

Layer 2 — Free account: To access the full organised library, song downloads, and weekly planning integration, teachers create a free Montree account. Now you have their email.

Layer 3 — School subscription: Teachers who love the materials explore the platform. They see curriculum tracking, AI parent reports, the parent portal. They tell their principal. Principal signs up for the $1,000/month school subscription.

The Copyright Question

Montessori curriculum is not copyrighted. The method, the progression, the material types — these are public domain, developed over 100 years ago by Maria Montessori and refined by AMI. What is copyrighted is specific commercial implementations (Nienhuis product designs, commercial card sets with specific illustrations). Since you're generating all original illustrations and writing all original text, everything you produce is yours to share freely. The songs are original compositions. The images are AI-generated originals. The curriculum structure is public domain AMI methodology. You're clear.
