# AMI Letter Groupings & Buildable Word Lists

## The AMI Sequence

Letters are introduced in groups designed so children can build real words as early as possible. Each group adds new letters AND a new vowel (except Group 6).

---

## Group 1: c, m, a, t

**New vowel:** a

**Words:** cat, mat, tam, cam, mac

**Why this group first:** With just 4 letters, children can already build real words. The sounds are distinct and easy to produce. "cat" and "mat" are familiar objects with available miniatures.

---

**Note:** This is where it EXPLODES. Eight letters unlock dozens of words. Children feel the power of reading.

## Curriculum Progression

### Phase 1: Sound Introduction (I Spy baskets)
- Introduce each letter's SOUND (not name) using miniature objects
- "I spy with my little eye something that starts with /k/..."
- Use physical objects from I Spy baskets

### Phase 2: Sandpaper Letters
- Trace while saying the sound
- Multi-sensory: see, touch, hear
- Introduce in AMI groups above

### Phase 3: Moveable Alphabet
- Build words from each group using loose letters
- Start with Group 1 (cat, mat) and progress
- Child builds the word, teacher reads it back

### Phase 4: Object-to-Word Matching
- Match miniature objects to their written word labels
- Start with Group 1 words, expand as groups are learned

### Phase 5: CVC Word Building
- Systematic word building across all vowel groups
- Use word lists above as reference
- Pink Series cards (3-part cards with pictures)

### Phase 6: Word Bingo & Picture Bingo
- **FINAL STEP BEFORE READING**
- Picture Bingo: match spoken word to picture (auditory comprehension)
- Word Bingo: match spoken word to written word (visual decoding)
- Combines ALL skills: sound recognition, letter knowledge, word reading
- Uses all CVC words from Groups 1-6

### Phase 7: Reading
- Child is now ready to decode simple CVC books
- Pink Series readers (3-5 word sentences using CVC words)

## Physical Materials Needed

Per AMI group, you need:
1. **Sandpaper letters** for each new letter (lowercase, cursive or print)
2. **Miniature objects** representing buildable words (from 1688.com shopping list)
3. **Word labels** (printed from card generator)
4. **Moveable alphabet** letters (enough duplicates for word building)

See also: `Montessori-English-Materials-List.pdf` for full shopping checklist.
