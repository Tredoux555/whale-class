Programme Overview
A complete 10-week phonics programme for PreK 4 students (ages 2-6) who are well-versed in CVC words and ready to tackle blends, digraphs, and vowel phonograms. Designed for Chinese ESL learners in the Whale Class at Beijing International School.

10-Week Structure

Weeks 1–4 — Blends + Digraphs: Each week introduces 1 jewelry box (3 blends × 2 objects = 6 objects) paired with 1 consonant digraph. Kids learn that blends = hear both letters, digraphs = one new sound.

Weeks 5–7 — Phonograms + Review: Pivot to ending digraphs (ck, ng) and vowel teams (ee, oo, ai, oa, ea). The Apples and Bananas song becomes the anthem. No new blends — all energy on the GREEN level words from the dictionary.

Weeks 8–10 — Mastery + Celebration: No new sounds. Reading push, free word building, decodable sentences, and a parent showcase where every child reads from their dictionary.

Key Terms

Blend — Two consonants pushed together where you can still hear both sounds: st, sp, sn, bl, cl, fl, br, cr, dr, tr, gr, pl. These are the BLUE level words in the Whale Class dictionary.

Digraph — Two letters that combine to make one brand new sound: sh, ch, th, wh, ck, ng. "When two letters hold hands, they make a new sound!"

Phonogram / Vowel Team — Two vowels that walk together: ee, oo, ai, oa, ea. "When two vowels go walking, the first one does the talking!" These are the GREEN level words in the dictionary.

CVC — Consonant-Vowel-Consonant words (cat, dog, sun). The kids already know these well. These are the PINK level words in the dictionary.

Week-by-Week Plan
Each week: 1 jewelry box (3 blends × 2 objects) + 1 digraph/phonogram introduced in phonics circle. Full daily cycle: Circle → Box → Bingo → Moveable Alphabet → Dictionary.

Phase 3 — Mastery & Celebration

Weeks 8–10 · No new sounds — reading push, free building, and parent showcase

Week 8
Review all vowel teams + reading push
All Boxes Available — Free Choice
All jewelry boxes on shelves. Kids choose independently. Focus shifts to reading: match objects to written labels without picture support. Can they read the word cold?
Phonogram Sentences
Build sentences together! "The goat is on the boat." "I see a bee in the tree." Each sentence uses at least 2 phonograms.

Bingo: WORD bingo (not pictures!) — can they read the words?
Moveable Alphabet
Free sentence building. Challenge: 3-word sentences minimum.
Dictionary
Kids add any new words they discover on their own
Week 9
Consolidation + confidence
Mixed Challenge Boxes
Remix objects across boxes — put a sheep next to a spoon next to a train. Can they sort by sound type? Blend vs digraph vs vowel team? This is the mastery test.
Decodable Reading
Simple decodable readers using ONLY sounds they've learned. "The sheep took the train in the rain."

Game: Spelling bee (gentle!) — say a word, kids build it with moveable alphabet.
Moveable Alphabet
Dictation — teacher says word, kids build it. The ultimate check.
Dictionary
Final entries — aim for every child to have 30+ words

🎓 Week 10 — Grand Celebration

All 12 boxes displayed. Sound wall poster with every blend and digraph. Kids give parents a "tour" — pick 3 boxes, show the objects, read the words. Apples & Bananas finale with ALL vowel variations. Each child reads 5 words from their dictionary. Certificates: "I can read __ sounds!" 🐋 Every Whale takes their dictionary home — their first real book they can READ.

Master Prompt — Copy This
STYLE GUIDE FOR ALL IMAGES:

You are creating a consistent set of children's book illustrations for a kindergarten phonics programme called "Whale Class" at Beijing International School. ALL images must follow these rules:

Art Style: Bright, friendly children's book illustration. Clean black outlines (not too thick). Soft pastel colour palette — think watercolour meets digital illustration. Simple compositions with one clear focal point. Characters are slightly chunky/rounded, making them feel approachable and toylike.

Background: White or very light solid colour. NO complex scenery or detailed backgrounds. Objects/characters float or sit on a minimal ground line. This is so the images work well when printed on cards.

Character Consistency: These characters appear across multiple images and MUST look the same each time:
— Snail: Brown spiral shell, soft yellow-green body, two eye stalks, friendly smile
— Whale: Friendly blue whale, slightly cartoonish, small flippers, gentle smile, spout on top
— Crab: Red-orange crab, two big claws, four small legs each side, dot eyes, smiling
— Sheep: Fluffy white wool, black face and legs, big round eyes, pink cheeks
— King: Gold crown, purple royal robe, friendly round face, rosy cheeks
— Duck: Yellow body, orange beak and feet, small wing bump, round body
— Bee: Yellow and black stripes, small transparent wings, cute face, tiny
— Goat: White fur, two small horns, little beard, gentle eyes
— Dragon: Green body, small wings, friendly face, tiny teeth showing, chubby
— Frog: Bright green, big round eyes, wide mouth, sitting pose

Mood: Silly, warm, and joyful. These images reward children for reading — they should make kids laugh or smile. Exaggerated expressions are great.

Technical: Square format (1:1 ratio). High resolution. No text in the images — text will be added separately on the printed cards. Each image should be clearly readable at approximately 7cm × 7cm print size.

IMPORTANT: When generating a sequence of images (e.g. 3-4 cards telling a story), the characters MUST look identical across all cards in that sequence. Same colours, same proportions, same design. Only the pose and context changes.

How to Generate

Step 1: Copy the Master Style Prompt above and paste it as context/system prompt.
Step 2: For each story, generate the cards in sequence (Card 1, then Card 2, etc.) in the same session/conversation so the AI maintains character consistency.
Step 3: Generate the sentence puzzle images separately — these are single standalone images, not sequences.
Step 4: Save all images with naming convention: w01_seq1_card1.png, w01_seq1_card2.png, w01_sent1.png etc.

Total images needed:
— Sequencing cards: 15 stories × 3-4 cards = ~54 images
— Sentence puzzles: 16 sentences × 1 image = 16 images
— Grand total: ~70 images
