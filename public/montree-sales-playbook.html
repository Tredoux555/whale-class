The Psychology of Selling to Schools

Selling edtech to schools is nothing like selling a product. Schools are trust-first environments. The decision-makers are educators, not businesspeople. They've been burned by flashy tech that doesn't deliver. Here's how to get past their defenses.

The 6 Psychological Principles at Work

1. Authority + Insider Status

You're not a salesman — you're an AMS-trained Montessori teacher who built this because you needed it. That's your single most powerful asset. Every email must lead with this.

2. Pain-First, Not Feature-First

Never lead with what Montree does. Lead with the pain they already feel: hours lost to paper tracking, inconsistent curriculum delivery, parents wanting more visibility, new teachers not knowing the sequence.

3. Social Proof (Even Pre-Revenue)

You don't have paying clients yet, but you have: 316 works mapped, 11 sensitive periods coded, a working demo. Frame it as "built in a real classroom with real children." That IS your proof.

4. The Free Pilot — Reduce Risk to Zero

Schools hate risk. Offer a 30-day free pilot. They try it with one classroom. Zero commitment. The psychology: once teachers use it for 30 days, switching back to paper feels like regression. The tool sells itself.

5. Reciprocity Through Value-First

Before asking for anything, give something useful: a curriculum guide, a printable set, an insight about their program. This creates an obligation to reciprocate (with their time, a meeting, a trial).

6. Urgency Without Pressure

Don't fake scarcity. Use natural urgency: "We're onboarding our first 5 partner schools this semester" or "I'm selecting schools for the pilot before the spring term." Real, believable, motivating.

The Golden Rule

Always contact the KG Director first. They're the internal champion. If they love it, they'll sell it upward for you. If you go straight to the principal or owner, they'll defer to the KG Director anyway — but now you've lost the advantage of the KG Director feeling like they discovered it.

The 5-Touch Sequence

Research shows it takes 5-8 touches to convert a B2B lead in education. Here's the sequence:

Touch 1: The Warm Intro Email

Personalized. Reference something specific about their school. Offer a free resource. Ask for nothing except "would you be open to a quick look?"

Touch 2: The Value Drop (3 days later)

Send something genuinely useful: a Montessori curriculum checklist, a printable, an observation template. No ask attached. Pure value.

Touch 3: The WeChat/LinkedIn Connect (5 days later)

Connect on WeChat or LinkedIn with a brief personal message referencing your email. In China, WeChat is where real business happens.

Touch 4: The Demo Offer (7 days later)

"I recorded a 3-minute walkthrough of how [School Name]'s curriculum would look in Montree. Want me to send it?" Personalized demo > generic pitch.

Touch 5: The Direct Ask (10 days later)

"I'm selecting 5 schools for a free pilot this term. Based on your Montessori program, HD Qingdao would be an ideal fit. Can we schedule 15 minutes this week?"
