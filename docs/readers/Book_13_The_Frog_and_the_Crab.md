# Pink Reader · Book 13 — "The Frog and the Crab"

**Gate:** read after **Lesson 51** (Phase 3 — the r-blends: `br cr dr fr gr pr
tr`).
**Pages:** cover + 12 story pages. **One to three short sentences per page.**

*Working title in the series plan was "The Green Frog" — but `green` carries
the `ee` vowel team, which is not taught until the Green Phase. Retitled to
feature the **frog** (the cast member who enters here) and a **crab** — both
r-blend words, so the title itself teaches the gate.*

**Word inventory (every word in the book):**
`A · frog · sat · in · the · grass · had · no · pal · crab · ran · to · can ·
I · be · said · Yes · is · glad · hop · grab · not · it · hid · Where · got ·
and · fun · nap`
— 29 words total.

**Heart words used:** `A` (L8), `the` (L11), `to` (L12), `I` (L10), `be`
(L19), `said` (L22), `Where` (L24), `and` (L13). All introduced well before the
gate. ✓
**Decodability check:** the new r-blends are the title and the verbs — `fr` in
`frog`, `cr` in `crab`, `gr` in `grass` and `grab`. `glad` carries an l-blend
(L50); `grass` also uses FLSZ doubling (L41). `no` and `Yes` are decodable
(n-o, y-e-s). `sat`, `had`, `ran`, `hid`, `got` are decodable past-tense words.
**Nothing outruns the child's phonics — and no vowel-team words** (the lesson
lists `green`/`bring` under r-blends, but `ee` and `ng` belong to later phases,
so they are kept out). ✓

**The shape:** Book 13 brings the **frog** into the cast and makes the r-blends
its story. A lonely **frog** in the **grass** meets a **crab** — and the small
ache of *"the frog had no pal"* is a feeling every child knows. The mirrored
cheer pages (*"Hop, frog, hop!"* / *"Grab, crab, grab!"*) celebrate what each
new friend can do, and then a gentle hide-and-grab game seals the friendship.
It closes warm, two new friends resting in the grass. **The frog's loneliness
and then its joy live in the art.**

## COVER

**Text:** The Frog and the Crab
*(small, below or above the art: "Pink Reader · Book 13")*

**Art brief:** A cheerful green **frog** sitting in soft grass, mid-friendly-
look toward a small round **crab** beside it — the crab with bright eyes and
two raised, waving claws, plainly saying hello. The two unlikely friends, side
by side in the green. Soft cream background, fresh warm light.

*Design the frog and the crab here — the frog: rounded, friendly, soft green,
big gentle eyes. The crab: small, round, warm orange, expressive claws. The
frog is recurring cast from here on; draw both consistently.*

**Composition:** Title large across the top in Andika lowercase (only "The",
"Frog", "Crab" capitalised as printed). Frog and crab centred in the grass.

## PAGE 1

**Text:** A frog sat in the grass. The frog had no pal.

**Art brief:** Establishing shot. A green **frog** sits alone in a wide patch
of soft **grass**, looking out at nothing in particular — not crying, just a
little quiet, a little wistful. Lots of empty green space around it to let the
aloneness read. Gentle, not sad.

**Composition:** Frog small and alone, centred in generous grassy space. Text
on a clean band along the bottom third.

---

## PAGE 2

**Text:** A crab! The crab ran to the frog.

**Art brief:** A small round orange **crab** comes scuttling sideways through
the grass straight toward the frog — claws up in a friendly wave, bright-eyed,
eager. The frog's head lifts, ears — well, the frog *perks up*, surprised and
hopeful.

**Composition:** Crab scuttling toward the frog, leading the eye. Bottom text
band.

---

## PAGE 3

**Text:** "Can I be a pal?" said the crab.

**Art brief:** The crab right in front of the frog now, one claw lifted in a
hopeful little offer, head tilted, friendly and kind. The frog looks back at
it — the wistfulness of page 1 melting into the start of a smile.

**Composition:** Crab and frog face to face, centred. Bottom text band.

---

## PAGE 4

**Text:** "Yes!" said the frog. The frog is glad.

**Art brief:** The frog's whole face opens into pure delight — the loneliness
gone. Maybe a happy little hop on the spot. The crab beams, claws up. A warm,
bright moment: a friend, found.

**Composition:** Frog mid-happy-bounce, crab cheering, both centred. Bottom
text band.

---

## PAGE 5

**Text:** The frog can hop. Hop, frog, hop!

**Art brief:** The frog showing the crab what it can do — a big joyful hop,
caught mid-air, legs spread, grinning. The crab watches, thrilled, claws up in
applause.

**Composition:** Frog mid-hop, crab watching below. Bottom text band. The cheer
is the fluent line.

---

## PAGE 6

**Text:** The crab can grab. Grab, crab, grab!

**Art brief:** The crab showing its own trick — claws opening and closing,
grabbing playfully at a blade of grass or a leaf, comic and proud. The frog
watches, delighted, applauding in its own froggy way.

**Composition:** Crab mid-grab, frog watching. Bottom text band. Mirror the
layout of page 5 — each friend shows its gift.

---

## PAGE 7

**Text:** The frog can hop. The crab can not grab it!

**Art brief:** A game has begun — the frog hops away just as the crab reaches
for it, claws closing on empty air. The frog grins back over its shoulder; the
crab gives a comic *missed-again!* look. Pure play, no meanness.

**Composition:** Frog hopping away, crab's claws snapping shut behind it.
Bottom text band.

---

## PAGE 8

**Text:** The frog hid in the grass.

**Art brief:** The frog tucked down low in the tall **grass**, only the top of
its head and two eyes peeking out, holding very still — playing hide-and-seek,
clearly delighted with itself.

**Composition:** Frog mostly hidden in the grass, just eyes showing. Bottom
text band.

---

## PAGE 9

**Text:** "Where is the frog?" The crab ran in the grass.

**Art brief:** The crab scuttling this way and that through the grass, claws
parting the blades, searching everywhere — head turning, comically thorough.
The hidden frog's eyes can just be seen peeking from one corner.

**Composition:** Crab mid-search, the frog's eyes hidden in a corner of the
frame. Bottom text band.

---

## PAGE 10

**Text:** The crab got the frog! Grab!

**Art brief:** The payoff of the game — the crab has found the frog and given
it the gentlest, most triumphant little claw-hug, both of them tumbling
together in the grass, laughing. Joyful, warm, completely friendly.

**Composition:** Crab and frog tangled happily together in the grass. Bottom
text band.

---

## PAGE 11

**Text:** The frog and the crab had fun.

**Art brief:** The two friends sitting close together in the grass, side by
side, both worn out and happy from the game — leaning a little into each other.
The frog of page 1 is not alone anymore.

**Composition:** Frog and crab side by side, centred, content. Bottom text
band. Echo page 1 — but now there are two.

---

## PAGE 12 — the button

**Text:** A frog and a crab in the grass. Nap, frog. Nap, crab.

**Art brief:** Final cosy spread. The **frog** and the **crab** are curled up
asleep together in the soft grass, two small shapes nested close in the warm
evening light. The frog has a friend now. The reader closes the book on the
quiet warmth of not being alone.

**Composition:** Frog and crab asleep together in the grass, centred, generous
calm space. Two short lines plus the two-line button, bottom band.

## Teacher note — how to use Book 13

**When:** hand it to the child during **Phase 3**, after Lesson 51 — once the
r-blends are introduced. Book 13 makes them its story, and brings the frog into
the cast for the rest of the series.

**The pattern:** the r-blend words carry the book — `frog`, `crab`, `grass`,
`grab`. As the child meets each, point out the consonant + `r` at the start and
blend them smoothly. The mirrored cheers (*"Hop, frog, hop!"* / *"Grab, crab,
grab!"*) give the fluent, predictable beats.

**The three reads:**

1. **Read 1 — decode.** Child sounds out each word. On the r-blend words, say
   the consonant, then the `r`, then the vowel — close together, no pause.
2. **Read 2 — smooth.** Child reads it again, "like talking." Read page 1
   quietly and a little wistful; pages 4 onward bright and warm.
3. **Read 3 — for the story.** Child reads it for fun. Talk about the pictures:
   *How does the frog feel on page 1? How does it feel on page 12? What
   changed?*

**The page-turn:** at page 9 (*"Where is the frog?"*) pause. Ask *"Can YOU find
the frog?"* — its eyes are hidden in the picture. Let the child spot it, then
turn the page.

**Comprehension question:** *"Why was the frog sad at the start?"* (Answer: it
had no friend.) Then: *"How did that change?"* — the crab came and asked to be
its pal. A gentle, important point for any classroom: a friendship can start
with one kind question.

**An r-blend extension:** after the three reads, find every r-blend in the book
— `frog`, `crab`, `grass`, `grab` — and have the child say each slowly, then
fast.

**ESL / Mandarin-L1 watch:** r-blends are the hardest blends for Mandarin-L1
children — the `r` is already the hardest single sound, and a blend doubles the
challenge. Keep the daily R/L minimal-pair drill running alongside this book.
Say the first consonant, then the `r`, then the vowel — slowly. Months of
patience; celebrate every small step.
