# Pink Reader · Book 2 — "The Pup in the Sun"

**Gate:** read after **Lesson 21** (all five short vowels + r, h). Single
consonants s–h, short a/e/i/o/u.
**Pages:** cover + 11 story pages. **One or two short sentences per page.**

**Word inventory (every word in the book):**
`Sam · has · a · pup · the · can · run · hop · tug · up · cat · get · not ·
nap · sun · is · hot · and · in`
— 19 words total.

**Heart words used:** `a` (L8), `the` (L11), `and` (L13). All introduced well
before the gate. ✓
**Decodability check:** every other word is pure CVC from letters taught by
Lesson 21 — `has` (h-a-s), `pup` (p-u-p), `run` (r-u-n), `hop` (h-o-p), `tug`
(t-u-g), `get` (g-e-t), `sun` (s-u-n), `hot` (h-o-t), `not` (n-o-t). `Sam` and
`cat` carry over from Book 1. **Nothing outruns the child's phonics.** ✓

**The shape:** the pattern returns — *"The pup can ___"* alternating with an
imperative cheer *"Run, pup, run!"* — so the child predicts and feels fluent.
Page 7 breaks the frame: the bouncy pup tries to wake the napping cat. The cat
(the calm one from Book 1) just naps on — no rejection, only a gentle joke.
The pup runs its energy out in the warm sun and learns the cat's wisdom: the
sun is the best place to nap. The words stay flat; **the pup's whole body does
the storytelling** — eager → puzzled → joyful → pleasantly worn out → cosy.

## Teacher note — how to use Book 2

**When:** hand it to the child after **Lesson 21**, once all five short vowels
and `r` and `h` are secure. It is the second milestone book — *"now you know
every short vowel, here is a bigger story."*

**The pattern:** pages 2–6 alternate *"The pup can ___"* with the cheer
*"___, pup, ___!"* Let the child hear the rhythm — by page 5 most children
predict the cheer and read it almost fluently. **That feeling of fluency is the
goal.** Don't rush past the pattern pages.

**The three reads** (one sitting or three short ones):

1. **Read 1 — decode.** Child sounds out each word, finger under the text.
   Slow is fine. Every word is decodable — the child owns all of them.
2. **Read 2 — smooth.** Child reads it again, a little faster, "like talking."
   The cheers (*"Run, pup, run!"*) are a gift here — read them with bounce.
3. **Read 3 — for the story.** Child reads it for fun. Now talk about the
   pictures: *How does the pup feel here? Is the cat being unkind, or just
   sleepy?*

**The page-turn:** at page 7 (*"Up, cat! Up!"*) pause. Ask *"Do you think the
cat will get up?"* Let the child turn the page to find out.

**Comprehension question:** *"Where did the pup nap at the end?"* (Answer: in
the sun, next to the cat.) Then: *"How do we know the pup was not sad when the
cat did not play?"* — the child reads the pup's face and tilted ear on page 8,
not the words. That is the comprehension skill.

**ESL / Mandarin-L1 watch:** the final /t/ in `cat`, `hot`, `not`, `get` and
the final /p/ in `pup`, `hop`, `up` — Mandarin-L1 children drop word-final
stops. Use the tissue test from the lesson content. `run` and `r` generally —
keep the daily R/L minimal-pair drill going; this book gives many warm reps of
initial /r/ in a happy context.
