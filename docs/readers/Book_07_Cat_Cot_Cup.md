# Pink Reader · Book 7 — "Cat? Cot? Cup?"

**Gate:** read after **Lesson 40** (Phase 2 — all five short vowels drilled,
the mixed minimal-pair lesson done).
**Pages:** cover + 12 story pages. **One to two short sentences per page.**

*Working title in the series plan was "Cat? Cot? Cut!" — but `cut` is an
action, not a place, and never fits the hide-and-seek story. Retitled to
`Cat? Cot? Cup?` — all three words appear in the book, and the trio captures
the short-a / short-o / short-u contrast that Lesson 40 drills.*

**Word inventory (every word in the book):**
`Where · is · the · cat · said · Sam · on · cot · No · it · a · in · cap · cup ·
bed · bin · sat · was · sad · ran · up · to · sun · had · nap · not`
— 26 words total.

**Heart words used:** `Where` (L24), `the` (L11), `said` (L22), `a` (L8),
`was` (L14), `to` (L12). All introduced well before the gate. ✓
**Decodability check:** the five near-miss words deliberately walk every short
vowel — `cot` (o), `cap` (a), `cup` (u), `bed` (e), `bin` (i) — and `cat` (a)
is the word the child is hunting for. `No` is a decodable CV word (n-o). `said`,
`sat`, `ran`, `had` are decodable / heart past-tense words. **Nothing outruns
the child's phonics.** ✓

**The shape:** Book 7 is built directly on the Lesson 40 minimal-pair drill —
**the search itself is a vowel-discrimination game.** Sam hunts for the cat
and keeps finding look-alike, sound-alike words instead. The frame *"Is the cat
[place]? No! It is a [thing]."* repeats five times — the child predicts it and
reads fluently, while the eye and ear practise telling `cat` from `cot`, `cap`,
`cup`, `bed`, `bin`. Page 7 breaks the frame with a small low point — Sam can't
find the cat — and the resolution is the warm series joke: the cat was in the
sunniest, cosiest, most obvious spot the whole time.

## COVER

**Text:** Cat? Cot? Cup?
*(small, below or above the art: "Pink Reader · Book 7")*

**Art brief:** A playful puzzle of a cover — the **tabby cat** in the middle,
and around it three look-alike objects drawn at the same size and angle: a
small folding **cot**, and a **cup**. The cat sits among them with a knowing
half-smile, as if enjoying the confusion. Soft cream background, the three
shapes spaced out and clear.

**Composition:** Title large across the top in Andika lowercase (only "Cat",
"Cot", "Cup" capitalised as printed — each followed by its question mark). The
cat and objects arranged below with calm space between them.

**Composition:** Cat moving toward Sam, centred. Bottom text band.

## PAGE 10

**Text:** The cat was in the sun. The cat had a nap.

**Art brief:** A small flashback-feeling image, or the cat looking back over
its shoulder toward a warm, sunlit windowsill (or sun-pool on the floor) where
a cat-shaped dent is still visible in a cushion. The answer to the mystery: the
cat was in the cosiest, sunniest, most obvious spot — exactly where a cat
always is — while Sam searched all the odd places.

**Composition:** The cat foreground, the sunny napping spot behind it. Bottom
text band.

## PAGE 11

**Text:** The cat sat on Sam.

**Art brief:** The cat curled into a contented circle on Sam's lap. Sam, sitting
cross-legged, looks down at the cat — the sadness completely gone, replaced by
pure relief and warmth. One hand resting gently on the cat.

**Composition:** Sam and cat together, centred — the emotional heart of the
book. Bottom text band.

---

## PAGE 12 — the button

**Text:** Sam is not sad. The cat is on Sam! Nap, cat.

**Art brief:** Final cosy spread. Sam sits in the warm sunlight with the **cat**
curled and asleep on their lap — found, safe, home. Sam beams down at it. Soft
gold light, generous calm space. The reader closes the book on warmth, the
worry of pages 6–7 fully resolved.

**Composition:** Sam and cat together, centred, in the sun. Three short
sentences — keep them well spaced; the last, *"Nap, cat,"* is the quiet button.
Bottom text band.

## Teacher note — how to use Book 7

**When:** hand it to the child during **Phase 2**, after Lesson 40 — the mixed
minimal-pair lesson. Book 7 turns that drill into a story.

**The pattern:** pages 2–6 run the frame *"Is the cat [place]? No! It is a
[thing]."* five times. The five "things" — `cot`, `cap`, `cup`, `bed`, `bin` —
deliberately walk all five short vowels. As the child reads each one, they are
doing the Lesson 40 vowel-discrimination drill inside a story they care about.
Slow down on each *"No!"* — it is the child telling `cat` apart from its
look-alikes.

**The three reads:**

1. **Read 1 — decode.** Child sounds out each word, finger under the text.
   When they reach `cot`, `cap`, `cup`, `bed`, `bin` — ask: *"Is that the same
   as `cat`? What is different?"* The vowel.
2. **Read 2 — smooth.** Child reads it again, "like talking." The repeated
   frame should now flow.
3. **Read 3 — for the story.** Child reads it for fun. Talk about the
   pictures: *How does Sam feel on page 7? How does Sam feel on page 12?*

**The page-turn:** at page 7 (*"Sam was sad"*) pause. Ask *"Where do you think
the cat really is?"* Then turn the page.

**Comprehension question:** *"Where was the cat all along?"* (Answer: in the
sun, having a nap.) Then: *"Why could Sam not find it?"* — Sam looked in odd
places (a cup, a bin) and not the obvious cosy sunny spot. A gentle, funny
point.

**A minimal-pair extension:** after the three reads, lay out the picture cards
for `cat`, `cot`, `cap`, `cup`, `bed`, `bin` and play the listen-and-point game
from the Lesson 40 drill. The book has primed the child for exactly this.

**ESL / Mandarin-L1 watch:** this is THE vowel book. `cat` / `cot` / `cup` is
the a–o–u triangle; `bed` adds /ĕ/, `bin` adds /ĭ/. Mandarin-L1 children find
/ă/ and /ĕ/ hardest — mirror work on those two. Final stops: /t/ in `cat`,
`cot`, `it`, `not`, `sat`; /p/ in `cap`, `cup`, `up`, `nap`. Tissue test.
