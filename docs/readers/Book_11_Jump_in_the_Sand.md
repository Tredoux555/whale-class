# Pink Reader · Book 11 — "Jump in the Sand"

**Gate:** read after **Lesson 48** (Phase 3 — ending blends: `st`, `nd`, `mp`,
`nk`, `nt`, `lt`).
**Pages:** cover + 12 story pages. **One to three short sentences per page.**

**Word inventory (every word in the book):**
`Sam · and · the · pup · ran · to · sand · Jump · in · said · can · dig · Come ·
What · is · felt · lump · a · up · Will · it · be · rock · junk · shell · had ·
best · fun`
— 28 words total.

**Heart words used:** `and` (L13), `the` (L11), `to` (L12), `said` (L22),
`Come` (L47), `What` (L27), `a` (L8), `be` (L19). All introduced by the gate. ✓
**Decodability check:** the new ending blends are the load-bearing words —
`nd` in `sand`, `mp` in `jump`/`lump`, `lt` in `felt`, `nk` in `junk`, `st` in
`best`. `shell` uses `sh` (L42) + the FLSZ doubling rule (L41); `rock` uses
`ck` (L17). `Will` uses FLSZ doubling. `ran`, `said`, `felt`, `had` are
decodable past-tense words. **Nothing outruns the child's phonics.** ✓

**The shape:** Book 11 turns the new ending blends into a small treasure hunt.
Sam and the **pup** jump and dig in the **sand** — every key word is a blend
the child has just learned. The cheers (*"Jump, jump, jump!"* / *"Dig, dig,
dig!"*) give fluent beats. The hook is a guessing question — *"Will it be a
rock? Will it be junk?"* — and the payoff is a small, clean treasure: a
`shell`. It closes warm. **The growing excitement of the dig lives in the
art.**

## Teacher note — how to use Book 11

**When:** hand it to the child during **Phase 3**, after Lesson 48 — once the
ending blends are introduced. Book 11 puts them straight to work.

**The pattern:** the ending-blend words are the load-bearing words of the
story — `sand`, `jump`, `lump`, `felt`, `junk`, `best`. As the child meets
each, point out the two consonants at the end and say each sound, then quickly
together — never break the cluster into separate syllables. The cheers
(*"Jump, jump, jump!"* / *"Dig, dig, dig!"*) give the fluent, predictable
moments.

**The three reads:**

1. **Read 1 — decode.** Child sounds out each word. On the blend words —
   `sand`, `jump`, `lump`, `felt`, `junk`, `best` — say the last two sounds
   close together, no pause between them.
2. **Read 2 — smooth.** Child reads it again, "like talking." Let the
   excitement build in the voice from page 6 to page 10.
3. **Read 3 — for the story.** Child reads it for fun. Talk about the pictures:
   *What did Sam and the pup think the lump might be? What was it really?*

**The page-turn:** at page 9 (*"Will it be a rock? Will it be junk?"*) STOP.
Ask *"What do YOU think is in the sand?"* Take guesses, then turn to page 10.

**Comprehension question:** *"How did the pup know something was in the sand?"*
(Answer: the pup *felt* a lump under its paw.) Then: *"Why was Sam so happy
with the shell?"* — it was a real find, dug up with their own hands.

**An ending-blend extension:** after the three reads, find every two-consonant
ending in the book together — `sand`, `jump`, `lump`, `felt`, `junk`, `best` —
and have the child say each blend slowly, then fast.

**ESL / Mandarin-L1 watch:** ending blends are the Mandarin-L1 wall — Mandarin
has no consonant clusters, so `sand` may become "san" and `jump` may become
"jum" or "ju-mu". The fix: keep the mouth moving from one consonant straight
into the next, no reset, no extra vowel between them. Slow first, then quick.
Be patient — this takes months.
