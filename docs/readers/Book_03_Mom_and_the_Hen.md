# Pink Reader · Book 3 — "Mom and the Hen"

**Gate:** read after **Lesson 24** (single consonants complete through `b`,
`f`, `l`; all five short vowels).
**Pages:** cover + 11 story pages. **One or two short sentences per page.**

**Word inventory (every word in the book):**
`Mom · has · a · hen · the · is · fun · sit · in · pen · said · did · not ·
ran · can · hop · peck · where · on · log · bag · bed · big · red · hat · up ·
sat · and`
— 28 words total.

**Heart words used:** `a` (L8), `the` (L11), `and` (L13), `said` (L22),
`where` (L24). All introduced by the gate. ✓
**Decodability check:** every other word is pure CVC from letters taught by
Lesson 24 — `hen` (h-e-n), `pen` (p-e-n), `peck` (p-e-ck), `log` (l-o-g),
`bag` (b-a-g), `bed` (b-e-d), `big` (b-i-g), `red` (r-e-d), `fun` (f-u-n).
`did`, `ran`, `sat` are decodable CVC past-tense words. `Mom` is a decodable
proper name (m-o-m). **Nothing outruns the child's phonics.** ✓

**The shape:** Book 3 brings in dialogue — the heart word `said` unlocks it.
The fussy, funny **hen** will not sit in its pen. Mom does not force it; she
simply wonders aloud — *"Where can the hen sit?"* — and the heart word `where`
turns that into the page-turn hook. The middle pages (*"Not in the pen. Not on
a log…"*) build a small search the child can predict. The payoff is a gentle
joke: the hen picks the one spot nobody offered. Mom stays warm and patient
throughout — **her calm face is the emotional anchor of the book.**

## Teacher note — how to use Book 3

**When:** hand it to the child after **Lesson 24**, once `b`, `f`, `l` are
secure and the heart words `said` and `where` are on the binder ring. This is
the first reader with **dialogue** — point that out: *"see the marks? someone
is talking."*

**The pattern:** pages 7–8 run the search frame *"Not in/on a ___."* Let the
child feel the rhythm and predict it. Pages 4–5 give the doubled-cheer pattern
(*"Hop, hop, hop!"*) — bouncy reps of final /p/ and final /k/.

**The three reads:**

1. **Read 1 — decode.** Child sounds out each word, finger under the text.
   Slow is fine. Every word is decodable.
2. **Read 2 — smooth.** Child reads it again, "like talking." Read Mom's lines
   warmly — she is never cross.
3. **Read 3 — for the story.** Child reads it for fun. Talk about the pictures:
   *Is Mom angry with the hen? How can you tell?* (She is not — her face is
   fond and patient on every page.)

**The page-turn:** at page 6 (*"Where can the hen sit?"*) pause. Ask *"Where do
YOU think the hen will sit?"* Take a guess or two, then turn the page.

**Comprehension question:** *"Where did the hen sit in the end?"* (Answer: on
Mom's big red hat.) Then: *"Did Mom mind? How do we know?"* — the child reads
Mom's delighted face on pages 10–11. That is the comprehension skill: the words
never say Mom is happy; the picture does.

**ESL / Mandarin-L1 watch:** the final /n/ in `hen`, `pen`, `in`, `can` is easy
for Mandarin-L1 children (Mandarin has final /n/) — use these as confidence
words. Watch the final /t/ in `hat`, `sat`, `not`, `sit` and final /k/ in
`peck` — the tissue test. The /r/ in `red`, `ran` — keep the daily R/L drill.
