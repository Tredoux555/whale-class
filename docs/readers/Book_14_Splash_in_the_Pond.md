# Pink Reader · Book 14 — "Splash in the Pond"

**Gate:** read after **Lesson 53** (Phase 3 — the `tw`/`dw` blends and the
triple blends `str scr spl spr thr shr`).
**Pages:** cover + 12 story pages. **One to three short sentences per page.**

*Working title in the series plan was "Three Strong Steps" — but `three`
carries the `ee` vowel team and `strong` ends in `ng`, neither taught in the
Pink Phase. Retitled to a fully decodable story line that still shows off the
triple blends (`splash`, `sprint`).*

**Word inventory (every word in the book):**
`The · sun · is · hot · Sam · pup · run · to · pond · said · and · sprint ·
frog · in · can · splash · twist · flip · ran · fun · had · sat · big · a`
— 24 words total.

**Heart words used:** `The` (L11), `to` (L12), `said` (L22), `and` (L13), `a`
(L8). All introduced well before the gate. ✓
**Decodability check:** the new blends are the showpiece words — `spr` in
`sprint`, `spl` in `splash`, `tw` in `twist`. `sprint` also closes with the
`nt` ending blend (L48), `twist` with `st` (L47); `flip` is an l-blend (L50),
`frog` an r-blend (L51), `pond` an `nd` ending blend (L47) — every blend family
the child has met. `said`, `ran`, `had`, `sat` are decodable past-tense words.
**Nothing outruns the child's phonics — and no vowel-team or `ng` words** (the
lesson lists `three`/`strong`/`spring` under triple blends, but `ee` and `ng`
belong to later phases, so they are kept out). ✓

**The shape:** Book 14 is the last "new pattern" reader, and the triple blends
are exuberant words — so the book is exuberant. A hot day; Sam and the **pup**
`sprint` to a **pond** where the **frog** (cast, from Book 13) is already
splashing. The hook — *"Can the pup splash?"* — pays off in a page of pure,
joyful water. The cheers (*"Splash, frog, splash!"* / *"Splash, splash,
splash!"*) make the title word the most fluent word in the book. **The cool
relief and the joy live in the art and the spray.**

## Teacher note — how to use Book 14

**When:** hand it to the child during **Phase 3**, after Lesson 53 — once the
`tw` blend and the triple blends are introduced. Book 14 is the last reader
that brings a new pattern; after this comes the finale.

**The pattern:** the showpiece words are the triple blends — `sprint`,
`splash` — and `twist`. As the child meets each, point out that THREE
consonants are working together at the start, and each keeps its own sound. The
cheers (*"Splash, frog, splash!"* / *"Splash, splash, splash!"*) make the title
word the most fluent, joyful word in the book.

**The three reads:**

1. **Read 1 — decode.** Child sounds out each word. On `sprint` and `splash`,
   say all three starting consonants close together, then into the vowel — no
   pause, no extra sound.
2. **Read 2 — smooth.** Child reads it again, "like talking." Let the energy
   build from page 3 (the sprint) to page 10 (the big splash).
3. **Read 3 — for the story.** Child reads it for fun. Talk about the pictures:
   *How did Sam and the pup feel on page 1? How did they feel on page 10?*

**The page-turn:** at page 7 (*"Can the pup splash in the pond?"*) pause. Ask
*"Do you think the pup will jump in?"* Then turn to page 8.

**Comprehension question:** *"Why did Sam and the pup go to the pond?"*
(Answer: they were too hot, and the pond was cool.) Then: *"Who never wanted to
stop splashing?"* — the frog, on page 12. The pond is the frog's home; it is
happiest there.

**A triple-blend extension:** after the three reads, find every blend in the
book — `sprint`, `splash`, `twist`, `flip`, `frog`, `pond` — and say each
slowly, then fast. Triple blends are the boss-level: a child who reads
`sprint` and `splash` confidently is ready for the finale.

**ESL / Mandarin-L1 watch:** triple blends are the hardest clusters in the
Pink Phase — three consonants with no vowels between, against a Mandarin-L1
ear that wants a vowel after every consonant. Stretch the `s`, then slide
through each consonant into the vowel: "sss-p-l-ash." Slow first, then quick.
Many Mandarin-L1 children need months to master triple blends — that is
completely normal. There is no rush.
