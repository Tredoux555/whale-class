# Pink Reader · Book 8 — "The Bell on the Hill"

**Gate:** read after **Lesson 41** (Phase 2 close — the FLSZ doubling rule:
`f`, `l`, `s`, `z` double after a short vowel at the end of a word).
**Pages:** cover + 12 story pages. **One to three short sentences per page.**

**Word inventory (every word in the book):**
`The · bell · on · hill · Sam · has · a · red · is · fun · ran · up · puff ·
sat · top · of · pup · hit · fell · off · get · said · cat · will · got · and ·
had`
— 27 words total.

**Heart words used:** `The` (L11), `a` (L8), `of` (L15), `said` (L22), `and`
(L13). All introduced well before the gate. ✓
**Decodability check:** the FLSZ doubling rule is the new tool, and the book
showcases it — `bell` (b-e-ll), `hill` (h-i-ll), `puff` (p-u-ff), `fell`
(f-e-ll), `off` (o-ff), `will` (w-i-ll). Every other word is plain CVC — `red`
(r-e-d), `top` (t-o-p), `hit` (h-i-t), `got` (g-o-t). `ran`, `sat`, `hit`,
`fell`, `got`, `had` are decodable past-tense words. **Nothing outruns the
child's phonics.** ✓

**The shape:** Book 8 closes Phase 2 by putting the brand-new doubling rule to
work in a story. Sam carries a **bell** up a **hill** — the doubled words are
the title and the setting. The bouncy **pup** (established cast) knocks the
bell, it `fell` `off`, and rolls down — every key beat is an FLSZ word the
child has *just* learned. The hook is a clean question using the new heart-rule
word `will`: *"Will the pup get the bell?"* It closes warm: the bell back on
the hill, the friends together. **The chase and the rescue play out in the
art** — the words stay short and FLSZ-rich.

## Teacher note — how to use Book 8

**When:** hand it to the child at the **close of Phase 2**, after Lesson 41 —
the FLSZ doubling lesson. Book 8 puts that brand-new rule straight to work.

**The pattern:** the doubled-FLSZ words are deliberately the load-bearing words
of the story — `bell`, `hill`, `fell`, `off`, `puff`, `will`. As the child
meets each one, pause and ask: *"Why are there two letters at the end?"* — the
short vowel before it. The child is applying the Lesson 41 rule inside a story.
The cheer *"Puff, puff, puff!"* (page 2) is the fluent, predictable moment.

**The three reads:**

1. **Read 1 — decode.** Child sounds out each word. On `bell`, `hill`, `fell`,
   `off`, `puff`, `will` — note that the doubled letters say ONE sound.
2. **Read 2 — smooth.** Child reads it again, "like talking." Build a little
   urgency in the voice on pages 5–9.
3. **Read 3 — for the story.** Child reads it for fun. Talk about the pictures:
   *Did the pup mean to knock the bell off? How can you tell?* (No — its
   surprised, flat-eared face on page 5 shows it was an accident.)

**The page-turn:** at page 9 (*"Will the pup get the bell?"*) STOP. Ask *"Do
you think the pup will catch it?"* Then turn to page 10.

**Comprehension question:** *"How did the bell end up safe at the end?"*
(Answer: the pup caught it, and Sam carried it back up and settled it so it
could not roll again.) Then: *"Was the pup in trouble? How do you know?"* — it
was an accident, and the friends simply fixed it together; no one is cross on
any page.

**An FLSZ extension:** after the three reads, find every doubled-letter word in
the book together (`bell`, `hill`, `fell`, `off`, `puff`, `will`) and have the
child say the short vowel that comes before each one. The book has primed
exactly this.

**ESL / Mandarin-L1 watch:** the doubled `ll`, `ff` say one sound — Mandarin-L1
children may want to say it twice ("be-l-l"). One finger-sweep, one sound.
Final stops: /t/ in `hit`, `sat`, `got`, `it`; /p/ in `up`, `top`. Tissue test.
The /r/ in `red`, `ran` — keep the daily R/L drill.
