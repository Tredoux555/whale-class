# Pink Reader · Book 15 — "The Big Pink Trip"

**Gate:** the **Pink Phase finale** — read after Lesson 53, when the whole Pink
Phase is complete. Full consolidation, full cast.
**Pages:** cover + 13 story pages. **One to three short sentences per page.**

*Working title in the series plan was "Sam Is a Reader" — but `reader` carries
the `ea` vowel team, not taught until the Green Phase. Retitled to a fully
decodable line; `Pink` is a quiet nod to the phase the child has just
finished.*

**Word inventory (every word in the book):**
`Sam · has · a · big · Pink · plan · it · is · trip · and · the · pup · ran · up ·
hill · cat · had · nap · on · top · frog · to · pond · splash · can · dig · sand ·
pig · got · in · mud · with · hen · sat · red · hat · fox · hid · box · ham ·
bun · Mom · Dad · fun · sun · not · hot · was · all · best`
— 50 words total.

**Heart words used:** `a` (L8), `and` (L13), `the` (L11), `to` (L12), `with`
(L34), `was` (L14). All introduced well before the finale. ✓
**Decodability check:** the finale draws on the whole Pink range — plain CVC
(`pig`, `mud`, `hat`, `bun`, `ham`), FLSZ doubling (`hill`, `all`), ending
blends (`pond`, `sand`, `best`, `Pink`), beginning blends (`plan`, `trip`,
`frog`), the triple blend (`splash`). `ran`, `had`, `got`, `hid`, `sat`, `was`
are decodable past-tense words. **No vowel-team words, no `ng` words, no `-s`
plurals, no `-ing`/`-ed`.** Every word can be sounded out by a child who has
finished the Pink Phase. ✓

**The shape:** Book 15 is the **finale** — a victory lap. Sam plans one big
trip, and the day revisits every place the series has loved: the **hill** (Book
8), the **pond** (Book 14), the **sand** (Book 11), the **mud** (Book 6), the
**hen** and a hat (Book 3), the **fox** and a box (Book 4). The whole cast is
together. A child who has read all fourteen earlier books will recognise every
callback with a glow of *I know this.* It closes on the biggest, warmest nap of
the series (Book 5) — and the quiet truth that the child can now read every
word of it, alone.

## Teacher note — how to use Book 15

**When:** hand it to the child at the **end of the Pink Phase**, after Lesson
53. This is the finale — the celebration of everything the child can now do.
Make it a real moment: *"you have finished the Pink Phase. You can read. Here
is your last Pink book — and it has everyone in it."*

**The pattern:** Book 15 has no single pattern — it is a tour. Its structure is
the day itself: a plan, the adventures, the picnic, the way home, the big nap.
Every place is a callback to an earlier book. As the child reads, pause and
ask: *"Do you remember this hill? This pond? This box?"* The recognition is the
reward for finishing the series.

**The three reads** — but Book 15 may earn a fourth:

1. **Read 1 — decode.** Child sounds out each word. By the finale most words
   should be quick — let the child feel how far they have come since Book 1.
2. **Read 2 — smooth.** Child reads it again, "like talking," with the joy of
   the day.
3. **Read 3 — for the story.** Child reads it for fun, finding every callback.
4. **Read 4 — aloud, to someone.** Let the child read Book 15 aloud to a
   parent, a teacher, or a younger friend. After fifteen books, reading TO
   someone is the natural next step — and this proud, full-cast finale is built
   for it.

**Comprehension question:** *"Which place on the trip did you like best?"* Then,
gently: *"You read this whole book by yourself. Every word. How does that
feel?"* — Book 15 is as much about the child as about Sam.

**A Pink Phase milestone:** consider a small classroom moment to mark it — the
child has decoded fifteen real books, every word earned. The Phase 1 close
ceremony in the lesson content (each child draws a favourite word) is a lovely
fit here too.

**What comes next:** a child who reads Book 15 confidently — including the
blends in `splash`, `trip`, `frog` — is ready for the **Blue Phase** (the
silent-`e`/VCe pattern, longer words, the first vowel teams). A child who is
not quite there yet: there is no rush. Re-read favourite Pink Readers, run more
Phase 3 review, and come back to Book 15 when it feels easy. Finishing well
matters more than finishing fast.

**ESL / Mandarin-L1 watch:** the finale touches every Pink sound — use it as a
final check. Listen for the final stops (`hat`, `pig`, `mud`, `box`), the
blends (`plan`, `trip`, `pond`, `sand`, `splash`), and the `th` of `with`,
`the`. Anything still soft is simply the next thing to practise — note it, and
carry it into the Blue Phase. The child has done something real and hard, and
for a Mandarin-L1 learner, something exceptional. Celebrate it.
