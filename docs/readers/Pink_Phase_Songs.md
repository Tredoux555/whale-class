# Pink Phase Sound Songs

A short circle-time song for **every lesson of the Pink Phase** — UFLI lessons
5 to 53, forty-nine songs. Each one is built around that lesson's target
sound, and each one sings a handful of that lesson's words.

---

## What these are, and why

A sound lands in the **ear and the body** before it ever lands on paper. Sing
the song at the very start of the lesson: the children hear the target sound,
say it, and move it — then they go to the sandpaper letter and the word work
already primed. Two or three minutes at the circle. Do the movement every
time. Sing the same song all week.

**These are heard, not decoded.** The children *sing* a song; they do not read
it. So — unlike the Pink Readers — the connecting lyrics use simple, natural,
ESL-friendly English. What is drilled is the lesson's **target sound** and a
handful of its **words**. Every song's header lists the lesson words it sings.

**They are Suno-ready.** Every song shares one base music style (below); each
song then adds a short accent line. Paste *base + accent* into Suno to generate
the track. Keep the whole set in one musical family so it feels like one
songbook.

### The base Suno style prompt — use for every song

> Bright, warm children's singalong for a kindergarten circle. Gentle acoustic
> ukulele or soft nylon-string guitar, light hand percussion — claps, shaker,
> a woodblock. One clear, friendly, unhurried voice. Nursery-rhyme simple: a
> short, easy melody a four-year-old can sing back after one listen. Very
> repetitive. Slow enough that every word is crisp. No competing backing
> vocals, no busy production.

Then add each song's own **Suno accent** line.

### ESL / Mandarin-L1 note

Every song's **movement** is tied to how the sound is *made* — the snake's
hiss for /s/, the tissue-flutter pop for /p/, the hand on the throat for the
voiced sounds. For Mandarin-L1 children this matters most on the hard sounds:
the final stops, /r/, /th/, /v/, /z/. The movement is not decoration — it is
the articulation lesson, set to music.

### Lesson 5 · `s` — /s/ — "The Snake Says Ssss"

**Sound:** /s/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the snake &nbsp;·&nbsp; voice off, long
**Words sung:** the song features the /s/ keyword words — *snake · sun · sock*

**Suno accent** — a touch slower and softer; hold every *ssss* long and breathy,
like a hush.

**Lyrics**

Ssss, ssss, ssss,
that is what the snake says.
Ssss, ssss, ssss —
soft and long, like this.

A snake in the sun, ssss.
A snake on a sock, ssss.
What does the snake say?

Ssss, ssss, ssss,
can you say it too?
Ssss, ssss, ssss —
s, s, s!

**Movement** — one hand becomes a snake, wiggling slowly through the air the
whole time the /s/ is held. Long, smooth, hissing. The /s/ never gets a vowel
after it — no "suh," just *ssss*.

### Lesson 7 · `t` — /t/ — "Tick-Tock, T!"

**Sound:** /t/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the ticking clock &nbsp;·&nbsp; voice off, sharp, short
**Words sung:** *top · tap · at · sat*

**Suno accent** — crisp march tempo; every /t/ clipped and clean, like a tiny
drum tap.

**Lyrics**

T, t, t — tick-tock, t!
A clock goes t, and so do we.

T for the top that spins around.
T for the tap, tap, tapping sound.
Sat on a mat — hear the t at the end!

T, t, t — tick-tock, t!

**Movement** — one finger taps the air sharply on each /t/. On *sat*, *mat*,

Mandarin-L1 children drop. Hold a tissue up: a real /t/ makes it flutter.

### Lesson 12 · `d` — /d/ — "D for the Dog"

**Sound:** /d/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the dog &nbsp;·&nbsp; voiced — throat buzz, short
**Words sung:** *dog · dad · dip*

**Suno accent** — friendly and warm; each /d/ a soft little drum-thud with a

**Lyrics**

D, d, d — hand on your throat,
feel it buzz on every note.

D for the dog — woof! — wagging its tail.

### Lesson 17 · `ck` — /k/ — "Two Letters, One Kick"

**Words sung:** *kick · sock · pick · stick · pack · dock*

**Suno accent** — punchy, with a strong beat to land the /k/ hard at the *end*

**Lyrics**

C-k, c-k — at the end!

### Lesson 20 · `r` — /r/ — "Rrr Goes the Engine"

**Sound:** /r/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the running engine &nbsp;·&nbsp; lips round, tongue curls
**Words sung:** *run · red · rat · rug · rock*

**Suno accent** — slow and steady, with a low rolling engine hum; give the
/r/ plenty of room.

**Lyrics**

R, r, r — start the engine,

R for run — run, run, run!

**Sound:** /ă/ /ĕ/ /ĭ/ /ŏ/ /ŭ/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the five vowels &nbsp;·&nbsp; vowel review
**Words sung:** *cat · pet · pig · pot · cup*

**Suno accent** — clear and steady, a five-step melody that climbs through the
vowels.

**Lyrics**

A says /ă/ — a cat, a cat!
E says /ĕ/ — a pet, like that.
I says /ĭ/ — a pig in a pen,
O says /ŏ/ — a pot, and then —
U says /ŭ/ — a cup, oh yum!

Five little vowels, here they come:
/ă/, /ĕ/, /ĭ/, /ŏ/, /ŭ/ —
five little vowels, me and you!

**Movement** — hold up one finger for each vowel as it is sung — five fingers
by the end. This is the vowel-discrimination song; for Mandarin-L1 children
linger on /ă/ and /ĕ/, the two hardest, with the mirror.

### Lesson 34 · review — "We Know the Alphabet"

**Sound:** every letter &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** Phase 1 complete &nbsp;·&nbsp; the closing song

We learned the letters, every one!

# Phase 2 — Vowel Power (Lessons 35–41)

Phase 2 drills the five short vowels to automaticity, one at a time, then
closes with the FLSZ doubling rule. These songs are **faster** — the children
already know the sounds; now they sing them quick and sure.

---

### Lesson 35 · short A — /ă/ — "Fast A!"

**Sound:** short /ă/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** apple &nbsp;·&nbsp; deep-dive — automaticity
**Words sung:** *cat · hat · man · bag · jam*

**Suno accent** — quicker and snappier than the Lesson 6 song; a brisk,
confident clip.

**Lyrics**

A, a, a — say it fast!
A, a, a — short a, at last!

Cat! Hat! A man with a bag!
Jam! Tap! Wag the dog's tag!
A, a, a — quick, quick, quick!

A, a, a — say it fast,
short a words go zooming past:
a, a, a!

**Movement** — clap a quick, steady beat — one clap per /ă/ word — and speed up
each verse. The mouth still opens wide for every /ă/; fast does not mean
sloppy. The hardest vowel for Mandarin-L1 ears — mirror it.

---

### Lesson 36 · short I — /ĭ/ — "Quick Little I"

**Sound:** short /ĭ/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the itsy insect &nbsp;·&nbsp; deep-dive — automaticity
**Words sung:** *pig · big · sit · win · six*

**Suno accent** — light, quick and tickly; a fast little patter.

**Lyrics**

I, i, i — quick and small!
I, i, i — the fastest of all.

A big pig! Sit in the pit!
Six! Win! A tiny bit!
I, i, i — quick, quick, quick!

I, i, i — quick and small,
short i words, we know them all:
i, i, i!

**Movement** — tiny quick claps — the sound is small, so the claps are small
and fast. A relaxed little smile for every /ĭ/. Keep the sit / seat contrast
alive for Mandarin-L1 children.

---

### Lesson 37 · short O — /ŏ/ — "Round and Fast, O!"

**Sound:** short /ŏ/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the octopus &nbsp;·&nbsp; deep-dive — automaticity
**Words sung:** *dog · hot · box · hop · log*

**Suno accent** — bouncy and round; a quick rolling beat.

**Lyrics**

O, o, o — round and fast!
O, o, o — short o, hold on tight!

A hot dog! A fox in a box!
Hop on a log! A pot! A rock!
O, o, o — quick, quick, quick!

O, o, o — round and fast,
short o words go rolling past:
o, o, o!

**Movement** — make a round O with the hands on each /ŏ/, fast — open, round,
short. Quick claps in between. The mouth stays round but the sound stays
short — not a long Mandarin "o."

### Lesson 38 · short E — /ĕ/ — "Steady E"

**Sound:** short /ĕ/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the egg &nbsp;·&nbsp; deep-dive — automaticity
**Words sung:** *bed · ten · pet · get*

**Suno accent** — clear and even; a touch slower than the others — /ĕ/ is often
the last vowel to settle.

**Lyrics**

E, e, e — steady and true!
E, e, e — short e, just for you.

Ten red hens! A pet in a bed!
Get the net! E, e — well said!
E, e, e — clear, clear, clear!

E, e, e — steady and true,
short e words, we know them too:
e, e, e!

**Movement** — hand under the chin, the jaw drops its small step on every /ĕ/.
This is the most stubborn vowel for Mandarin-L1 children — go a beat slower,
and run pet / pit beside it.

### Lesson 39 · short U — /ŭ/ — "Sunny Fast U"

**Sound:** short /ŭ/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the umbrella &nbsp;·&nbsp; deep-dive — automaticity
**Words sung:** *sun · fun · bug · cup · hug*

**Suno accent** — sunny, warm and quick; a happy bounce.

**Lyrics**

U, u, u — quick and snug!
U, u, u — short u, give a hug!

Fun in the sun! A bug in a cup!
Run! Jump! A pup — get up!
U, u, u — quick, quick, quick!

U, u, u — quick and snug,
short u words, all warm and snug:
u, u, u!

**Movement** — a quick self-hug on each *hug* / *snug*; clap the rest. Jaw just
drops, lips relaxed, the /ŭ/ short — never a long open "ah."

---

### Lesson 40 · minimal pairs — "Cat? Cot? Cut?"

**Sound:** all five short vowels &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** listen close! &nbsp;·&nbsp; the Mandarin lesson
**Words sung:** *cat · cot · cut · pin · pen · pan*

**Suno accent** — slow, clear, almost a riddle; leave a tiny gap before each
vowel so the ear can catch it.

**Lyrics**

Listen close — which one is it?
Cat? Cot? Cut? — get it, get it!

Cat has /ă/ and cot has /ŏ/,
cut has /ŭ/ — now you know!
Pin? Pen? Pan? — listen well,
the little vowel is what you tell.

Listen close — which one is it?
One small sound — you've got it, got it!

**Movement** — cup a hand to the ear for "listen close." For each trio, hold up
one finger per word and point to the one the teacher says. This is the
vowel-discrimination game — the most important Phase 2 song for Mandarin-L1
children. Slow, careful, playful.

---

### Lesson 41 · FLSZ doubling — "Two at the End"

**Sound:** the doubling rule &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** f, l, s, z double up &nbsp;·&nbsp; Phase 2 close
**Words sung:** *bell · hill · doll · off · buzz*

**Suno accent** — playful, with a little "double-tap" on the ending of each
word.

**Lyrics**

F, l, s, z — at the end,
after a short vowel, they double, friend!

A bell on a hill — two l's, you see!
A doll, a buzz from a bumble-bee.
Off we go — two letters strong!

F, l, s, z — that's the song,
double them up, you can't go wrong:
two at the end!

**Movement** — hold up **two** fingers at the end of each doubled word — bell,
hill, doll, off, buzz. Teach the rule with the mnemonic: after a short vowel,
the "floss letters" — f, l, s, z — double up.

# Phase 3 — Digraphs and Blends (Lessons 42–53)

Phase 3 brings letters together: **digraphs** — two letters, one new sound —
and **blends** — two or three letters, each keeping its own sound, slid
together. These are the hardest songs to sing cleanly, and the most important
for a Mandarin-L1 ear, which has no consonant clusters at all. Sing them slow
first, then quick.

---

### Lesson 42 · `sh` — /sh/ — "Sh! Be Still"

**Sound:** /sh/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** finger on lips, "sh!" &nbsp;·&nbsp; two letters, one sound
**Words sung:** *ship · fish · dish · shop · wish*

**Suno accent** — soft and hushed; a quiet, gentle lull, almost a lullaby.

**Lyrics**

Sh, sh, sh — finger on your lips,
sh, sh, sh — a quiet little hiss.

A fish in a dish, sh, sh, sh.
A ship to the shop — make a wish!
S and h — they hold hands tight.

Sh, sh, sh — finger on your lips,
two little letters, one soft hiss:
sh, sh, sh!

**Movement** — one finger held to the lips for "sh!" on every /sh/ — the
quiet-time sign. Run one finger under "sh" — two letters, **one** sound, never
"s-h."

---

### Lesson 43 · `ch` — /ch/ — "Ch-Ch Goes the Train"

**Sound:** /ch/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the chugging train &nbsp;·&nbsp; two letters, one sound
**Words sung:** *chick · chip · chop · chat*

**Suno accent** — a bright chugging rhythm, like a little train picking up
speed.

**Lyrics**

Ch, ch, ch — like a chugging train,
ch, ch, ch — down the lane!

A chick on a chip — chat, chat, chat!
Chop, chop, chop — how about that?
C and h — they hold hands tight.

Ch, ch, ch — like a chugging train,
two little letters, say it again:
ch, ch, ch!

**Movement** — bend the arms and chug like a train engine on each /ch/. One
finger-sweep under "ch" — two letters, one sound.

---

### Lesson 44 · `th` (voiceless) — /θ/ — "Th — Tongue Peeks Out"

**Sound:** /θ/ voiceless &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the tongue peeking out &nbsp;·&nbsp; voice off
**Words sung:** *thin · thick · bath · moth · path*

**Suno accent** — slow and careful; leave space around each /th/ so the mouth
can get it right.

**Lyrics**

Th, th, th — tongue peeks out,
th, th, th — that's what it's about!

A thin, thin path — a thick, thick wall.
A moth in the bath — that's all!
Tongue between the teeth — th, th, th!

Th, th, th — tongue peeks out,
gently blow, with no shout:
th, th, th!

**Movement** — stick the tongue tip out a little, **between** the teeth, and
blow gently — *th*. No voice. This is the single hardest English sound for
Mandarin-L1 children; the mirror is essential, every time. Months of patience.

---

### Lesson 45 · `wh` — /wh/ — "Wh — the Asking Sound"

**Sound:** /wh/ &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the question words &nbsp;·&nbsp; rounded lips
**Words sung:** *when · whiz · wheel*

**Suno accent** — curious and lilting, every line a little question rising at
the end.

**Lyrics**

Wh, wh, wh — the asking sound,
wh, wh, wh — look around!

When? What? Where? Why? Who?
Wh words ask a thing of you.
A wheel goes whiz — wh, wh, wh!

Wh, wh, wh — the asking sound,
round your lips and spin around:
wh, wh, wh!

**Movement** — shrug the shoulders and open the hands in a "?" gesture on each
question word. Teach the secret: most words that *ask* a question start with
*wh* — when, what, where, why, who.

---

### Lesson 46 · `th` (voiced) — /ð/ — "Th — Now It Buzzes"

**Sound:** /ð/ voiced &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the buzzing tongue &nbsp;·&nbsp; voice on
**Words sung:** *this · that · them · then · they*

**Suno accent** — warm and low; a soft buzz running under each /th/.

**Lyrics**

Th, th, th — now it buzzes,
th, th, th — hand on the throat, it does!

This and that — they hold them tight.
Then they go — it feels just right.
Tongue peeks out, and now — it buzzes!

Th, th, th — now it buzzes,
voiced and warm, that's what it does:
th, th, th!

**Movement** — tongue between the teeth, **same** as Lesson 44 — but now a hand
on the throat to feel the **buzz**. Sing the two `th` songs back to back:
*thin* (no buzz) then *this* (buzz). Voiced `th` usually settles a few months
after the voiceless one — that is fine.

---

### Lesson 47 · ending blends — "Snap It at the End"

**Sound:** `st` `nd` `mp` &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** two sounds at the end &nbsp;·&nbsp; each keeps its sound
**Words sung:** *fast · hand · sand · jump · lamp*

**Suno accent** — punchy, with a strong double-beat landing on the end of each
word.

**Lyrics**

Two at the end — don't let them go!
Say each sound — now, nice and slow.

Fa-st — fast! Ha-nd — hand!
Ju-mp — jump in the sand!
A lamp, the end — two sounds, snap!

Two at the end — now say it quick,
fast and hand and jump — that's the trick:
snap it at the end!

**Movement** — for each blend word, hold up two fingers and "snap" them
together — say each ending sound, then snap them into one. Mandarin-L1 children
drop a final consonant or split it with a vowel — the snap keeps both sounds
joined, no vowel between.

### Lesson 48 · ending blends — "Pink, Tent, Belt"

**Sound:** `nk` `nt` `lt` &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** more endings &nbsp;·&nbsp; each keeps its sound
**Words sung:** *pink · sink · tent · went · belt*

**Suno accent** — bright and crisp; the same punchy double-beat ending.

**Lyrics**

More at the end — here we go!
Two little sounds, now you know.

Pi-nk — pink! A sink, a wink!
We-nt — went to a tent, quick as a blink!
A belt, the end — two sounds, snap!

More at the end — now say it quick,
pink and tent and belt — that's the trick:
snap it at the end!

**Movement** — the same two-finger snap as Lesson 47. The `nk` ending lets the

### Lesson 49 · s-blends — "S Blends Off We Go"

**Sound:** `sl sm sn sp st sw` &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** s at the start &nbsp;·&nbsp; two sounds, slid together
**Words sung:** *slip · sled · snap · spin · stop · swim*

**Suno accent** — flowing and slidey; stretch the /s/ a moment, then slide on.

**Lyrics**

S at the start — slide it along!
Ssss — then go — that's the song.

Slip on a sled! Snap! Spin around!
Stop! Swim! Listen to the sound.
S blends off — here we go!

S at the start — slide it along,
ssss-then-go, you can't go wrong:
s blends — off we go!

**Movement** — stretch the /s/ with a long sliding hand, then let the hand
shoot forward into the rest of the word — *ssss-lip, slip!* No vowel sneaks in
between the *s* and the next letter.

### Lesson 50 · l-blends — "L Blends Hold On"

**Sound:** `bl cl fl gl pl` &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** l at the start &nbsp;·&nbsp; two sounds, slid together
**Words sung:** *clap · clock · flag · flip · glad*

**Suno accent** — bright and brisk; a clean, happy clip.

**Lyrics**

L at the start — hold on tight!
Two little sounds, and you'll get it right.

Clap by the clock — a flag flies free!
Flip! Plus a plan — oh, glad are we!
L blends hold — here we go!

L at the start — hold on tight,
clap and flag and flip — that's right:
l blends — hold on tight!

**Movement** — clap on *clap*, wave a flag-hand on *flag*, flip the hand on
*flip*. Say the first consonant, then slide straight into the /l/ — for
Mandarin-L1 children watch that *bl/cl/fl/gl/pl* does not turn into *br/cr…*
(the l/r mix-up).

### Lesson 51 · r-blends — "R Blends, Strong and True"

**Sound:** `br cr dr fr gr pr tr` &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** r at the start &nbsp;·&nbsp; two sounds, slid together
**Words sung:** *frog · crab · drum · grab · trip*

**Suno accent** — slow and deliberate, a low rolling groove; give the /r/ room.

**Lyrics**

R at the start — strong and true!
A consonant, then r — that's what to do.

A frog and a crab! A drum goes drip!
Grab your bag — we're off on a trip!
R blends roll — here we go!

R at the start — strong and true,
frog and crab and drum — that's you!
r blends — strong and true!

**Movement** — say the first consonant, then the /r/, then the vowel — slow,
one slide. The hardest blends for Mandarin-L1 children — the /r/ is already the
hardest sound, and a blend doubles it. Keep the R/L drill running alongside.
Slow progress is normal.

### Lesson 52 · `tw` / `dw` blends — "Twist and Twirl"

**Sound:** `tw` `dw` &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** the twirling twins &nbsp;·&nbsp; two sounds, slid together
**Words sung:** *twin · twist · twig*

**Suno accent** — playful and spinning, a little waltzing twirl.

**Lyrics**

Tw, tw, tw — twist and twirl!
Tw, tw, tw — give it a whirl!

A twin, a twig, on the twisting tree,
twist them around — one, two, three!
Tw, tw — round we go!

Tw, tw, tw — twist and twirl,
say it as you spin and whirl:
tw, tw, tw!

**Movement** — twist and twirl on the spot on each *tw* word. Lips round for
the /w/ right after the /t/ — *t* then *w*, slid together.

### Lesson 53 · triple blends — "Three Sounds Strong"

**Sound:** `str scr spl spr thr shr` &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Keyword:** three letters together &nbsp;·&nbsp; the Pink finale
**Words sung:** *splash · sprint · strap · scrub · split*

**Suno accent** — big and triumphant, building to a strong finish — the last
Pink Phase song.

**Lyrics**

Three sounds strong — here they come!
Three little letters, beat the drum!

Splash in the water! Sprint down the lane!
A strap, a scrub — say it again!
Split it up — three sounds, one go!

Three sounds strong — you've come so far,
you can read — look how strong you are!
Three sounds strong — hurray!

**Movement** — three quick claps for the three sounds, then say the whole blend

## Using the songs — the weekly routine

- **Sing it first.** The sound-song opens the lesson — two or three minutes at
  the circle, before the sandpaper letter and the word work. The children hear
  the sound, say it, and move it, then go to the materials already primed.
- **Do the movement every time.** The movement is the articulation lesson set
  to music — not optional decoration. For the hard sounds (the final stops,
  /r/, /th/, /v/, /z/) it is the most important part of the song.
- **Sing the same song all week.** Repetition is how a four-year-old comes to
  own a song. By Friday they sing it without you.
- **Slow first, then quick.** Especially the Phase 3 blend songs — sing them
  slow enough that every sound is clean before you let the tempo lift.
- **Heard, not read.** The children sing these; they do not decode them. Keep
  the songs joyful and loose. The point is the sound in the ear and the body,
  not words on a page.
- **Pair them with the Pink Readers.** When a child reaches a gate, the
  matching reader is the milestone book — and the songs from the lessons up to
  that gate are a warm-up the child already knows by heart.

Forty-nine songs, one for every lesson — sing the Pink Phase from the first
soft hiss of the snake to the triumphant *hurray* of the triple blends.
