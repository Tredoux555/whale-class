# Pink Reader · Book 5 — "A Big Nap"

**Gate:** read after **Lesson 34** (Phase 1 close — every letter mastered,
three review lessons done).
**Pages:** cover + 12 story pages. **One to three short sentences per page.**

*Working title in the series plan was "Sam Can Read" — but `read` carries the
`ea` vowel team, which is not taught until the Green Phase. Retitled to a fully
decodable line that also lands the series' closing motif.*

**Word inventory (every word in the book):**
`a · cat · and · pup · hen · fox · Sam · Mom · Dad · they · can · run · hop ·
the · nap · on · mat · dig · peck · sit · with · sun · is · hot · it · fun ·
big`
— 27 words total.

**Heart words used:** `a` (L8), `and` (L13), `the` (L11), `they` (L31),
`with` (L34). All introduced by the gate. ✓
**Decodability check:** every other word is pure CVC from letters taught in
Phase 1 — `dig` (d-i-g), `peck` (p-e-ck), `mat` (m-a-t), `sun` (s-u-n), `fun`
(f-u-n), `big` (b-i-g). `Sam`, `Mom`, `Dad`, `cat`, `pup`, `hen`, `fox` are all
decodable proper names / nouns from earlier books. The whole book is present
tense — no past-tense forms at all. **Nothing outruns the child's phonics.** ✓

**The shape:** Book 5 is the **Phase 1 capstone** — so it gathers the whole
cast the child has collected across Books 1–4. The first three pages are a warm
roll-call: the cat and pup, the hen and fox, then Sam, Mom and Dad. The new
heart word `they` lets the book speak about the group — *"They can run! They
can hop!"* — a joyful consolidation of every CVC verb the child now owns. It
closes on the biggest version of the series' signature image: not one sleeper,
but **everyone**, piled together. The reader finishes Phase 1 on a page that
says, quietly, *you know all of these now.*

## Teacher note — how to use Book 5

**When:** hand it to the child at the **close of Phase 1**, after Lesson 34.
This is the celebration book — *"you have finished Phase 1. You can read every
short-vowel word. Here is the book with everyone in it."*

**The pattern:** pages 1–3 are a roll-call the child can read with rising pride
— each page names friends the child already knows. Pages 4–5 (*"They can
run! They can hop!"*) are pure consolidation: short, joyful, fluent. Pages 7–8
bring back the doubled cheer (*"Dig, pup, dig!"*).

**The three reads:**

1. **Read 1 — decode.** Child sounds out each word. By Phase 1 close most
   children decode CVC words quickly — let them feel that speed.
2. **Read 2 — smooth.** Child reads it again, "like talking," with the joy the
   pictures show.
3. **Read 3 — for the story.** Child reads it for fun. Talk about the
   pictures: *Can you find every friend? Which one is your favourite?*

**The page-turn:** Book 5 has no single suspense hook — it is a celebration,
not a mystery. Instead, pause on page 3 and ask *"Who do you think they will
ALL do together?"* — then enjoy pages 4–5 as the answer.

**Comprehension question:** *"Who is in the big nap at the end?"* (Answer:
everyone — the cat, the pup, the hen, the fox, Sam, Mom and Dad.) Then: *"Which
friend did you meet first?"* — a chance to look back across Books 1–4.

**A Phase 1 milestone:** consider letting the child read this one aloud to a
parent or a younger friend. After five books, reading TO someone is the natural
next step — and Book 5, with its warm roll-call, is built for it.

**ESL / Mandarin-L1 watch:** this book is consolidation — use it to check
final stops across the whole cast of words: final /t/ (`cat`, `mat`, `hot`,
`it`, `sit`), final /p/ (`pup`, `hop`, `nap`), final /k/ (`peck`), final /g/
(`dig`, `big`, `pup`… ). Tissue test on any that have gone soft. The /r/ in
`run` and the /th/ in `they`, `the`, `with` — keep the daily drills going.
