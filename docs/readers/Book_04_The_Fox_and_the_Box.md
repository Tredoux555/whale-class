# Pink Reader · Book 4 — "The Fox and the Box"

**Gate:** read after **Lesson 31** (the alphabet is complete — every single
letter, plus `ck` and `qu`).
**Pages:** cover + 12 story pages. **One or two short sentences per page.**

**Word inventory (every word in the book):**
`Sam · has · a · big · box · fox · the · ran · to · what · is · in · can ·
tap · not · get · tip · did · cat · was · and · sat · nap`
— 23 words total.

**Heart words used:** `a` (L8), `the` (L11), `to` (L12), `was` (L14), `and`
(L13), `what` (L27). All introduced by the gate. ✓
**Decodability check:** every other word is pure CVC from letters taught by
Lesson 31 — `box` (b-o-x) and `fox` (f-o-x) use `x` from Lesson 28; `big`
(b-i-g), `tap` (t-a-p), `tip` (t-i-p), `get` (g-e-t). `ran`, `did`, `sat` are
decodable CVC past-tense words. `Sam` and `cat` carry over. **Nothing outruns
the child's phonics.** ✓

**The shape:** Book 4 is the milestone reader for finishing the alphabet — so
it earns a little more story. A curious **fox** finds a mysterious **box**.
The heart word `what` carries the hook — *"What is in the box?"* — and the
middle pages build mild, gentle suspense as the fox taps and tips it. The
payoff reaches back across the whole series: the **cat** from Books 1–3 was
napping inside all along. Fright melts into friendship, and the book closes on
the cosy box-nap the cat has been teaching everyone since Book 1. The words
stay simple; **the fox's curiosity and the surprise both live in the art.**

## COVER

**Text:** The Fox and the Box
*(small, below or above the art: "Pink Reader · Book 4")*

**Art brief:** A bright-eyed, slender **fox** — warm orange, bushy tail, alert
pointed ears — peering with intense curiosity over the rim of a plain brown
cardboard **box**, both front paws up on the edge, nose down, sniffing. Just
the right amount of mischief in the eyes. The box is shut, ordinary,
mysterious. Soft cream background.

*Design the fox here — every later book draws it exactly the same: orange,
slim, bright-eyed, a touch of mischief.*

**Composition:** Title large across the top in Andika lowercase (only "The",
"Fox", "Box" capitalised as printed). Fox and box centred. Calm space.

## PAGE 1

**Text:** Sam has a big box.

**Art brief:** Establishing shot. **Sam** — drawn exactly as in Books 1–3 —
sitting on the home rug beside a large, plain, shut cardboard **box**. Sam pats
the box, pleased with it, the way a child treasures an ordinary box. Cosy
living room, soft daylight. *(Optional: the faintest hint that the box is not
quite empty — a whisker, a tuft of fur — but keep it almost invisible.)*

**Composition:** Text on a clean band along the bottom third. Sam and the box
centred above it.

## PAGE 2

**Text:** A fox! The fox ran to the box.

**Art brief:** The fox arrives — bounding into the room at speed, tail
streaming, eyes locked on the box, drawn to it like a magnet. Sam sits back,
surprised and delighted, watching this new visitor. Energy and arrival.

**Composition:** Fox running left to right toward the box, leading the eye.
Sam at the side. Bottom text band.

## PAGE 3

**Text:** "What is in the box?"

**Art brief:** The hook. The fox stands right at the box now, leaning in close,
nose almost touching it, ears pricked, one paw lifted — every line of the body
asking the question. The box stays shut and silent. *What IS in there?*

**Composition:** Fox and box centred, the fox's whole posture pointing into the
box. Bottom text band.

## PAGE 4

**Text:** The fox can tap the box. Tap, tap, tap!

**Art brief:** The fox tapping the side of the box with one careful paw — head
tilted, listening hard for any answer from inside. Three little tap-marks or
sound-puffs. Comic concentration.

**Composition:** Fox tapping the box, both centred. Bottom text band.

## PAGE 5

**Text:** The fox can not get in.

**Art brief:** The fox trying to climb the box — front paws scrabbling up the
side, back legs pushing, slipping back down — the box too tall and smooth, the
lid shut. Determined and a bit comically thwarted. Sam watches, hand to mouth,
amused.

**Composition:** Fox mid-scrabble against the box. Bottom text band.

## PAGE 6

**Text:** The fox can tip the box.

**Art brief:** New idea. The fox has put both front paws low on the side of the
box and is shoving — body braced, tail out for balance, a clever gleam in its
eye. The box leans, just starting to rock.

**Composition:** Fox pushing low, box tilting. A slight diagonal to the whole
scene. Bottom text band.

---

## PAGE 7

**Text:** Tip, tip... the box did tip!

**Art brief:** The box goes over — caught at the moment it topples onto its
side, lid flapping open, a little puff of motion. The fox hops back, eyes wide.
Sam leans in. *Something is about to come out.*

**Composition:** Box mid-topple, open end starting to face the reader. Fox
recoiling at the side. Bottom text band. This is the page-turn page — leave the
reader hanging.

## PAGE 8

**Text:** A cat!

**Art brief:** The surprise. Out of the open box rolls the **tabby cat** from
Books 1–3 — sleepy, rumpled, blinking, mid-stretch, distinctly unimpressed at
being woken. The fox sits bolt upright, ears flat, eyes huge — a comic fright.
Sam bursts out laughing.

**Composition:** Cat tumbling out of the box centre, fox startled at the side.
Bottom text band. The fox's frozen surprise is the joke.

## PAGE 9

**Text:** The cat was in the box.

**Art brief:** A calm beat. The cat sits beside the tipped-over box, fully
awake now, giving its paw a dignified lick — *of course I was in the box, I nap
everywhere.* The fox stares at the cat, fright fading into wonder. Sam grins.

**Composition:** Cat and box centre, fox watching. Bottom text band.

**Composition:** Fox and cat side by side, centred, facing each other. Bottom

**Composition:** Box centred, fox and cat climbing in from each side. Bottom

**Text:** A fox and a cat in a box. Nap, fox. Nap, cat.

## Teacher note — how to use Book 4

**When:** hand it to the child after **Lesson 31** — the alphabet is complete.
Mark it: *"you have learned every letter in English. Here is your book for
that."* Book 4 is a real milestone.

**The pattern:** Book 4 is less of a strict pattern book and more of a small
story with a beginning, a middle and a turn — that is the growth from Books
1–3. The repeated *"Tap, tap, tap!"* (page 4) and *"Tip, tip…"* (page 7) still
give the child predictable, fluent moments.

**The three reads:**

1. **Read 1 — decode.** Child sounds out each word, finger under the text.
   Slow is fine. Every word is decodable.
2. **Read 2 — smooth.** Child reads it again, "like talking." Build a little
   suspense in the voice on pages 6–7.
3. **Read 3 — for the story.** Child reads it for fun. Talk about the
   pictures: *What did the fox think was in the box? What was REALLY in it?*

**The page-turn:** at page 7 (*"the box did tip!"*) STOP. Ask *"What do you
think will come out of the box?"* Take guesses. Then turn to page 8.

**Comprehension question:** *"Who was hiding in the box?"* (Answer: the cat.)
Then: *"How did the fox feel when the cat came out? How do you know?"* — the
child reads the fox's flat ears and huge eyes on page 8. The words never say
the fox was startled; the picture does. That is the comprehension skill.

**Series note:** this is the first book where the cast crosses over — the cat
the child has known since Book 1 surprises everyone. If the child has read the
earlier books, ask: *"Have you met this cat before?"*

**ESL / Mandarin-L1 watch:** the `x` in `box` and `fox` is a /ks/ blend —
Mandarin-L1 children tend to simplify it to /s/ ("bos"). Say it slowly:
"bo-k-s." Watch the final /p/ in `tap`, `tip` and final /t/ in `cat`, `sat`,
`not`, `what` — the tissue test. The /r/ in `ran` — keep the daily R/L drill.
