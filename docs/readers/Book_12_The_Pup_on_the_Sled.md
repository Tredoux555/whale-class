# Pink Reader · Book 12 — "The Pup on the Sled"

**Gate:** read after **Lesson 50** (Phase 3 — beginning blends: the s-blends
`sl sm sn sp st sw` and the l-blends `bl cl fl gl pl`).
**Pages:** cover + 12 story pages. **One to three short sentences per page.**

*Working title in the series plan was "Stop! Spin! Splash?" — but `splash` is a
triple blend (`spl`), not taught until Lesson 53. Retitled to a fully decodable
story line; the book still drives hard on s-blends (`stop`, `spin`, `slip`,
`sled`) and l-blends (`glad`, `flip`).*

**Word inventory (every word in the book):**
`Sam · has · a · sled · the · pup · ran · to · got · on · is · big · hill · can ·
slip · went · fast · glad · spin · Who · stop · said · Don't · it · fun · flip ·
fell · off · up · had · and`
— 31 words total.

**Heart words used:** `a` (L8), `the` (L11), `to` (L12), `Who` (L50), `said`
(L22), `Don't` (L50), `and` (L13). All introduced by the gate. ✓
**Decodability check:** the new beginning blends carry the story — s-blends in
`sled` (sl), `slip` (sl), `spin` (sp), `stop` (st); l-blends in `glad` (gl),
`flip` (fl). `hill`, `fell`, `off` use FLSZ doubling (L41); `went` (nt) and
`fast` (st) use ending blends (L47–48). `ran`, `got`, `went`, `fell`, `had`,
`said` are decodable past-tense words. **Nothing outruns the child's
phonics.** ✓

**The shape:** Book 12 makes the new beginning blends into a fast, funny ride.
The bouncy **pup** gets on a **sled** at the top of a **hill** — and the
sled's verbs are all blends the child has just learned: it can `slip`, it can
`spin`, Sam wants it to `stop`. The hook uses the new heart word `Who` —
*"Who can stop the sled?"* — and the joke is the pup's answer with the other
new word: *"Don't stop! It is fun!"* It closes warm: the pup tumbles off,
unhurt and delighted. **The speed and the glee live in the art.**

## Teacher note — how to use Book 12

**When:** hand it to the child during **Phase 3**, after Lesson 50 — once the
beginning blends are introduced. Book 12 puts the s-blends and l-blends
straight to work.

**The pattern:** the blend words are the load-bearing words — `sled`, `slip`,
`spin`, `stop` (s-blends) and `glad`, `flip` (l-blends). As the child meets
each, point out the two letters at the *start* and blend them smoothly into the
vowel — no extra sound between them. The cheers (*"Slip, slip, slip!"* /
*"Spin, sled, spin!"*) give the fluent, predictable beats.

**The three reads:**

1. **Read 1 — decode.** Child sounds out each word. On the blend words, say the
   two starting consonants close together, then straight into the vowel:
   "s-l-ed," then "sled."
2. **Read 2 — smooth.** Child reads it again, "like talking." Let the speed
   build in the voice from page 4 to page 9.
3. **Read 3 — for the story.** Child reads it for fun. Talk about the pictures:
   *Was the pup scared? How do you know?* (No — it grins on every page, even
   when it falls off.)

**The page-turn:** at page 7 (*"Who can stop the sled?"*) pause. Ask *"Do you
think the pup WANTS the sled to stop?"* Then turn to page 8 for the funny
answer.

**Comprehension question:** *"Sam was worried about the pup. Was the pup
worried?"* (Answer: no — the pup was having a wonderful time and did not want
to stop.) A nice chance to talk about how two characters can feel completely
different things about the same moment — the reader sees both on pages 7 and 8.

**A blend extension:** after the three reads, find every beginning blend in the
book — `sled`, `slip`, `spin`, `stop`, `glad`, `flip` — and have the child
say each one slowly, then fast, never breaking the cluster.

**ESL / Mandarin-L1 watch:** beginning blends are hard for Mandarin-L1 children
— Mandarin has no clusters, so `sled` may become "se-led" with an extra vowel.
The fix: stretch the `s` (or `f`/`g`), then slide straight into the next
consonant with no vowel between. The s-blends are hardest because of the long
`s` friction. Slow first, then quick.
