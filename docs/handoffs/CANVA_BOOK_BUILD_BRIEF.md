# Canva Book Build Brief — Pink Readers, Books 1–3

**For a browser-Claude with Canva access.** This brief is self-contained: it
holds the workflow, the locked style, the character prompts, and the full
page-by-page text + image prompt for the first three Pink Readers. Build them
as finished, printable little books in Canva.

The full series (all 15 manuscripts, each page's text + image prompt) lives at
**`montree.xyz/pink-readers.html`**. The two guides on that page —
*Teacher Guide* and *Canva Production Guide* — are the canonical reference.
This brief condenses them for execution.

---

## Mission

Produce **Book 1, Book 2 and Book 3** of the Pink Readers as printable little
books in Canva — one Canva design per book. Each is a decodable reader for a
3–6 Montessori classroom of Mandarin-L1 ESL children. Then make the
comprehension worksheet for each (see the final section).

Quality bar: the three books must look like **one shelf of books** — same
characters, same palette, same font, same layout. Consistency is everything.

---

## The non-negotiables

1. **Type the page text EXACTLY as given.** These are decodable readers — every
   word is chosen so the child can sound it out. Do not add, remove, swap, or
   "improve" a single word. A changed word can break the book.
2. **One locked style for all three books** (and all 15 eventually).
3. **Generate the characters ONCE, reuse them.** Sam must be the same child on
   every page of every book.
4. **Font: Andika**, lowercase, large. (Fallback: Comic Sans MS. Never a
   decorative or serif font — the child is matching letters to sounds.)
5. **No text inside the AI-generated images.** You add the text yourself.

---

## The locked visual style

- **Illustration:** flat, warm, modern children's-book style. Soft rounded
  shapes, clean, uncluttered, lots of calm space.
- **Palette:** soft cream background `#FBF6EC`, sage green `#8BA888`, warm
  terracotta `#CC7E5B`, gentle gold `#E8C96A`, a soft pink accent `#E9B7BE`
  (used sparingly).
- **Text:** near-black `#2E2A22` on a clean off-white band `#FFFDF7`.

### Typography

- Font **Andika** (Canva → add font, search "Andika"), lowercase except the
  first word of a sentence and names.
- Body text ~28–36 pt, wide line spacing, one short line where possible.
- Text always sits on the clean band, **never over a busy part of the picture.**

### Page setup

- Canva design: **square, 20 × 20 cm**. One Canva page per book page (the cover
  is its own page).
- Reserve the **bottom third** of every page for a soft rounded text band in
  `#FFFDF7`; keep the band in the same place on every page of a book.
- Art fills the upper two-thirds. Keep everything ≥1.5 cm from the page edge.

---

## STEP 0 — Generate the cast (do this once, before any pages)

Paste each prompt below into **Canva Magic Media**. Pick the best result,
remove its background (*Edit image → BG Remover*), and save it to a "Pink
Readers cast" folder. You then drop the **same** saved character into every
scene — this is what keeps Sam the same child across all the books.

Books 1–3 need these five:

- **Sam** — *A young child about five years old, gender-neutral, round friendly
  face, soft dark hair, warm simple clothing in cream and sage green, a gentle
  smile, standing relaxed, full body, flat warm children's-book illustration,
  soft rounded shapes, plain white background, no text*
- **The cat** — *A plump friendly tabby cat, soft brown and grey stripes, big
  gentle eyes, curled in a relaxed sitting pose, flat warm children's-book
  illustration, soft rounded shapes, plain white background, no text*
- **The pup** — *A small round puppy, golden-brown, floppy ears, big eager
  eyes, short legs, sitting bouncy and alert, flat warm children's-book
  illustration, soft rounded shapes, plain white background, no text*
- **Mom** — *A warm gentle mother, soft round face, kind eyes, hair tied back,
  soft clothing in warm terracotta and cream, a calm smile, standing relaxed,
  full body, flat warm children's-book illustration, soft rounded shapes,
  plain white background, no text*
- **The hen** — *A plump cheerful hen, warm reddish-brown feathers, a small red
  comb, one bright alert eye, a slightly comic expressive face, standing, flat
  warm children's-book illustration, soft rounded shapes, plain white
  background, no text*

*(The full 8-character cast sheet is in the Canva Production Guide on
`pink-readers.html` — you will need the fox, frog and Dad for later books.)*

---

## STEP 1 — Build each page

For every page in the tables below:

1. Add a new Canva page (square 20 × 20 cm). For page 1, build the template:
   place the bottom-third text band; duplicate this page for every later page.
2. Paste that page's **Image prompt** into Canva Magic Media to generate the
   scene and background.
3. Drop your saved **character elements** on top, posed as the prompt
   describes. The prompt names the characters and their expressions — match
   them. (You may also generate the whole scene from the prompt and accept some
   drift, but compositing the saved cast is what keeps them on-model.)
4. Add the page **Text** in Andika, lowercase, ~32 pt, near-black, on the text
   band. Type it **exactly** as the table gives it.
5. Check: text clear of the busy art, characters on-model, calm space.

---

## STEP 2 — Assemble and export each book

- Order the Canva pages: cover, then 1, 2, 3 … to the last page.
- Export as **PDF Print** (Share → Download → PDF Print).
- Print single-sided on the heaviest paper available; stack in order and staple
  twice down the left edge (or fold-and-staple if printed two-up). Trim edges.
- Laminate the cover. Write the book number on the back so the set stays
  ordered on the shelf.

# Comprehension worksheets — one per book (all 15)

After each book is built, make its **comprehension worksheet** — a single
printable **A4 portrait page**, almost entirely pictures, for a 3–6
Mandarin-L1 ESL class.

## Who these are for — and what that means

The child is ~4½–6, decoding the Pink Phase, and **cannot read a worksheet
instruction**. So:

- **The worksheet is teacher-administered.** The teacher reads each instruction
  aloud; the child does the picture task. Instructions are written for the
  adult — a short English line plus a small grey **Chinese gloss** so a
  Chinese-speaking teacher or parent can run it.
- **Every task is pictures.** The child responds only by **circling, drawing a
  line, numbering 1-2-3, or drawing.** Never writing words.
- **Big clear pictures, lots of white space.** It is a "talk about the book"
  page, not a test. ~5–10 minutes, teacher-paced.

## The layout

A4 portrait. A title strip at the top (book title in Andika + `Name: ____`),
then **6 boxes in a 2-column × 3-row grid**. Each box: a big icon, the English
instruction, the small grey Chinese gloss, and the picture task.

## The 6 boxes — fixed for every book

| # | Box | Instruction (English) | Chinese gloss (small, grey) | The task |
|---|-----|----------------------|------------------------------|----------|
| 1 | **Who?** | "Circle the friends in this book." | 圈出这本书里的朋友 | 5 character pictures; child circles the ones in this book. |
| 2 | **Match** | "Draw a line. Match the word to the picture." | 连线：把单词和图片连起来 | 3 words (left) and 3 pictures (right); child draws a line. |
| 3 | **What happened?** | "Number the pictures: 1, 2, 3." | 给图片排顺序：1、2、3 | 3 shuffled scenes; child numbers them in story order. |
| 4 | **Think!** | *(per book — see below)* | *(per book)* | 3 picture options; child circles the answer. |
| 5 | **Feel!** | *(per book)* + "Circle the face." | 圈出表情 | 1 character + 3 faces (happy / sad / surprised); circle. |
| 6 | **Draw!** | *(per book)* | *(per book)* | An empty box; child draws. |

## How to build a worksheet in Canva — reuse what you already made

- **Box 1 (Who)** — use the **saved cast elements** (Step 0). No new images.
- **Box 2 (Match)** — generate 3 small spot-illustrations of the words, same
  flat style: *a small [object], flat warm children's-book illustration, soft
  rounded shapes, plain white background, no text.* Set the 3 words in Andika.
- **Box 3 (What happened)** — reuse **the book's own page illustrations**,
  shrunk to thumbnails, placed out of order.
- **Box 4 (Think)** — 3 small option pictures (reuse page crops or generate
  simple objects).
- **Box 5 (Feel)** — a crop of the named page + 3 simple round face icons.
- **Box 6 (Draw)** — just an empty rounded box.

Export each worksheet as its own **PDF Print**, A4. One worksheet per book.

---

## Per-book worksheet content

For each book: **Who** lists the characters that ARE in it (circle these) and,
in brackets, the distractors to also show. **Match** gives the 3 words.
**Sequence** gives the 3 scenes in correct order (show them shuffled; the page
numbers are the book pages to reuse). **Think** and **Feel** give the question
+ options + answer. **Draw** gives the prompt.
