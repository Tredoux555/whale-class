## What it is

The Olive Branch for Children (TOBFC) is a Canadian-registered NGO (also a registered charity in Ireland and a registered association in Germany) operating entirely in the **Mbeya Region of western Tanzania** (Mbarali District). It is NOT a single school — it's a large multi-program development charity, founded 2005, now reaching 200,000+ people/year across 50+ communities via education, health, community, climate, and financial-autonomy programs. Montessori education is one pillar of a much bigger operation. 100+ staff, all based in Tanzania.

## Scale of the Montessori operation

- **The Olive Branch Montessori Academy** (est. 2016), Majengo Mapya / Uyole, Mbeya — one physical campus, ~38–50 students, ages 1.5–13 (Toddler → Grade 7). Mix of fully-sponsored (Zion Peace Home) kids, scholarship kids, and fee-paying kids from the wider community.
- **20 Montessori Kindergarten (Casa) classrooms** spread across partner communities in Mbeya Region — 2,200+ children enrolled in 2024. Community-built/TOBFC-funded, staffed by locally trained teachers (some hold full AMI Montessori Diplomas via scholarship). TOBFC has also helped 10 more classrooms open outside their own network — 30 early-learning classrooms total. They're a recognized leader in Tanzanian early-childhood education.

## Why this matters for a Montree pitch

This is a genuinely strong multi-classroom use case — 20+ geographically scattered classrooms plus one larger academy under one central org that already talks about meeting "national standards" and reporting to donors. That's a better structural fit for a multi-classroom SaaS pitch than a typical single private school.

**But:** this is a donor-funded NGO serving vulnerable + fee-paying families in rural Tanzania — not a private school with a discretionary software budget. Standard per-student SaaS pricing likely won't land as-is. Worth going in framed around impact/reporting value to their donor network rather than a stock pitch, and be ready to discuss a founding/discounted/sponsored access model given the org's profile (similar to schools already flagged in the "underprivileged" outreach track).
