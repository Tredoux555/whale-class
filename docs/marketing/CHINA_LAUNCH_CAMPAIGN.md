## 1. THE STRATEGIC FRAME

**The market moment (verified July 2026):**
- China closed 21,000+ kindergartens in 2024 (~2 per hour). 41,500 closed since the 2021 peak.
- Private kindergartens hit hardest: −9% year-on-year, vs. much smaller decline for public.
- Enrollment down ~25% in 4 years (48M → 40.9M children). Projected 15,000 closures/year through 2030.
- Reported reality on the ground: private 园长 recruiting ~20 new students to replace 60 graduates. They are actively increasing marketing spend.

**Translation:** Every private kindergarten principal in China is asking one question this summer: *凭什么家长选我们?* (Why would a parent choose us?) Montree is a direct answer: *because parents can SEE their child's growth, every week, with photos and a real narrative.* No other kindergarten in their district can show that.

**The two-track funnel:**

| Track | Audience | Platform | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| A — Parent pull | Mothers 25–40 choosing/judging kindergartens | 小红书 (primary), Douyin (cross-post) | Plant the QUESTION parents ask schools: "你们怎么让我看到孩子的成长?" Build the account + social proof. |
| B — Principal push | Private kindergarten 园长/投资人 | 视频号 WeChat Channels (primary), Douyin | Crisis-frame + edge-frame. Every video ends one WeChat message away from a demo. |

**Honest mechanics for a solo founder:** these videos do NOT need to go viral to work. Their primary job is (1) demand-gen lottery tickets (volume beats a cold algorithm) and (2) a **credibility asset** — when a principal checks you out before a demo, they find a foreign AMS-certified Montessori teacher, teaching in Beijing, publishing consistently, with parents in the comments. That alone changes the sales conversation.

**Your unfair advantages, in priority order:**
1. **外教 who actually teaches in a Beijing kindergarten.** Foreign-teacher perspective content massively over-performs with Chinese parents. You are not a marketer selling software; you are a teacher showing his craft.
2. **The artifact.** The Montree parent report IS the emotional payload. A mother seeing a beautiful, photo-rich, personal narrative about HER child's week produces the feeling no ad can: *finally, I know.* Show the artifact, not the app.
3. **The crisis math** (Track B). You have the numbers; principals feel them daily.

**Positioning discipline (the over-complication fix):**
- Parent content: ONE loop only — photo → understanding → parent report. Astra, appointments, messaging, billing NEVER appear.
- Principal content: lead with the loop + enrollment edge. School-management features are a closer's line ("它还能管理整个园所"), never the opener.
- One message per video. If a script makes two points, cut one.

## 2. REGULATORY GUARDRAILS (do not cross)

The Double Reduction ad ban targets **off-campus academic tutoring**. A school-management SaaS marketed on transparency + child development is outside it — and Montessori's whole-child, anti-cramming approach ALIGNS with official 去小学化 policy. Keep it that way:

1. **Never claim academic advancement for preschoolers.** No 识字量, no 数学超前, no "prepares for primary school." Say 专注力、独立性、动手能力、内在秩序 — development language, not curriculum language.
2. **No superlatives.** 最/第一/唯一 are Advertising Law violations. Say 第一个这样做的 only if literally provable; safer: "很少有幼儿园能做到".
3. **No children's faces without written guardian consent.** Default: hands, materials, backs of heads, wide shots from behind, or staged shots with no children. This is both law and trust — and it's ON-MESSAGE: "我们记录孩子的工作,不消费孩子的脸."
4. Organic personal-account content ≠ regulated advertising, but claims still need to be true. The honesty firewall from the script brain applies unchanged.

## 3. PLATFORM PLAYBOOK

**小红书 (Track A primary).**
- Search + interest discovery engine; notes surface for months. Kindest platform to a new account.
- Mix formats: **vertical video hooks** AND **image-carousel notes** (carousels are cheap, search-friendly, and often outperform video for decision-research topics like 选幼儿园).
- Post 1 video + 1 carousel per day during the campaign burst. Titles are search queries: 「选幼儿园必问的5个问题」「蒙氏幼儿园到底好在哪」.
- Account persona: 「北京幼儿园外教 Tredoux」 — a teacher sharing his classroom craft, not a brand account.

**视频号 WeChat Channels (Track B primary).**
- Principals live in WeChat. 视频号 content forwards natively into 园长 group chats — the distribution IS the group-chat forward.
- Same vertical videos, principal-facing scripts. Profile links to your WeChat for demo requests.
- Also send B-track videos DIRECTLY to principal contacts as WeChat messages. A 20-second video beats a cold text pitch.

**抖音 Douyin (both tracks, cross-post).**
- Zero marginal cost: same files. Expect the cold-algorithm pattern from the TikTok brain — don't read early plateaus as failure; outpace with volume.

**Cadence target:** 20 videos in 20 days (holiday burst). Batch-film: one Saturday = 8–10 videos with these scripts.

**Metrics that matter (from the growth brain):** completion rate + first-3-second retention. Diagnose the first frame before blaming the script. Winners get re-cut into 2 variants; losers get replaced, not mourned.

## 4. FORMAT RULES (every video)

- **12–20 seconds.** ~60–85 Chinese characters spoken max.
- **First frame = its own deliverable.** Your face mid-motion or a striking material close-up + big readable overlay text. Never a logo or title card.
- **On-screen subtitles always** (most viewing is muted; also aids non-native accent).
- **You speak Chinese on camera where possible** — imperfect Mandarin from a foreign teacher is endearing and stops the scroll; have a native speaker sanity-check pronunciation on key lines. Fallback: speak English with big Chinese subtitles (the 外教 frame makes this natural).
- **CTA ladder:** in-video CTA is soft or absent (story-first). The ask lives in caption + comment-pin + bio. Track B may pitch directly — B2B shopping context differs from cold consumer scrolling.
- **Consistent sign-off beat** for account memory: 「关注孩子,而非记录。」 (Tend to the child, not the observation.)

## 5. TRACK A — PARENT SERIES 「你知道孩子今天做了什么吗?」

### A1 — The "玩" video (THE FLAGSHIP — film this first)
**First frame:** you, close, mid-gesture, overlay: 「孩子说:玩。」
**Script (说词):**
> 每天放学你问孩子:今天在幼儿园干嘛了?
> 他说:玩。
> 但今天他第一次自己完成了一千颗金珠的加法。
> 他专注了四十分钟,没有人打断他。
> 你不知道。因为没有人告诉你。
> 你的孩子每天都在长大——只是你看不见。

*(EN gloss: Every day you ask your child what they did at kindergarten. "Played." But today he completed golden bead addition by himself for the first time. He concentrated for 40 minutes, uninterrupted. You don't know — because nobody told you. Your child is growing every day. You just can't see it.)*
**Shot list:** talking head → cut to close-up of golden beads being laid out (hands only) → back to you for the last line. 3 shots.
**Caption:** 有的幼儿园,每周会把这些瞬间拍下来,写成一份属于你孩子的成长报告。选园的时候,记得问一句:你们怎么让我看到孩子的成长?#选幼儿园 #蒙特梭利 #幼儿园那些事

**Caption:** 把这个问题存下来,下次参观的时候问。真正好的园,答得出来。#幼儿园参观 #选园攻略

## 8. PRODUCTION SYSTEM (batch day)

1. **One Saturday, 8–10 videos.** Tripod + phone + classroom (empty) + lav mic if available. Same shirt = one "season"; change shirt halfway to create two posting weeks.
2. **Subtitles:** CapCut auto-caption (剪映) → manual fix. 剪映 also auto-generates the first-frame text overlay.
3. **Demo/report footage:** use ghost school data ("Greenfield Montessori" / demo children). Never real children without consent.
4. **Chinese delivery:** record key lines slowly; 剪映 can tighten pauses. Alternative for early videos: English voice + full Chinese subtitles + a spoken Chinese first line as the hook (「你的孩子今天做了什么?」) — the code-switch itself is a scroll-stopper.
5. **Posting:** XHS + 视频号 + Douyin same day. Best windows: 7–9am and 8–10pm for parents; lunch 12–1pm for principals.
6. **Comment protocol:** reply to EVERY comment in the first 2 hours (velocity signal). Pin a comment carrying the CTA so the video itself stays clean.

## 9. THE FREELANCER DECISION (framework, not a hire)

Do NOT hire a retainer "Chinese media expert" now. The deliverable isn't defined yet and you can't evaluate their work. Instead, staged:

- **Stage 0 (now, ¥0):** Self-produce 2 weeks from this pack. This creates the baseline data any freelancer would need anyway.
- **Stage 1 (cheap, per-piece):** If Chinese delivery/copy is the bottleneck → hire a native speaker per-video for copy-polish + pronunciation check. ¥50–150/video on 淘宝/闲鱼/Boss直聘 part-time, or a local university student. This is likely all you need.
- **Stage 2 (only after signal):** If a video format demonstrably converts and volume is the constraint → part-time XHS/Douyin operator (~¥3–5k/month part-time) to edit + post + manage comments while you film and sell.
- **Never outsource:** your face, your classroom stories, principal WeChat conversations. That's the moat.

## 10. MEASUREMENT + THE CONVERSION PATH

**The path:** video → profile → WeChat (bio/私信) → 15-min demo (screen-share or in-person Beijing) → 1-classroom free pilot (30 days, existing trial system) → paid via Alipay/WeChat invoice rails (already built, Session 111).

**Weekly review (10 min):**
- Completion rate per video (platform analytics) — the master lever.
- 私信/WeChat adds — the only conversion metric that matters this month.
- Which HOOK generated the adds (ask new contacts: 哪条视频看到我的?).

**Success in 30 days ≠ virality.** Success = 3–5 principal demos booked from ~20 posted videos + an XHS account that makes you credible on contact. One pilot school that converts pays for the whole month.
