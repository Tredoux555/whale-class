## 1 — Hard brand rules (NEVER break these)

1. **NEVER name the founder.** Never say "Tredoux", "Tredoux Willemse", or any specific person built Montree. If a comment asks who's behind it: *"Montree is built by a practicing AMS-certified Montessori educator. Montree Limited is registered in Hong Kong."* No first names, no school identifiers (no city, no class age range like "PreK 4 in Beijing").
2. **NEVER promise outcomes you can't deliver.** No "guaranteed", no "your kids will read by 4", no medical/developmental claims about specific children.
3. **NEVER compare Montree to a specific competitor by name** (no "better than Transparent Classroom / Montessori Compass / Lillio"). You can say "the next step in classroom management" — that's it.
4. **NEVER use the gold "M" mark as the avatar** on a video thumbnail — use the **sprout logo** (the leaf-and-stem mark). The gold M is the wordmark partner, not a standalone identity.
5. **Always include `montree.xyz`** in every caption (in the bio link too).
6. **No emojis in LinkedIn captions.** Use sparingly elsewhere (1–2 max, never emoji-stuffing).
7. **No misleading thumbnails.** Don't tease something the video doesn't deliver — TikTok and Instagram algorithms punish bait-and-switch.

## 2 — Headline / first-line formulas (the "hook")

The first 1–3 seconds of a short and the first 60–80 characters of the caption decide whether the video gets watched. Use ONE of these formulas per video — never combine.

| # | Formula | Example |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Photo → Outcome** | "Photo in. Progress report out. That's it." |
| 2 | **Pain → Relief** | "Teachers spend 4 hours a week on observations. Now it's 0." |
| 3 | **POV** | "POV: you took 47 photos this week. Montree wrote every observation." |
| 4 | **Provocation** | "What if teachers never wrote another observation again?" |
| 5 | **Declarative + identity** | "Montessori classrooms now run on AI. Quietly." |
| 6 | **The secret** | "The secret every Montessori principal wishes they had." |
| 7 | **Specificity / numbers** | "12 languages. 1 teacher. 20 children. Reports done by Friday." |
| 8 | **Maria voice** | "*'Tend to the child, not the observation.'* — Maria Montessori, 1909. Montree, 2026." |
| 9 | **Direct address** | "If you're a Montessori principal, watch this." |
| 10 | **Demo prompt** | "Watch a teacher run her whole week in 30 seconds." |

**Rule:** the hook in the caption must match the hook in the video's first 2 seconds. If they diverge, retention dies.

## 6 — Title heuristics (the in-video heading + the thumbnail text)

If a video has burned-in text or a thumbnail, the heading should:
- Be **≤ 7 words.**
- Have **one verb.**
- **Never** be a question (questions test poorly in feed-based formats — they invite a "no" scroll).
- **Be the same as the caption hook** OR a tighter version of it.

**Good thumbnail headings:**
- *Photo → progress report.*
- *12 languages. One teacher.*
- *AI did the observation.*
- *Friday at 3pm. Reports done.*
- *Montessori, automated.*

**Bad headings (do not use):**
- *Have you tried Montree?* (question)
- *The AI revolution in Montessori classrooms is here* (too long, too vague)
- *You won't believe this* (clickbait — algorithm punishes)
- *Try the magic of Montree today* (sales-speak)

## 8 — Engagement playbook (the first 60 minutes after posting)

The first hour shapes the next 24. Algorithmic feeds reward posts with early engagement.

1. **Reply to every comment in the first hour.** Even "thanks" gets a reply.
2. **Pin one comment** that restates the hook or links to montree.xyz (e.g. *"Try it free for 30 days → montree.xyz"*).
3. **If a comment asks a real question** (price, languages, AMS compatibility, recording video calls, parent portal) — answer it factually. Pull from the brand answer bank in section 9 below.
4. **Never argue.** If someone says Montessori shouldn't use screens, reply once: *"Montree is a tool for teachers and principals. Children don't use it. The teacher takes a photo at the moment of observation — that's the only screen contact."* Then move on.
5. **Don't reply to obvious bots.** Hide them if abusive; ignore otherwise.

## 10 — Per-video posting workflow (do this exactly, every time)

For each video Tredoux sends you to post:

1. **Identify the video number.** The campaign is 13 videos (1 hero + 12 feature). Note which one this is so the hook matches the video's content.
2. **Pick ONE hook formula** from section 2 — the one that best matches the video's content angle.
3. **Build the caption** using the platform template in section 3. Replace `[HOOK]` and `[1 line value statement]`.
4. **Append the hashtag recipe** for that platform from section 5.
5. **Upload the video.**
6. **Set the title / heading** to ≤ 60 characters following section 6 heuristics.
7. **Pick a thumbnail** that shows either (a) the avatar's face mid-sentence with a calm expression, or (b) a clean text card with the heading from section 6. Never a thumbnail of materials with no human or no text.
8. **Schedule** (don't post live) — pick the window from section 7.
9. **First hour after post fires:** open the platform, reply to every comment, pin a CTA comment, hide abusive bots.
10. **Log it.** Add to the spreadsheet: video #, platform, post time, headline used, hook formula # used, hashtag recipe used. (This builds the dataset for learning what works.)

## 12 — What success looks like

You're doing this right when:

- Views per video on TikTok exceed 1,000 within 24 hours, on Reels exceed 800, on Shorts exceed 500, on LinkedIn exceed 200 impressions.
- The first comment within an hour is something other than spam (a real question, an emoji, a tag).
- The retention curve on TikTok / Reels shows ≥ 50% of viewers reach the 3-second mark.
- The bio link click-through rate is ≥ 1% of views.
- The comments mention `montree.xyz` or "the magic of Montree" — that means the messaging is sticking.

You're doing this wrong when:

- Captions read sales-y or hype-y.
- The founder is named.
- Hashtag count exceeds the platform recipe.
- The thumbnail says "you won't believe this".
- Replies argue with detractors.
