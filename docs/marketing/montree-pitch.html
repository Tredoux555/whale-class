Demo Cheat Sheet
1
Open — Build the moment
~2 min
Start with the Friday feeling. "Think about the end of the week. Reports unwritten. Observations you haven't captured. Parents who are going to ask questions on Monday. What's on your desk?"
Let them answer. Let them feel it. Then: "What if none of that existed?"
Don't pitch yet. Ask one question first: "How many hours a week would you say your teachers spend on admin — observations, reports, tracking progress?" (The answer is almost always 10–20. Remember it.)
2
The demo — one photo
~5 min, the heart of it
Take a photo of literally anything Montessori. A shelf, a mat, your own table if needed. The visual recognition is the product.
While it processes: "A teacher in my classroom does this once. That's it. No dropdown. No typing. No observation form. One photo — and the rest happens automatically."
When the result appears: Show the work name recognised, the progress record, the child it's linked to.
Then click the quick guide. "Any work in the curriculum — whether you've presented it a hundred times or never — the guide is here. Materials. Steps. Why it matters at this stage. A new teacher can walk into a classroom and present like a veteran."
If they look skeptical about recognition accuracy: "It learns. Every correction a teacher makes teaches it the difference. Our classroom trained it over months. When a new school joins, it gets better fast."
3
The shelf — what Montessori actually prescribed
~3 min
Open a child's shelf. "Every Monday morning. Five works. One from each curriculum area. Not what the teacher remembers — what the evidence says this child needs next."
The line that lands: "The Montessori curriculum is 100 years deep. Maria Montessori spent a lifetime developing this sequence. Montree makes sure none of it gets left on a shelf because a teacher is tired or hasn't encountered it before."
This is the confidence point: "You open the app on Monday and the guidance is better than what you could have produced yourself. That's a different kind of morning."

"It does better than I can. And I'm an AMS-certified teacher who built the system."

4
Parent reports — the most underrated feature
~3 min
Show a generated parent report. Point to the photos, the narrative, the curriculum language.
"This is what a parent receives. Not a template. Not a checkbox. A genuine account of what their child is learning and why — with the photos as evidence."
The insight that changes minds: "When parents already know, there's nothing difficult to say at pickup. The meeting that was going to be uncomfortable doesn't happen. The worried parent becomes a confident one."
Then: "Montree doesn't just support teachers. It sells the school. Every report a parent reads is a reason to stay, and a reason to tell someone."
If they ask about personalisation: "The AI reads the child's history before writing. It knows what they worked on, what they're ready for, and how to frame it for a parent. Every report is unique."

6
Close — one ask, clearly made
~1 min
One classroom. One teacher. Two weeks. "Let me set it up in one of your classrooms. Not a commitment — just enough to see it work. I'll be there for the setup call personally."
If hesitation: "What would you need to see to feel confident? Tell me and I'll show you that specifically."
If yes: Get an email. Confirm the day. Send them montree.xyz before you leave the room.
↯
Objections — stay calm, stay specific
as needed
"We already have a system." → "What does it do with photos? Who writes the parent reports? How does the shelf get decided?" (They'll tell you the gaps. You don't have to.)
"Our teachers are resistant to technology." → "The only thing teachers do in Montree is take a photo. Everything else is automatic. If they can use a camera, they can use Montree."
"Is it AMI-compatible?" → "The curriculum is based on AMI scope and sequence — all 384 works, five areas. The terminology, the sequence, the sensitive periods — it's all there."
"What if the AI gets it wrong?" → "Teachers correct it. And every correction makes it smarter — not just for them, but for the whole classroom. The more you use it, the better it gets."
"We're a small school, I'm not sure we need this." → "Small schools feel admin pressure more than anyone. One teacher doing the work of three. That's exactly who this is for."
★
The reframe — when they ask about cost
use this if they hesitate on price
Don't defend the price. Reframe what it is. "Most schools think of this as software. It isn't. It's three things you're already paying for — advertising, teacher training, and teacher support — collapsed into one monthly cost."
Advertising: "Every report Montree writes is a reason a parent stays — and a reason they tell someone. Word of mouth is your best marketing. Montree generates it automatically, every week."
Teacher training: "384 works. Every guide, every step, in seconds. A new teacher walks in and presents like a veteran. That's not software — that's training."
Teacher support: "The observations, the progress tracking, the parent reports — done. Better than a tired person could do it on a Friday. That's what Montree costs you. What does not having it cost?"

"It's not costing you money. It's saving you money and upgrading your school from the core out."
