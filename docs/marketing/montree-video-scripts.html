The Story
Duration 5 minutes Words ~750 Pace Story-pace. Personal. Tone Honest. Authoritative. Warm.
Opens with the truth

I'm going to tell you something that took me years to realise.

Montessori teaching is extraordinary. The method works. The curriculum is a hundred years of wisdom, distilled into a system that meets children exactly where they are.

But somewhere between the classroom and the paperwork — we lose something.

The problem

I'm Tredoux. I'm an AMS-certified Montessori teacher. I also built Montree — because the problem I'm about to describe was my problem. Every single week.

By Thursday, I had twenty children's observations in my head and not enough of them on paper. By Friday afternoon, I had reports to write from memory. Progress to update across five curriculum areas. Works to plan for twenty different children — at twenty different stages.

And I had to do all of it tired.

Fifteen to twenty hours a week. Not teaching. Admin.

[pause]

And I thought — this shouldn't exist. Not in a system as elegant as Montessori.

[pause]

So I built Montree.

One photo

A teacher takes a photo. That's it. That's the entire input.

Montree sees the photo and identifies the work. It knows the difference between Sandpaper Letters and Sandpaper Numbers. Between the Trinomial Cube and the Binomial Cube. And if it's wrong — one correction, and it gets smarter. Not just for that teacher. For the whole classroom. It learns.

The observation is recorded. The child's progress is updated. All of it, automatically, in the time it takes to lower the phone.

Monday morning

You open the app. Each child has a shelf.

Five works. One from each curriculum area. Selected by the system based on their actual progress — not what you remember, not what you meant to get to. What the evidence says they're ready for right now.

And if the shelf suggests something you haven't presented before? The guide is right there. Method. Materials. What the child should discover. Why it matters developmentally. Thirty seconds — and you walk in confident.

A new teacher walks in and presents like someone with twenty years of experience. Because the curriculum is right there. All of it. All 384 works. Accessible in seconds.

The parent reports

By the end of the week — the reports are written.

Not templates. Real, detailed, photo-evidenced accounts of what each child worked on, what they're developing, and what comes next. In language that's warm and accessible and builds trust.

The parent who might have complained on Monday already knows the answer. The meeting that was going to be difficult doesn't happen. And that same parent tells a friend about the school.

That's not a feature. That's the point.

What you're actually paying for

Think about what you're really bringing into the school when you use Montree.

You're paying for your most effective advertising — because parents who feel genuinely informed become your best ambassadors. That referral engine runs every week, automatically.

You're paying for teacher training — because every work in the Montessori curriculum is documented, guided, and available to any teacher, on day one.

And you're paying for teacher support — because the work that drains a teacher at the end of every week is done. Better than they could do it tired. Done automatically.

One monthly cost. Three things your school was already spending on — now done better than before.
