## 1 — Group Lesson Suggester · `group-lessons`
*9:16 · ~24s · talking-head*

**NARRATION** *(5 scenes)*

> You know every child's level in every area. But who has time to cross-reference twenty children to find the ones ready for the same lesson?

> Montree does it the moment you open your dashboard.

> "Henry, Kevin, Lucky and Stella are all ready for two-handed pouring — present it together on Tuesday." It reads across your whole class and finds the groups you'd never spot by hand.

> One presentation instead of four. More time on the mat, less time planning.

> This is what it looks like when your classroom thinks with you. The magic of Montree — montree.xyz.

**On-screen captions**

| Scene | Caption |
|---|---|
| 1 | Who's ready for the same lesson? |
| 2 | Montree finds the group for you |
| 3 | "These four are ready — present it together" |
| 4 | One presentation, not four |
| 5 | The magic of Montree · montree.xyz |

**Producer note —** The hook names a real chore (cross-referencing 20 kids) and
the payoff is a concrete, named group + a day. The number that sells it is the
collapse from *four presentations to one*. Keep the named children in scene 3 —
specificity is what makes it feel real, not generic.

**3-second cutdowns to A/B against scene 1:**
"Your classroom just told you who's ready for the same lesson." ·
"Stop cross-referencing twenty children by hand."

## 2 — Curriculum Gap Radar · `gap-radar`
*9:16 · ~24s · talking-head*

**NARRATION** *(5 scenes)*

> Every teacher has a blind spot. An area of the curriculum that quietly goes weeks without a single child touching it — and you don't notice until it's been a month.

> Montree notices on day one.

> "Mathematics has gone quiet — twenty days since the last Maths work, while the rest of your room has been busy this week." It watches every shelf, and tells you the one that's slipped.

> Not a scolding. A gentle radar — so nothing in your classroom drifts.

> Montree keeps an eye on the whole curriculum, so you can keep your eyes on the children. montree.xyz.

**On-screen captions**

| Scene | Caption |
|---|---|
| 1 | Every teacher has a blind spot |
| 2 | Montree catches it on day one |
| 3 | "Maths has gone quiet — 20 days" |
| 4 | A gentle radar, not a scolding |
| 5 | The magic of Montree · montree.xyz |

**Producer note —** The emotional truth is the relief of *not having to hold it
all in your head*. Scene 3's exact phrasing mirrors the real product output —
keep it verbatim so a teacher who's seen the card recognises it. "Not a
scolding" defuses the fear that the tool is judging them.

**3-second cutdowns to A/B against scene 1:**
"Which shelf has your class quietly stopped using?" ·
"Montree watches the corner of the curriculum you forgot."

## 3 — Predictive Readiness · `predictive-readiness`
*⏸ ON HOLD — do not produce yet.*

Predicting *when* a child will master a work needs clean presented→mastered
timing data. As of June 2026 that data isn't trustworthy yet (mastery dates
aren't reliably stamped, and presentation dates default to import date). The
feature would produce confident-but-wrong dates — the opposite of the brand
promise. Script drafts when the data foundation is in place and a few months of
clean timestamps have accrued. (This is the network-effect moat doing its job:
it genuinely needs time + real classroom use to become accurate.)
