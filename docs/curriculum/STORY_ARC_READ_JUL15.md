## 4. Potato-gag discipline — 2 VIOLATIONS FOUND (beyond the known W11 grandfather)

The spec's own W58 celebration text makes an explicit promise: *"the POTATO — shouted from pictures
since Week 1, never once printed in 58 weeks — is PRINTED at last."* Checking every `book.spreads[].text`
field for the literal string "potato":

- **W01, spread 10: `"It's a POTATO!"`** — printed in full caps as page text.
- **W03, spread 9: `"And… a POTATO is on the mat!"`** — printed in full caps as page text.
- **W11, spread 9: `"A potato in the pot?!"`** — printed (this one is the known, Tredoux-approved
  grandfather per standing session notes — not flagged as new).

By contrast, the later reveal pattern the story establishes and keeps at **W22** and **W26** is
correct: the artNote explicitly says *"No noun printed — the class SHOUTS the word from the picture"*
and the page text itself only ever has punctuation ("The vet is...?!", "It can...?!") — never the word.
**W01 and W03 break that convention two ways: earlier than W11, and with the word fully spelled out in
caps rather than withheld.** Since only W11 is on record as a deliberate exception, W01 and W03 read as
genuine drift — most likely because these are the earliest-authored weeks (session notes: "W3-4
Fable-authored exemplars"), predating the "no noun printed" rule that later weeks (and the W58 payoff
itself) depend on to make sense. As written, the W58 line "never once printed in 58 weeks" is currently
false — it's printed 3 times before W58, not the 1 grandfathered time.

   Week-15/Week-16/Week-24/Week-25/Week-6 callbacks are called out by name in the artNotes for nearly
   every spread, culminating in the potato's own graduation-cap cameo. Strong payoff.

   W42, matching the doc's own claim), though the celebration text's "the whole cast crosses the
   bridge" slightly overstates a scene that only actually features 4 characters (Sheep, Snake, Chick +

   `MASTER_SPINE.md`'s own "Total: 5 vowel lights + 32 leaves + 1 crown"), the potato is finally
