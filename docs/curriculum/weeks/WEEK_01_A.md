# WEEK 1 — A
**Word unlocked: a** · "It's a potato." · Vowels on the wall · a vs an

## 1. Target language — the letter's THREE jobs this week

1. **The SOUND /ă/** — the phonics core. Words that BEGIN with /ă/:
   **apple · ant · alligator · astronaut · ambulance · anchor · abacus · acrobat · axe · add**
   🚨 Keep the sound pure — EXCLUDE words where 'a' says something else: ~~acorn, ape, apron~~ (long ā), ~~arm, art~~ (ar), ~~all, ball~~ (aw), ~~airplane~~ (ai). The basket is short /ă/ only.
2. **The word:** a — one letter, already a word. The frame "It's a ___." names the entire classroom: a chair, a table, a mat, a cup, a book, a pencil, a door.
3. **The article:** a vs **an** — an apple, an egg, an ant. The excuse to put all five vowels on the wall.

The sound layer is what Week 2 stands on — "at" only exists for a child who owns /ă/.

### 🚨 THE COLLISION RULE (the joint of the week — handle deliberately)

The /ă/ words are exactly the words that CAN'T take "a" — apple, ant, alligator all demand **an**. The letter a generates both the article and its own exception. That's the point, not a problem — but the three materials must NEVER mix mid-sentence:

1. **Frame drilling** (Naming Walk, object tray, SONG) — consonant-start objects ONLY: a cup, a mat, a chair. "It's a ___" stays pure. No apple in the song, ever.
2. **Sound games** (basket, I Spy) — NO article at all: "/ă/! — ant!" Sound + noun, nothing else.
3. **The pocket sort** — the one place they collide, on purpose, Wednesday, as the twist: "an apple… listen — /ă/-apple. It starts with a WALL letter. Wall letter → an."

The vowel wall is the an-detector. That's why the vowels go up in Week 1 — a child checks the wall to pick a or an. The rule stays operable all year.

## 1b. Sound work — /ă/

**Sound basket (the shelf work):** a basket of 6–8 miniatures/objects beginning with /ă/ — apple, ant, alligator, astronaut, ambulance, anchor (+ the class abacus). Presented like any Montessori sound game: say the SOUND, never the letter name. "/ă/ — apple. /ă/ — ant."

**I Spy (Dwyer sound game, daily at circle):** 3–4 objects from the basket on a mat. "I spy, with my little eye, something that begins with… /ă/-/ă/-/ă/…" Child picks, class confirms: "/ă/ — ant!" By Friday, flip it: hold the object, children give the sound.

**Sandpaper 'a' pairing:** child traces the sandpaper 'a' → says /ă/ → picks one object from the basket that starts with it. Trace, say, match. That's the sound-symbol lock.

**/ă/ sound cards (printable):** 8 picture cards for the shelf and the wall under the big vowel A. Midjourney — same book suffix, subjects:
`a shiny red apple` · `a single black ant, macro` · `a small green alligator figurine` · `a toy astronaut` · `a toy ambulance` · `a brass anchor, miniature` · `a wooden abacus` · `a tiny acrobat mid-handstand`

## 4. Circle-time game — The Naming Walk + The Vowel Ceremony

**Naming Walk (daily):** teacher carries a pointer/wand, touches an object, class chants "It's a ___!" Speed rounds by Friday. Kids take turns being the pointer — the pointer picks, the class answers.

**A/AN sort (from Wednesday):** two big wall pockets labeled **a** and **an**. Picture cards (chair, cup, mat, book, pencil, table / apple, egg, ant, orange, umbrella). Child draws a card, class says it aloud, child posts it in the right pocket. Self-correcting: an-cards have a small red dot on the back.

**Vowel Ceremony (Monday, once):** the five vowel cards go up on the wall one at a time, class repeats each. Then the Bingo Vowel Song for the first time. The wall stays all year — each vowel "lights up" (gets its gold star) the week its sound is taught: A now, I in W8, O in W11, E in W14, U in W16.

## 6. The week at a glance

- **Mon:** Vowel Ceremony + Bingo Vowel Song + sandpaper 'a' → /ă/ + I Spy with the sound basket + Naming Walk begins
- **Tue:** I Spy /ă/ + object tray presented + trace-say-match work on the shelf
- **Wed:** a/an twist introduced ("an apple — try saying 'a apple'… feels wrong!") + pocket sort goes up
- **Thu:** Book arrives — first read-aloud, class echo-reads, shouts the potato page. I Spy flipped (object → child gives the sound).
- **Fri:** Suno track drops in circle time. Speed-round Naming Walk. Every child says one "It's a ___" solo.

**Daily constant:** I Spy /ă/ at circle, every day, 3 minutes. The sound game is the heartbeat; everything else rotates around it.

**Success check:** by Friday every child can (1) give /ă/ when shown the letter and name something that starts with it, (2) point at anything in the room and say "It's a ___", (3) chant A-E-I-O-U — and some are already self-correcting "a apple → an apple."
