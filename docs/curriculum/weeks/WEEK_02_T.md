# WEEK 2 — T
**Word unlocked: at** · "She's at the park." · The Big Map · builds on: a (W1)

---

## 1. Target language — the letter's three jobs

1. **The SOUND /t/** — words that BEGIN with /t/:
   **turtle · tiger · tomato · taxi · teddy · towel · toothbrush · table · ten**
   🚨 Keep the sound pure — EXCLUDE: ~~the, this, three, think~~ (th digraph — different sound; "the" comes in W4), ~~train, tree, truck~~ (tr blends — save for later). The basket is clean initial /t/ only.
2. **The word: at** — a + t. The FIRST word they build from two taught sounds. /ă/…/t/… at. This is the moment blending begins.
3. **The frame: "She's at the ___."** — real location language, lived on the Big Map. Where is Segina? She's at the park / at school / at the mall.

Grammar note (teach by ear, never explain): "at THE park / mall / zoo" but "at school / at home." The frame carries it; kids absorb the exception.

---

## 1b. Sound work — /t/

**Sound basket:** turtle, tiger, tomato, taxi, teddy, towel, toothbrush miniatures — plus the classroom TABLE (point: "/t/ — table!" they already own the word from W1).

**I Spy (daily, 3 min):** same ritual as W1. "I spy something that begins with… /t/-/t/-/t/." Mid-week: mix /ă/ and /t/ objects in one basket — first sound DISCRIMINATION game.

**Sandpaper 't' pairing:** trace → say /t/ → match an object.

**The blending moment (the week's crown):** sandpaper 'a' + sandpaper 't' side by side. Finger on a: /ă/. Finger on t: /t/. Slide: "at." First read word built from sounds. Do it at circle with drama. When a child does it alone, that's a Montree photo.

## 2. Songs — TWO per week (locked from here on)

Every week ships two Suno tracks: **2a. the SOUND song** (the letter + basket vocab,
stutter pattern "T-t-turtle!" isolates the pure sound — no schwa, no letter name) and
**2b. the WORD song** (the week's sentence frame). Same dark-trap style string = one album.

---

### 2a. SOUND SONG — "T-T-TURTLE"

Paste into Suno:

**Title:** T-T-Turtle

**Style:**
```
dark trap, 68 bpm, heavy 808 bass, sparse hi-hats, deep whisper-rap call,
kids choir response, playful spooky, minimal, clean vocals, nursery trap
```

**Lyrics:**
```
[Intro — whispered]
t… t… t… t…
(tick, tick, tick)

[Hook 1 — kids chant]
T! T! T-t-t!
What begins with T?
T-t-turtle! T-t-turtle!
It's a turtle! (a turtle!)

[Hook 2 — kids chant]
T! T! T-t-t!
What begins with T?
T-t-tiger! T-t-tiger!
It's a tiger! (a tiger!)

[Hook 3 — kids chant]
T! T! T-t-t!
What begins with T?
T-t-tomato! T-t-tomato!
It's a tomato! (a tomato!)

[Breakdown — whispered, bass drops out]
t… t… t…
t-t-taxi… t-t-teddy… t-t-towel…

[Final Hook — big]
T! T! T-t-t!
What begins with T?
A potato?! (NO!)
P-p-potato! Not T!
T-t-table! T-t-table!
It's a table, table, table!
```

ONE pattern: "T-t-X! It's a X!" — the stutter is the sound isolation, and the answer
line REUSES Week 1's frame (It's a ___) so the songs stack like the letters do. The
potato tries to sneak in and the class shouts NO — that's initial-sound discrimination
disguised as a joke, and it mirrors the mixed-basket I Spy game mid-week. Ends on
"table" — a real /t/ thing they can slap on the beat.

### 2b. WORD SONG — "WHERE IS SEGINA?"

Paste into Suno:

**Title:** Where Is Segina?

**Style:**
```
dark trap, 68 bpm, heavy 808 bass, sparse hi-hats, deep whisper-rap call,
kids choir response, playful spooky, minimal, clean vocals, nursery trap
```

**Lyrics:**
```
[Intro — whispered]
t… t… t…
Where is Sejeena?

[Hook 1 — call and response]
Where is Sejeena? (where? where?)
Where is Sejeena? (where? where?)
She's at the park! At the park!
She's at the park, park, park!

[Hook 2 — call and response]
Where is Sejeena? (where? where?)
Where is Sejeena? (where? where?)
She's at school! At school!
She's at school, school, school!

[Breakdown — whispered, bass drops out]
t… t… t…
at… at… at…
where is Sejeena…?

[Hook 3 — call and response]
Where is Sejeena? (where? where?)
Where is Sejeena? (where? where?)
She's at the mall! At the mall!
She's at the mall, mall, mall!

[Final Hook — quiet, then big]
Where is Sejeena? (where? where?)
Where is Sejeena? (shhh…)
She's at home. She's in bed.
Sejeena is asleep…
WAKE UP, SEJEENA!
```

🚨 Name spelling: lyrics use **Sejeena** (phonetic, so Suno pronounces it
/se-JEE-na/). Everything PRINTED — book, cards, dictionary, map — uses **Segina** (her real spelling).

ONE pattern: "Where is Segina? She's at the ___." Place swapped per hook. Ends on the
book's twist (home, asleep) and a full-class shout. In circle, swap Segina for real
children's names and real places — the song is a machine, the class feeds it.

**Unplugged circle version:** same words to Frère Jacques ("Where is Segina? Where is
Segina? At the park! At the park!") — the daily map-game soundtrack, no speaker needed.

## 4. Circle game — The Big Map

**The Map (A3/A2 print, laminated):** illustrated town — park, school, mall, police
station, zoo, bus stop, supermarket, houses — connected by roads. Lives on the shelf
with Segina + animal figures all year.

**Daily game:** teacher hides Segina at a location while eyes are closed. "Where is
Segina?" — class chants, one child finds her: "She's at the zoo!" Then children take
turns hiding her. Friday: two figures ("Where is Segina? Where is the teddy?").

**Shelf work:** map + Segina + 3-4 figures in a basket. Child places figures, says the
sentence. Photo-worthy work.

## 7. The week at a glance

- **Mon:** sandpaper 't' + /t/ I Spy + Big Map ceremony (map goes up, Segina introduced, first hide)
- **Tue:** map game + sound basket work + "at" built with sandpaper letters at circle (THE moment)
- **Wed:** /ă/ + /t/ mixed basket discrimination + map shelf work presented
- **Thu:** Book arrives — Where Is Segina? Class echo-reads, whispers the last page
- **Fri:** Suno track drops. Kids' names in the song. Every child hides + finds Segina solo.

**Daily constant:** I Spy /t/ + one round of the map game. W1 materials stay on the shelf.

**Success check:** by Friday every child can (1) give /t/ and name something starting
with it, (2) blend /ă/-/t/ → "at" with the sandpaper letters, (3) answer "Where is
Segina?" with "She's at the ___" for any location on the map.
