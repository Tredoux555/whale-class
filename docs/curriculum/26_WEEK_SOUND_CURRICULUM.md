# 26-Week Sound Curriculum — Master Framework
**Tredoux · ESL Montessori · Beijing · ages 3–6 · one sound per week**

> instruction at the bottom (prompts in Section 7), and it will generate whatever you need

> curriculum. Do not wire into Montree code.

## 1. The Vision (Tredoux's words — preserved)

"I want to teach one sound per week. Then the lessons that progress forward build on the
sounds that we learn. My thought is we bomb it with language. First start with a — it's a
natural first letter and the most common letter and it's a word by itself. 'It's a —
potato.' And in the same week we teach the difference between a and an — it gives me an
excuse to put the vowels on the wall — and we learn the bingo vowel song. Kids learn so
much faster than we give them credit for when they're engaged.

Week 2 is 't' making 'at' — a big map in the circle. Where is Kevin? He's at the mall,
he's at the park, he's at school. Week 3 is 'm' making 'mat' — prepositions. Where is the
apple? It's on the mat, under the mat, next to the mat. And the etiquette of Montessori
work and the importance of the mat. Week 4 we go to C and we have cat. This week the
whole class can read: The cat is on the mat — the first book. This week we start digraphs
and sight words: 'th' and 'the'. The book: left page repeats 'Where is the cat?' — right
page 'The cat is on the mat', 'The cat is next to the mat', 'The cat levitating above the
mat'. We can have real fun with Midjourney here — realistic graphics that are awesome and
funny.

Then each week a new book to read and some Montessori work to back up that letter, and the
ones we did in previous weeks stay on the shelves. All weeks, one letter per week — a
26-week curriculum. After 26 weeks the kids have all the letters and 26 books. And the
SONG — the song links it all: the letter, the books, the sentences — they all complement
each other."

**The logic:** Real utility first. Decoding second. Comprehension emerges naturally. Each
new letter unlocks a word that unlocks a real sentence the child can USE that same week.
Grammar is embedded in context, never taught as grammar. The environment teaches — the
mat, the shelf, the map, the classroom objects. Funny beats earnest: if it makes them
laugh, they remember.

## 2. The Seven Elements of Every Week

1. **Letter/sound** — one per week, in the sequence below (never a standard scope & sequence — THIS order)
2. **SOUND WORK** — the phonics core, every week, no exceptions: an initial-sound word list (words BEGINNING with the sound, pure — exclude words where the letter says something else), a sound basket of miniatures, daily I Spy at circle (Dwyer sound game — the SOUND, never the letter name), and sandpaper-letter trace→say→match. This is the layer everything else stands on.
3. **Word(s) unlocked** — built ONLY from letters already taught
4. **One real sentence** — usable in actual classroom life that week
5. **Activity focus** — the Montessori shelf work + circle game that lives the sentence
6. **Songs — TWO per week:** (a) the **SOUND song** — the letter + sound-basket vocab, stutter pattern ("T-t-turtle!") to isolate the pure sound with no schwa and no letter name, answer line reuses a prior week's frame so songs stack cumulatively; (b) the **WORD song** — the week's sentence frame. One pattern per song; lyrics only from taught words + minimal glue. Same dark-trap style string across all tracks = one album. (W1 is the exception: "It's A (Potato)" covers both jobs — a IS the sound and the word.)
7. **Book** — cumulative reader; question on the left page, answer + picture on the right; Midjourney art; absurd and funny

Previous weeks' materials STAY on the shelf. The library grows as the children grow.

## 3. Rules (non-negotiable)

- **Decodability:** book and song text uses only taught letters + deliberately introduced glue words.
- **Glue/sight words:** `the` + `is` (W4, with digraph th) · `I` (W6, for "Can I…?") · `and` becomes fully decodable W10 · `on` used orally from W3, becomes DECODABLE W11 — celebrate that moment ("you've been saying this word all year — now you can read it") · `says` (W25). Oral-with-picture-support allowed: `where`, `your`.
- **Weeks 1–3 books are teacher-read/echo-read picture books** (the child "reads" via memory + pictures). From W4 every book is child-decodable. That's how 26 weeks = 26 books — and it makes W4 the moment they can *really* read one themselves.
- **The cast grows:** Cat (W4) → Ant (W6) → Dog (W12) → Rat (W15) → Bug (W18) → Duck (W21) → Fox (W24). The books become an ensemble universe — characters return, the vet visit in W22 reunites everyone.
- **Songs:** one per week, to a tune the children already know. Reused tunes are deliberate (familiar frames). The counting tune (Ten Little Indians) is always the counting tune.
- **Vowel wall:** all 5 vowels go up W1 (a/an lesson). Each vowel lights up as its sound is taught: A(1), I(8), O(11), E(14), U(16). W16 = vowel wall complete = celebration week.
- **ESL always:** these children acquire the language and the reading simultaneously. Every sentence must be immediately usable in real life.
- **No worksheet grind.** The book, the map, the mat, the objects in the room are the materials. Teacher builds physical materials (Bambu 3D printer, Midjourney, laminator).

## 6. Book Format (locked)

- Left page: the repeating question ("Where is the cat?")
- Right page: picture + answer text above ("The cat is on the mat.")
- 6–10 spreads. Last spread is the twist/laugh.
- Midjourney: realistic, absurd, funny. The levitating cat is the standard. Laughter is retention.
- Print A5 landscape, laminate, book corner in the English area.

## Song Format (locked)

- Known kindergarten tune, named per week in the spine.
- Lyrics from that week's cumulative bank + minimal glue only.
- 2 verses max + repetition. Must survive 25 three-year-olds shouting it.
- The song IS the link: it carries the letter sound, the target word, and the book's sentence in one melody.

## 7. Reusable Prompts (paste this whole doc + one of these)

**PROMPT 1 — Build a week in full:**
"Build Week [N] in full from the spine: (1) target word list from the cumulative bank, (2) complete song lyrics to the named tune, (3) the full book script page by page with a Midjourney prompt per spread, (4) the circle-time game script, (5) the Montessori shelf materials to make, with 3D-printable or printable specs where possible. Follow all rules in Section 3."

**PROMPT 2 — Song only:**
"Write the Week [N] song in full: named tune, only words from the cumulative bank at that checkpoint plus listed glue words, 2 verses + chorus, with movement/gesture notes for circle time."

**PROMPT 3 — Book only:**
"Write the Week [N] reader page by page: left-page repeating question, right-page answer + picture description, one Midjourney prompt per spread (realistic, absurd, funny), decodability check against the cumulative bank, and a twist ending."

**PROMPT 4 — Materials list:**
"For Week [N], list every physical material for the English shelf: what to print, laminate, 3D print, or buy. Include the map, cards, objects, and book. Keep previous weeks' materials in mind — nothing leaves the shelf."

## 8. Production Pipeline (locked)

**Books — Midjourney → Canva:**
1. Canva: one master doc "26 Readers — Book Template", **A5 landscape**. Left page: dark forest background `#0a1a0f`, big white question text. Right page: full-bleed image, answer text in a bar on top.
2. **Font: Andika** (SIL, free) — upload to Canva Brand Kit. Single-storey Montessori 'a' — the 'a' in the books must be the 'a' the children write. Never a double-storey display font.
3. Midjourney: one consistent style suffix per book (given in each week's build file). Generate → upscale → drop into Canva.
4. Export print PDF → print A5 → laminate → ring-bind. Book corner in the English area.
5. Fallback assembly line: once MJ images exist in a folder, Fable can generate print-ready PDFs via HTML→Chrome (same pipeline as the Dark Phonics flashcards) if Canva gets tedious around book 10.

**Songs — Suno, dark trap, slow BPM:**
- One sonic identity for all 26 tracks — the year is an album.
- Base style string (adjust per week, keep the core):
  `dark trap, 65-72 bpm, heavy 808 bass, sparse hi-hats, deep whisper-rap verses, kids choir chant hook, playful spooky, minimal, clean vocals, nursery trap`
- Lyrics only from the week's cumulative bank + minimal glue. Hooks must survive 25 three-year-olds shouting them.
- **ONE SONG = ONE PATTERN.** Each track teaches exactly one frame ("It's a ___"), repeated with one word swapped per hook. ~10-15 unique words max. Never stack a second concept into a song — vowel chants, grammar rules, etc. live in their own rituals/materials. The song teaches the frame; the classroom fills the slot.
- Each week's build file contains a copy-paste Suno box: Title / Style / Lyrics.
- After generating: pick the take, save mp3, print a QR card for the shelf (same QR-to-song pattern as Dark Phonics).

**Week build files live at** `docs/curriculum/weeks/WEEK_NN_X.md` — one per week, generated with Prompt 1.

## 8b. THE WEEKLY PACK (locked — every week ships these)

One folder per week (`Desktop/English Curriculum 2026/Week NN/`). Design rule: **books are dark forest; all cards/worksheets are WHITE background** (ink rule) with the dark-green frame accent + Andika — same standard as the Dark Phonics flashcards.

1. **Book** — A5 reader (print same PDF at A3 scale-to-fit for circle time)
2. **Songs** — TWO Suno mp3s (sound song + word song) + QR cards for the shelf
3. **Letter worksheet** (A4) — big hollow letter to color + tracing rows + sound pictures to color
4. **3-part cards** — weekly vocab: control (picture+label) / picture / label. Pre-reading weeks: used as picture-object matching; label matching activates ~W4+
5. **Sentence match cards** — control strip / picture card / sentence card, from the week's book sentences
6. **Dictionary pages** (A4, journal format) — per row: line-art picture to COLOR + writing lines with the word in TRACEABLE gray + open space to WRITE it independently. Color + trace + write, one page per week into the "My Dictionary" binder → by June each child owns a dictionary they built (and colored, and wrote) themselves
7. **Coloring pages** — line-art outlines of the week's vocab (SVG, green stroke), word caption beneath. The star item gets a full page.
8. **Word–picture matching sheet** — words left, outline pictures right (shuffled), draw the line.
9. **Bingo** — 16 words: learnt vocab + sound-basket stretch words (new vocab acquired THROUGH the game). **6 unique 4×4 picture boards** (full pool per board, shuffled arrangement, THICK borders for laminating) + **duplex calling cards, 3×3 per page (~63mm)**: picture front, word back, back page col-mirrored per row, pages interleaved F1,B1,F2,B2 — print duplex, **flip on short edge**.
10. **Week-specific extras** (from the week file: e.g. W1 vowel wall cards + a/an pocket cards)

**🚨 ASSET MANIFEST RULE (locked):** every week file MUST contain a complete manifest of ALL images the week needs — book spreads + sound cards + bingo stretch words + extras — each with its ready-to-run MJ prompt. Nothing gets built until the manifest is written; Tredoux generates from the manifest in one MJ session, drops files in `Week NN/images/`, Fable assembles everything via the build-script pipeline (`docs/curriculum/tools/`).
