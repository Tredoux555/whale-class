### 2. Bidirectional Confusable Pairs
**File:** `app/api/montree/guru/corrections/route.ts`

When a teacher corrects A → B:
- Previously: writes negative on A ("NOT B")
- Now: ALSO writes reverse negative on B ("May look like A — [distinguishing features]")

Both directions of the confusable pair are captured. The reverse negative is built from Sonnet's `distinguishing_features` field in the correction analysis.

**Code location:** Lines 753–765 in corrections/route.ts. Three edits: `let reverseNegative = ''` declared after `isRealCorrection`, populated inside the `tool_use` handler when `distinguishing` is present, then written after `appendVisualFingerprint()`.

### 3. Auto-Sonnet for Low-Confidence Photos
**File:** `app/api/montree/photo-identification/process/route.ts`

Photos with `identification_confidence < 0.70` now auto-trigger `generateSonnetDraft()` fire-and-forget after Haiku completes. Previously this was only teacher-initiated via "Ask Sonnet" button.

### 4. VM Cap Raised 20 → 50
**File:** `lib/montree/photo-identification/context-loader.ts`

Pass 2 now loads up to 50 visual memory entries (was 20). Filtered to teacher-validated only (`teacher_setup` ≥1.0 OR `correction` ≥0.9 OR `is_custom=true`).

### 5. Area-Aware Pass 2b Candidates
**File:** `lib/montree/photo-identification/two-pass.ts`

Pass 2b (re-examination for low-confidence results) now filters candidate works to the same area as the initial guess, preventing cross-area confusion in the re-examination step.

### 6. Pass 1 Background Ignore (this session)
**File:** `lib/montree/photo-identification/two-pass.ts`

Added explicit instruction to the Pass 1 (vision/describe) system prompt:

> "⚠️ CRITICAL: Describe ONLY materials on the child's immediate work surface. COMPLETELY IGNORE everything visible in the background — shelves, walls, other children's mats, posters, cards hanging or stored nearby."

**Root cause of Sand Tray → Blue Series confusion:** Photos of children at the sand tray had large word cards (e.g., "up") visible on shelves/walls behind them. Pass 1 was describing those background cards as "secondary objects," which then caused Pass 2 to match Blue Series work. The new instruction should prevent this.

### 7. ✓ Correct Button on Sonnet Cards (this session)

The `sonnet_drafted` card block now has a green "✓ Correct" button (calls `onConfirmDraft`, same handler as `haiku_drafted` confirms) alongside the existing "✏️ Fix" button. Previously, after Sonnet reviewed a photo correctly, the only option was "✅ Accept" (which opens the full This Is sheet) or "✏️ Fix". The direct confirm was missing.

### Second attempt
Rewrote all 20 in first-person supervisor voice ("I introduced...", "I've observed..."). Hayden's entry was rewritten to honestly acknowledge no language documentation this month rather than asserting false mastery.

**File:** `scripts/build-language-report.mjs` — generates `Whale_Class_April_Language_Summary.docx`
**Output:** `/Users/tredouxwillemse/Desktop/Master Brain/ACTIVE/whale/Whale_Class_April_Language_Summary.docx`

### Known problem: DB data is unreliable
`montree_child_progress` is the current data source for language status. At least one confirmed case (Hayden) of incorrect mastery records. Likely others. The DB gets updated via:
1. Photo confirmations → `status='presented'`
2. Teacher manually updating via curriculum view
3. Weekly Wrap generation (which writes to the DB)

**Implication for Opus:** Any report that reads from `montree_child_progress` and presents `status='mastered'` as fact may be inaccurate. Reports should either (a) source from Weekly Wrap output which is richer and teacher-reviewed, or (b) clearly caveat that mastery requires teacher confirmation.

### The teacher's own description of Stella (sent this session)
> "Stella had a productive April in language. She moved through three distinct phases: initial exploration of Sandpaper Letters in weeks one and two, a spontaneous deepening of letter tracing in week three (sessions running 8–12 minutes without teacher initiation), and first attempts at the Moveable Alphabet in week four. 19 language works documented this month..."

This is the level of accuracy and specificity needed. The system's DB records do NOT capture this kind of nuance. The Weekly Wrap (once running) is a better source — but even that depends on photos being correctly identified first.

## The AREA_LABELS_EN Crash

**File:** `components/montree/reports/WeeklyWrapTab.tsx`

Line 76 had a comment: `// AREA_LABELS_ZH and AREA_LABELS_EN imported from @/lib/montree/i18n/area-labels`

But the actual import on line 10 only brought in `getAreaLabel as getAreaLabelI18n`. `AREA_LABELS_EN` was never imported. The function `toCanonicalArea()` at lines 124/126 used `AREA_LABELS_EN[lower]` and `AREA_LABELS_EN[n]` — both throw `ReferenceError: AREA_LABELS_EN is not defined` at render time.

This crash prevented the Weekly Wrap from rendering at all. Fix: added `AREA_LABELS_EN` to the import on line 10.

**Likely introduced during** the Ukrainian/Russian session (Session 73) when area-labels.ts was extended — the import line in WeeklyWrapTab was probably edited to remove AREA_LABELS_EN during the multilingual refactor without noticing it was still used in `toCanonicalArea()`.
