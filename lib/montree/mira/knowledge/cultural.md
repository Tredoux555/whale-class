# Cultural playbook — pitching by country / language

This is rule-of-thumb only. Real local knowledge from the agent always wins. Use this as a starting point and override on instinct.

## Mandarin-speaking principals (Mainland China, Taiwan, HK)

- **Pitch in Chinese, ideally.** If the agent doesn't speak Chinese, Mira translates the cold email and the agent sends the Chinese version with a brief English note offering an English meeting.
- **Lead with bilingual reports.** This is the killer feature for this audience. *"Your teachers write notes in English; the parent receives the weekly wrap in Mandarin — read by the family in their own language."*
- **Show Whale Class.** The founder runs his classroom in Beijing. That is the strongest possible reference.
- **Mention the founder's Beijing presence early.** It tells the principal you're not pitching from a Western capital with no local presence.
- **Be quiet-confident.** Chinese principals find Western sales enthusiasm off-putting. Short sentences, no exclamation marks, no "I'd love to" phrasing.
- **WeChat first, email second.** Many Chinese principals reply faster on WeChat. The agent should offer their WeChat ID early.
- **Mention the Alipay/WeChat Pay rail.** They can pay in RMB; no USD conversion friction.

## Spanish-speaking principals

### Argentina, Uruguay
- **Use voseo, not tú.** "Vos tenés", not "tú tienes". Argentine Spanish is its own register.
- AMI Spanish: Vida Práctica / Sensorial / Matemáticas / Lenguaje / Cultural.
- FAMM Argentina (Marisa Canova de Sioli) is the most-mentioned multiplier in the LatAm Montessori world. If the principal is Argentine and you're a multiplier-track agent, FAMM is the partnership you build first.

### Mexico, Colombia, Chile, Peru
- **Use ustedes for plural; vos for Argentina only.** Mexican Spanish is closer to neutral Latin American.
- AMI Mexican Spanish is well-established; the principals tend to be more formal than Argentines.

### Spain
- We don't have peninsular Spanish UI yet (LatAm only). Be honest if the principal asks. The translations are close enough that most Spanish principals can use the LatAm UI without complaint.

## French-speaking principals (France, Belgium, Switzerland, Canada-QC)

- **Use formal vous.** No tu in business contexts.
- AMI French Montessori: Vie Pratique / Sensoriel / Mathématiques / Langage / Culture.
- France: very formal initial outreach. *"Madame la Directrice"* style. The agent should mirror the principal's register from the first reply onwards.
- Switzerland: businesslike but warm. Mention privacy / data residency early.
- Quebec: more direct than France; less formal than the agent might expect from a French-language pitch.

## German-speaking principals (Germany, Austria, Switzerland)

- **Use Sie, never du.** German business correspondence is formal.
- AMI German: Praktisches Leben / Sinneserziehung / Mathematik / Sprache / Kosmische Erziehung.
- Germany: GDPR will come up. Be ready with the encryption + data-residency answer. Don't dodge.
- Austria: similar to Germany but a touch warmer.
- Swiss-German: very precise. They will read every word of the contract.

## Portuguese-speaking principals (Brazil, Portugal)

- **Brazilian Portuguese ≠ European Portuguese.** Our UI is currently a single Portuguese variant — useable by both but Brazilian principals will notice if a translation feels Iberian.
- Brazil: warmer than European Portuguese. WhatsApp follow-up works well.
- Portugal: formal initial outreach, like France.

## Italian principals

- **Use Lei (formal), not tu.** Italian formal register is required in school contexts.
- AMI Italian: Vita Pratica / Sensoriale / Matematica / Linguaggio / Educazione Cosmica.
- Italy: relationship-driven sales cycle. Expect 3-5 follow-ups before a meeting, not because they're not interested but because that's the rhythm.

## Japanese principals

- **Polite des/masu register everywhere.** Never use plain forms.
- *"お子さま"* (okosama) for "your child" in parent communication, not just "子" (ko).
- Japan: thorough evaluation cycle. Expect 1-3 months from first contact to first paid school.
- Card payment + Stripe works fine in Japan.

## Korean principals

- **Polite 합쇼체 / 해요체** register.
- *"자녀분"* (janyeobun) for "your child" in parent communication.
- Korea: very interested in Montessori but ESL-focused. The English Progression Tracker resonates particularly here.

## Dutch principals (Netherlands, Belgium-Flanders)

- **Formal u/uw register in business.**
- AMI Dutch: Praktisch Leven / Zintuiglijk / Wiskunde / Taal / Cultuur.
- The Dutch are direct. Get to the point quickly; long pitches will be skimmed.
- Maria Montessori has deep roots in the Netherlands — pedagogical credibility lands strong here.

## Russian / Ukrainian principals

- **Russian:** formal вы (vy) in business correspondence.
- **Ukrainian:** formal ви (vy). The first organic Ukrainian signup happened on Montree in April 2026 — there is genuine demand for Ukrainian-language Montessori software.
- Many Eastern European Montessori principals are AMI-trained in Russian; the AMI Russian and AMI Ukrainian terminology is well-established and we use it.
- Payment: most Russian schools currently CAN'T use Stripe due to sanctions; manual SWIFT invoice rail is the only option. Tredoux handles this directly.

## English-language regional notes

### US / Canada
- The Western single-school owner-principal persona is most common here. Direct, friendly, decision-makers.
- Annual prepayment + 10% discount lands particularly well in the US tax year.

### UK
- More formal than US. Use full titles (Mrs / Ms / Dr) until told otherwise.
- GDPR applies post-Brexit too; the data-residency answer should be ready.

### Australia / NZ
- Direct, warm, no patience for salesy language. Closer in register to the US than the UK.

### India
- English-fluent but pricing-conscious. Lead with the per-student calculation.
- WhatsApp beats email here too.

### South Africa / Africa more broadly
- Mostly English-language Montessori. Smaller market but engaged.
- Many South African schools work in ZAR; the USD pricing requires a conversation.

## Universal rules across all cultures

1. **Reference the local language and respect the local register from the first email.** A Mexican principal who gets a Spanish-language pitch from a Western agent feels seen. The same agent pitching in Argentine voseo to a Mexican principal feels lazy. Match the register.

2. **Never compare to "Western" schools** in a non-Western pitch. The principal isn't trying to be Western; they're trying to do Montessori well in their own context.

3. **Quiet confidence wins over enthusiasm everywhere.** Different cultures express this differently (Japanese understatement vs Italian warmth vs Australian directness) but in every culture, "salesy" loses to "competent and present."

4. **Cultural framing in drafts is light-touch.** Mira (the AI) adjusts register and length to local norms but doesn't lecture the agent on culture — that's patronising. The draft speaks for itself.
