# Follow-up templates — by meeting outcome

The follow-up note is the second-most-important communication of the sales cycle (the first is the demo itself). It is the agent's chance to be specific and accountable — to name what the principal said and what comes next.

The rules:
1. **Within 24 hours of the meeting.** Later than that and it feels like an afterthought.
2. **Name something specific the principal said.** "Thank you for sharing the story about Sofia" beats "thanks for the meeting" every time.
3. **One concrete next step.** Even if the next step is "no follow-up needed", say so.
4. **No bullet points unless the principal asked for them.** Real prose.
5. **Sign with the agent's first name, not a corporate sign-off.** Personal.

## Outcome: trial signup agreed

> Subject: Thanks — and your trial link
>
> Hi [name],
>
> Thank you for making time today. The thing that stayed with me from our conversation was [the specific thing she said — e.g. "your concern about Mrs Chen burning out on the Friday paperwork"]. That is exactly the moment Montree was built for.
>
> Here is your trial link: [link]. It is good for 30 days, one classroom, no card needed. I would suggest starting with [name of teacher she mentioned] — she'll be the fastest read on whether this fits.
>
> I will check in with you on [date — typically 7 days after trial start] to see how the first week landed. If you need anything before then — a question, a teacher who needs help getting in — message me.
>
> [Agent first name]

## Outcome: interested, wants to think about it

> Subject: Thinking about it
>
> Hi [name],
>
> Thank you for the time today. I appreciated [the specific thing — e.g. "the question you raised about how this works for a teacher who has never used AI before"]. I would not be surprised if other principals are asking the same question.
>
> The free month is there whenever you are ready — [link]. No deadline on it from our side. If you want to talk through any of the follow-up questions you mentioned ([list 1-3 specific ones she raised]), I am happy to set up a 15-minute call.
>
> [Agent first name]

## Outcome: not interested

> Subject: Thank you for your time
>
> Hi [name],
>
> Thank you for taking the meeting. I understand Montree isn't the right fit for [school name] right now, and I appreciate you being straight with me. If anything changes — the teacher workload conversation, the parent comms thing, anything — you have my email. No pressure to revisit.
>
> Wishing you and the children a great rest of the term.
>
> [Agent first name]

The "no" follow-up is the most under-rated. A graceful no-follow-up turns a "no" into a "maybe later" in about 12% of cases (based on Tredoux's own outreach data). Don't skip it.

## Outcome: she asked for a written follow-up on something specific

This is a STRONG signal — the principal is doing real evaluation work. Take the question seriously.

> Subject: The [X] question you asked
>
> Hi [name],
>
> You asked me [the specific question — verbatim, not paraphrased]. Here is what I can tell you.
>
> [The answer. Be specific. Include the names of features, links to relevant pages, or honest "we don't have that yet" lines. Don't hedge.]
>
> If this raises new questions, the easiest path is to set up a follow-up call — I can hold a 30-minute slot any of [3 specific times in her timezone]. Let me know.
>
> [Agent first name]

## Outcome: she wants references / case studies

We have a small number of paying schools right now. Be honest about that. Offer the founder as the reference call.

> Subject: References
>
> Hi [name],
>
> On the reference question — we are in early commercial release, so the number of paying schools is small. The strongest reference call I can offer is with Tredoux, our founder, who runs his own classroom on Montree in Beijing. He can spend twenty minutes walking you through what changed for him on Friday afternoons. He is also a current Montessori teacher and a builder of the product — you'd get both lenses.
>
> Beyond that, the free month is the strongest possible reference: your own teachers, in your own classroom, for 30 days, no card. If at the end of that you don't have a clear picture, no commitment.
>
> Would either feel useful?
>
> [Agent first name]

## Outcome: she wants a multi-campus / association deal

This is the multiplier-partner outcome. Loop Tredoux in.

> Subject: Pilot for [organisation name]
>
> Hi [name],
>
> I want to make sure we get the right plan together for [organisation]. Let me bring in Tredoux (our founder) so the three of us can think through what would work for [number] campuses / [number] affiliated schools. He is best placed to talk through pilot design, pricing structure, and how a revenue-share partnership would work on your side.
>
> Are you available for a 30-minute call this week? I can hold [2-3 specific times in her timezone].
>
> [Agent first name]

## What to NEVER send as a follow-up

- An auto-generated "thank you for the meeting" email with no specifics.
- A pricing email when the principal didn't ask about pricing.
- A second pitch deck. If the demo didn't land, sending a deck won't fix it.
- A "circling back" email after 4 weeks of silence. By then she has moved on. Either send a fresh value (a feature she didn't see, a relevant case study, a piece of Montessori writing) or let it lie.

## Cadence rules

- Day 0: initial outreach (cold pitch)
- Day 3-4: first nudge if no reply
- Day 7-10: second nudge if no reply
- Day 14: a final "is this still on your radar?" then stop
- Post-meeting: 24h follow-up (always)
- Post-trial-start: day 7 check-in (always)
- Post-trial-end: day 30 conversion conversation (always)

Past 30 days of silence from a principal who didn't open the trial: archive, don't keep nudging. They'll find Montree again when they're ready, or won't.
