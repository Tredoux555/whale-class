# Proof points

## The real story — Whale Class

The founder runs Whale Class himself, in Beijing. It is the platform's first and most-used school. Every feature in Montree has been built against a real classroom of 20 children. Every report that goes out has been read by a real parent. Every Tracy answer has been used to handle a real conversation.

This is not "we have a product idea." This is "we have a working product and a working school, and the founder is in the classroom every day."

## When the principal asks "how many schools are using this?"

Be honest. The platform is in early commercial release. Multiple schools are running it in production today (the agent doesn't need to know the exact number — Mira's `get_platform_signal` tool returns it live). Don't inflate. Don't compare yourself to a competitor's customer count.

The honest version: **"We are working with a small number of schools right now and onboarding new ones as we go. The founder runs his own classroom on the platform — we use it before we ship it. We want each new school to feel personally cared for, which is why we have a partner-agent program rather than a sales floor."**

That's a stronger answer than a number, because it tells the principal she's not lost in a customer-success ticket queue.

## What we DON'T have yet (be honest)

The agent should know what NOT to promise:

- We don't have an iPhone or Android native app yet. The web app is PWA — installs to home screen, works offline-first, but it's not on the App Store.
- We don't have native integrations with school payment systems (e.g. Brightwheel-style auto-tuition). Tuition is the school's billing problem; we charge them, they charge their parents.
- We don't have an LMS or scheduling system. Montree is Montessori-specific.
- We don't have content moderation for parent-uploaded media (parents can't upload media — only teachers do).
- We don't have a built-in payroll module.

If the principal needs these, gracefully say "Montree is Montessori-specific — for [tuition / payroll / LMS] you'll want a separate tool. Most schools we work with run their tuition system separately."

## The "this is built BY a Montessori teacher" angle

The founder is an AMS-certified Montessori Young Learner Specialist (AMI-aligned curriculum). The product is built BY someone who has stood in front of a circle, said "would you like to come and work with me on this?", and watched a four-year-old normalise.

This matters more than any number. The pitch is: *Montree is not Silicon Valley software pretending to know Montessori. It's a Montessori teacher who happens to build software.*
