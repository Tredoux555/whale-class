# Product overview — what Montree actually is

This file is the ground-up product picture for an agent who may know nothing about Montree yet. Once you know this cold, you can talk to any head of school as a product expert. Everything else in the knowledge base (pricing, objections, demos) sits on top of this.

## In one breath

Montree is the AI-powered classroom system built specifically for Montessori schools. A teacher takes a photo of a child working. Montree does the rest — it recognises the work, records the observation, tracks the child's progress, and suggests what comes next. It turns the documentation burden that drowns Montessori teachers into something that happens by itself, and it gives parents and principals a living window into the classroom.

The line that lands: **"A teacher takes a photo. Montree does the rest."**

## The problem it solves

Montessori teaching depends on observation and record-keeping — it's the heart of the method. But in practice teachers spend evenings and weekends writing up what each child did, by hand or in spreadsheets, and it never keeps up. Records go stale, progress is guessed at, parents get a generic termly report, and the principal has no real-time view of the school. The observation that's supposed to serve the child becomes paperwork that competes with the child.

Montree removes the paperwork without removing the observation. The teacher stays present with the children; the documentation assembles itself from the photos she's already taking.

## The magic moment

This is what you want a prospect to *see*, not just hear: a teacher points a phone at a child building the Pink Tower, taps once, and Montree instantly names the work, logs it against that child's record, updates their progress in the Sensorial area, and notes what to present next. No typing. No end-of-day catch-up. By Friday, a warm, specific, personalised report for every parent has written itself from the week's photos.

If you can get a school to try this with one real child for one week, the product sells itself.

## The four people Montree serves

Montree is one system with four front doors. Knowing each one lets you pitch to whoever is across the table.

### The teacher (the daily user)
- Opens the app, takes photos through the day as children work.
- The AI identifies the Montessori work in the photo (this is "Smart Capture"), writes an observation, and files it against the right child — including group photos that belong to several children.
- A per-child shelf shows what each child has been presented, is practising, and has mastered, with the next works suggested.
- "Guru" is a built-in Montessori advisor — think Maria Montessori in your pocket — that the teacher can ask anything about a specific child or a specific work.
- The teacher can confirm or correct any AI guess in seconds; the system learns the classroom's own materials over time.

### The parents (the trust surface)
- Get a private portal showing their own child's week — photos, what they worked on, milestones.
- Receive the "Weekly Wrap": a genuine, personalised narrative of what their child learned and why it matters — not a template. This is the thing parents fall in love with.
- Can message the school and book parent meetings through the app.

### The principal / head of school (the buyer)
- Gets a whole-school cockpit: every classroom, every child, every teacher, at a glance.
- Has their own chief-of-staff AI, **Astra**, who knows the entire school and can answer "how is this child doing?", draft a parent message in the principal's voice, or prepare a full dossier before a parent meeting.
- Sees which classrooms are active, which children need attention, and which teachers are engaged — real operational visibility they've never had.

### The child (the point of all of it)
- Everything above exists so the adults spend less time on records and more time observing and guiding the child. The pedagogy stays human; the bookkeeping becomes invisible.

## The product surfaces, plainly

You don't need to demo all of these, but you should be able to name them confidently:

- **Smart Capture / photo pipeline** — point, shoot, the AI identifies the work and logs the observation. The headline feature.
- **The child's shelf / progress** — presented → practising → mastered across the five Montessori areas (Practical Life, Sensorial, Mathematics, Language, Cultural), with next-work suggestions.
- **Weekly Wrap** — auto-written teacher reports and warm parent narratives, every week, from the photos.
- **Guru** — the teacher- and parent-facing Montessori advisor for any child or any work.
- **Astra** — the principal's whole-school chief-of-staff AI (distinct from Guru).
- **Parent portal** — the private per-child window + messaging + meeting booking.
- **Curriculum** — the full Montessori album: 329 works across the five areas, with making-guides and presentation steps, already built in.
- **Library tools** — printable classroom materials: three-part cards, picture bingo, sentence/sorting work, a decodable reading framework (Pink/Blue/Green phases), a shared photo bank.
- **12 languages** — the entire experience, including AI-written reports, works natively in 12 languages. This is a real differentiator in international and bilingual schools.

## Why Montessori schools specifically

Montree is not a generic daycare or childcare app with a Montessori skin. It's built around the actual Montessori curriculum and the observation-based way Montessori teachers work. The progress model is the real five-area album; the language is Montessori language; the AI was trained to recognise Montessori materials. To a Montessori head of school, this is obvious within a minute — and it's the difference between "another piece of software" and "someone finally built this for us."

## What it runs on (only if asked)

It's a web app that works on any phone, tablet, or computer — nothing to install, though it can be added to the home screen like an app. Schools sign up and are running the same day. You do not need to be technical to use any part of this, and neither does the teacher.

## The honest boundaries (so you never oversell)

- Montree assists the teacher's observation; it doesn't replace the teacher's judgment. The teacher confirms or corrects what the AI sees.
- The AI's photo recognition is strong and gets better as a classroom uses it, but it's not infallible on day one — corrections take seconds and teach the system.
- It is a documentation, progress, and communication system — not an attendance/billing/payroll suite. If a school needs those, say what Montree does do and don't pretend.
