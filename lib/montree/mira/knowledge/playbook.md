# Agent playbook — exactly what to do, from zero

This is the operating manual for a brand-new agent who has never sold Montree. When the agent asks "where do I start?", "what do I actually do?", "I have a school interested — now what?", or "how do I get paid?", walk them through the relevant phase here, step by step, in plain language. Give them ONE next action at a time — never dump the whole manual at once.

## What the job actually is

An agent refers Montessori schools to Montree and earns a recurring revenue share on every school that signs up through them and stays. You are not a cold-calling telemarketer — you are a knowledgeable guide who shows Montessori schools a tool built for them. The schools that convert are the ones who *see* the product, not just hear about it.

The economics (state them plainly, they're motivating and you should never be cagey):
- Montree costs the school **$7 per active student per month**, after a **30-day free trial with no credit card**.
- The agent earns a **recurring revenue share** (the standard is **20%**) on every school they bring, for as long as that school stays — roughly **$1.40 per student per month**. A single 60-child school is about **$1,000/year, recurring**. Multipliers (training centres, associations) can be worth many schools each.
- You get paid automatically once your Stripe Connect payout is set up (or by manual wire in countries Stripe doesn't support).

## The whole arc, at a glance

Find a school → reach out → get them to look (demo or trial) → help them sign up with your code → support the first week → follow up → earn. Below, each phase step by step.

## Phase 1 — Know the product cold (before you pitch anyone)

You can't sell what you don't understand. Before your first outreach:
1. Read the product overview until you can say, in your own words, what Montree is and the one magic moment ("a teacher takes a photo, Montree does the rest").
2. Be able to name the four people it serves — teacher, parents, principal, child — and the one thing each gets.
3. Know the price ($7/student/month, 30-day free trial, no card) and your share.
4. Ask Mira to quiz you, or to explain any surface in depth — she can pull the full detail on any topic.

→ If the agent is brand new, this is always the first step. Offer to walk them through the product in 5 minutes.

## Phase 2 — Find schools

Your prospects are Montessori schools, training centres, and associations.
1. Start with what you know — schools near you, schools you've worked at or visited, your own network. Warm beats cold every time.
2. Then widen: search "Montessori school [city/country]", Montessori association member directories, training-centre alumni networks.
3. **Multipliers are gold.** One Montessori training centre or national association can introduce you to dozens of schools. Prioritise them — one relationship can be worth a year of cold outreach.
4. Keep a simple list: school name, country, contact, language, and a one-line note on why they'd care.

→ A good first action for a new agent: "List five Montessori schools you already have some connection to."

## Phase 3 — Reach out

The goal of outreach is not to close — it's to earn a look.
1. Lead with the magic, not a feature list. The opener is the photo-to-everything moment.
2. Personalise: the school's name, the contact's name, their language, one specific thing about them.
3. Keep it short and human. Offer one thing: a quick look at what Montree does.
4. Match the cultural register — direct for Anglo schools, warmer and more relationship-first for many others. (Mira can draft this in any of Montree's 12 languages — just ask.)
5. Send, then track. Most schools need 2–3 touches before they engage; that's normal, not rejection.

→ Mira can draft the cold email for a specific school in the right language — the agent just reviews and sends from their own inbox.

## Phase 4 — Get them to look (demo or trial)

The single highest-converting move is getting a real classroom to try it for one week.
1. **The free trial is your best weapon.** One classroom, 30 days, no credit card. There is almost no reason for a curious school to say no.
2. If they want to see it first, run a demo: open the product, take a photo of any Montessori work, show the instant identification, the child's shelf, and a sample Weekly Wrap. Ten minutes is enough to land it.
3. Aim the demo at the person across the table — teacher (the daily relief), principal (the whole-school view + Astra), parents-angle (the Weekly Wrap they'll love).

→ Mira has sequenced demo flows for 10 / 30 / 90 minutes — ask her to walk through the right one for the meeting.

## Phase 5 — The signup mechanics (this is where your code matters)

This is the step new agents are unsure about, so be precise:
1. You issue the school a **referral code** from your agent dashboard (under **Codes**). Codes look like `YOURNAME-XXXX`.
2. The school signs up at **montree.xyz/montree/try?ref=YOUR-CODE** (the code pre-fills when they use your link). That link is how the school gets permanently linked to you — **if they sign up without your code, you don't get credit.** Always give them the link with your code in it.
3. The code becomes the **principal's login**, and the school starts its **30-day free trial** immediately — no credit card.
4. After the trial, the school is billed $7/active student/month, and your revenue share begins.

→ The concrete action when a school says yes: "Generate a code and send them your signup link" — Mira can confirm the agent's codes via `list_my_codes`.

## Phase 6 — Support the first week (this is what makes it stick)

A school that signs up but never logs in earns you nothing. The first week is everything.
1. Make sure the principal logs in and the first teacher takes their first photos. The "aha" is in the first real captures.
2. Point teachers at Guru (their in-app Montessori advisor) and the parent Weekly Wrap.
3. Check in once, warmly, a few days in — "how are the first photos going?" — and clear any small confusion fast.
4. Watch the school's signal on your dashboard: student count, last login, whether the principal has actually started.

→ Mira can read any of the agent's schools via `school_health` and flag the ones going quiet.

## Phase 7 — Follow up (the money is in the follow-up)

Most conversions happen on the second or third touch, not the first.
1. Stalled prospect (interested, went quiet): a short, warm nudge — not a guilt trip. Reference what they cared about.
2. Trial school that hasn't activated: offer a hand, not pressure — "want me to help your lead teacher get the first photos in?"
3. Polite "no for now": leave the door open, check back in a season.
4. Keep cadence light and human. Three good follow-ups beat ten desperate ones.

→ Mira drafts follow-ups by outcome — ask her for "a nudge for [school]".

## Phase 8 — Get paid

1. Set up your payout once, in the agent dashboard under **Payouts**: Stripe Connect (bank + tax details handled on Stripe's secure form) in supported countries, or a manual bank-wire arrangement where Stripe isn't available.
2. After that it's automatic — your share of every paying school you brought is calculated each month and paid out.
3. Your **Earnings** page shows what's pending and what's been paid, per school.

→ If an agent in an unsupported country is stuck, the move is "set your payout method to manual wire in the dashboard, or ask Tredoux" — Mira can message Tredoux on the agent's behalf.

## The mindset to coach

- Show, don't tell. The trial and the demo close; the pitch only earns the look.
- The follow-up is the job. One touch is a hello; conversions live at touch two and three.
- Multipliers compound. One association beats fifty cold emails.
- Be honest about what Montree is and isn't — trust is the whole asset.
- You are a Montessori guide who happens to carry a tool, not a software salesperson. That framing wins this audience.
