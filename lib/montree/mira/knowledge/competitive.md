# Competitive landscape — what to say and what NOT to say

## The rule

**Don't name competitors in the dossier or the cold pitch.** Mira's drafts produce Montree-positive language. If the principal asks "how is this different from X?", THAT is when Mira can produce competitive talking points. The agent quotes them aloud; Mira's written outputs don't.

## The main names you'll hear

### Montessori Compass
- The most-mentioned competitor. Wide-spread in established Montessori schools.
- Per-classroom pricing. Mid-tier capability.
- Strong: lesson planning, progress trees, parent emails.
- Weak: AI is bolt-on, not native. No photo-to-observation pipeline. No Tracy / Guru equivalent. English-only UI. No native Chinese support.
- **What to say if the principal mentions them:** "Many of the schools we work with came from Montessori Compass. Compass is great for tracking what was presented. Montree adds the layer that automates the observation itself, and writes the parent report at the end of the week."

### Transparent Classroom
- US-focused. Strong in established schools, especially in the northeast.
- Per-teacher pricing. Tiered.
- Strong: parent communication, attendance, billing integration.
- Weak: no AI. Heavy admin lift. English-only.
- **What to say:** "Transparent Classroom is excellent for parent comms and billing integration. Where Montree is different is that we put AI in the loop on the back-office side — the teacher snaps a photo, the system writes the observation."

### Brightwheel / Lillio / Tadpoles
- General-purpose childcare platforms. Not Montessori-specific.
- These are the "all-in-one" platforms that include tuition + attendance + meals + comms.
- **What to say:** "Brightwheel is built for general childcare. Montree is Montessori-specific — we go deeper on the pedagogical layer (work tracking, sensitive periods, the three-period lesson) and we don't try to be your tuition system."

### EdAdmin / SchoolCues / Edsby
- ERP-style school management. Not pedagogy-aware.
- **What to say:** "Those are your finance + admin systems. Montree sits alongside, focused on the classroom and the parent communication."

### "We use Google Classroom / Sheets / paper"
- Most common in smaller schools, especially outside US/UK.
- The conversation: pricing aside, this is the moment to demo the photo-to-observation flow. Watching a Sonnet-generated weekly wrap arrive in their language tends to close.

## The killer differentiators (use these when the principal is comparing)

1. **Photo to observation in one tap.** No competitor has this. The two-pass Haiku → Sonnet pipeline is genuinely novel.

2. **Tracy.** No competitor has a chief-of-staff AI. The dossier for the difficult parent meeting is the feature that makes the principal refuse to run a parent meeting without us, once she's used it once.

3. **12 languages, native quality.** Most competitors are English-only or have machine-translated UI. Our Mandarin / Spanish / etc. are real translations, and the AI generates parent reports in the family's language.

4. **One plan, one price.** No tier shenanigans, no per-feature upselling. Most competitors have at least 3 tiers; the principal pays for "the version that has X". We don't do that.

5. **Built by a Montessori teacher.** Use this whenever the conversation feels like the principal is talking to a software vendor. Montree is the founder's classroom; the founder is the agent's product manager.

## When the principal says "we'll think about it"

Don't push on price. Don't compare again. The graceful close:

> *"Of course. Take the free month — onboard one classroom, see how Friday afternoon feels with the Weekly Wrap doing the heavy lifting. If it works for you, we keep going. If it doesn't, no card, no commitment, no awkward exit conversation."*

Then follow up in 3-4 days with a short nudge offering to set up the trial (Mira drafts the email).
