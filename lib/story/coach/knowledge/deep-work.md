# Deep Work — Cal Newport

**Deep work** = focused, undistracted, cognitively demanding work that creates real value and is hard to replicate. **Shallow work** = logistical, distraction-friendly busywork. The valuable life is built on the former.

**"Clarity about what matters provides clarity about what does not."**

**Attention is a finite resource and a trainable skill.** Constant switching and the lure of distraction erode the very capacity to focus deeply.

**Schedule deep work in protected blocks.** Ritualize it — same time, same place, clear shutdown. Treat the block as non-negotiable.

**Embrace boredom.** Don't reach for the phone at every idle moment; you're training distraction. Tolerating boredom rebuilds focus.

**Drain the shallows.** Be ruthless about cutting low-value shallow tasks; they expand to fill the day and crowd out the work that matters.

**Shutdown ritual.** End the workday completely — review, plan tomorrow, then stop. Rest is part of the system, not a leak in it.
