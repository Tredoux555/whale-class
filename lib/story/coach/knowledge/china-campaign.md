## The mission and the honest success bar
Montree (his Montessori classroom SaaS) launches into the Chinese market during the summer holiday window, when private kindergarten principals are deciding how to compete for autumn enrollment. **Success in 30 days ≠ virality. Success = ~20 videos posted + 3–5 principal demos booked + an account that makes him instantly credible when a 园长 checks him out.** One converted pilot school validates the whole month. If he starts chasing views instead of demos, pull him back to this bar.

## Why the moment is real (use this when his conviction wobbles)
China closed 21,000+ kindergartens in 2024 (~2/hour); private ones hit hardest (−9% YoY); enrollment down ~25% in 4 years; projections ~15,000 closures/year to 2030. Private principals are actively increasing marketing spend and asking exactly one question: 凭什么家长选我们? Montree's answer: because parents can SEE their child's growth every week. His fear that he "overcomplicated the app" is handled by positioning discipline, not rebuilding: every video carries ONE message, and parent content shows ONLY the core loop (photo → understanding → parent report). Astra/school-management never appears in parent content.

## The two-track strategy (the spine — never let a video blur the tracks)
- **Track A — Parent pull, 小红书 primary (Douyin cross-post).** Product NEVER named in-video. The payload is a QUESTION planted in parents' mouths for kindergarten tours: 「孩子每天的成长,你们怎么让我看到?」 The ask lives in caption/bio only (his proven sell-without-selling position). Persona: 「北京幼儿园外教 Tredoux」 — a teacher showing his craft, not a brand.
- **Track B — Principal push, 视频号 primary.** Direct pitch is CORRECT here (B2B shopping context, unlike cold consumer scrolls). Crisis-frame + enrollment-edge frame. Every video ends one 私信 away from a demo. Also sent DIRECTLY to principal WeChat contacts — a 20-second video beats a cold text.

## The execution sequence (coach him through IN THIS ORDER; one step per session is fine)
1. **Accounts** — register 小红书 + 视频号 + Douyin as 「北京幼儿园外教 Tredoux」. Bio links route to WeChat.
2. **Ghost-school demo assets** — build the demo parent report from ghost-school data (Greenfield Montessori pattern). Hard rule: NO real children in any product shot.
3. **Batch-film day** — one Saturday, 8–10 videos from the script pack. Tripod, phone, empty classroom, CapCut(剪映) auto-captions. Film A1 (the 「玩」 video) and B1 (the kindergarten-closure video) FIRST — they're the flagships.
4. **Posting cadence** — ~1/day for 20 days across all three platforms. Parent windows 7–9am / 8–10pm; principals lunch 12–1pm. Reply to every comment in the first 2 hours; pin a comment carrying the CTA.
5. **Direct principal sends** — B-track videos into principal WeChat chats + group forwards. This is the realistic conversion path for a solo founder: video → 私信/WeChat → 15-min demo → 1-classroom free 30-day pilot (his trial system) → paid via his Alipay/WeChat invoice rails.
6. **Weekly 10-min review** — completion rate per video, WeChat adds (the ONLY conversion metric this month), and which hook produced the adds (ask new contacts: 哪条视频看到我的?).

## Hard guardrails (never let him cross these, even for a "better" video)
- **No children's faces without written guardian consent.** Hands, materials, backs, or ghost-school shots. This is law AND on-message.
- **No academic-advancement claims for preschoolers** (Double-Reduction adjacency). Development language only: 专注力、独立性、动手能力、内在秩序. Montessori's anti-cramming stance ALIGNS with official 去小学化 policy — that's a strength, say it.
- **No superlatives** — 最/第一/唯一 violate the Advertising Law.
- **Honesty firewall unchanged** — no fake scarcity, proof, or numbers.

## The decided positions that transfer from his TikTok data (treat as settled)
- **Volume beats a cold algorithm.** A plateaued video is NOT a failed video — the move is post the next one, same day if possible, not "change the formula." When he spirals on one video's numbers, this is the reframe.
- **First frame is its own deliverable** — face mid-motion or striking material close-up + big overlay text; never a logo/title card. Drop-off at 0:01 = fix the frame, not the script.
- **12–20 seconds, ~60–85 Chinese characters, subtitles always.** His imperfect spoken Mandarin as a 外教 is an asset (scroll-stopper), not a flaw — fallback is English voice + full Chinese subtitles + one spoken Chinese hook line.
- **Consistent sign-off:** 「关注孩子,而非记录。」

## The freelancer question (he'll raise it — hold this line)
NOT a retainer hire now. Staged: Stage 0 = two weeks self-produced from the pack (creates the baseline data any hire needs). Stage 1 = per-video native-speaker copy-polish + pronunciation check, ¥50–150/video (淘宝/闲鱼/a local student) — probably all he needs. Stage 2 (only after a format demonstrably converts) = part-time XHS/Douyin operator ~¥3–5k/月 for edit+post+comments. NEVER outsourced: his face, his classroom stories, principal WeChat conversations — that's the moat.
