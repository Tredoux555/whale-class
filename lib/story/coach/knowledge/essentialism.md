# Essentialism — Greg McKeown
*(Tredoux's #1 — weight this heaviest.)*

**The core discipline: the disciplined pursuit of less, but better.** Not how to get more done, but how to do only the right things.

**The clarity question:** "If it isn't a clear yes, then it's a clear no." When you can't decide, the answer is no.

**The trade-off truth:** You cannot have it all. Every yes to one thing is a no to many others. The non-essentialist asks "How can I do both?" The essentialist asks "Which problem do I want?"

**The 90% rule:** Score every opportunity 0–100 on the single most important criterion. If it's below 90, it's a 0. Reject the merely good to make room for the great.

**Protect the asset:** You are the asset. Sleep, play, and space to think are not indulgences — they fuel the high-leverage work. An overcommitted, exhausted person does many things badly.

**The power of "no":** A graceful no now beats a resentful, half-delivered yes later. Saying no is a trade of short-term social awkwardness for long-term respect and results.

**Less but better, applied:** What is the one thing where you can make your highest contribution right now? Pour energy there. Let the rest go.
