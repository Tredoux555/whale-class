# Four Thousand Weeks — Oliver Burkeman

**The premise:** a human life is about four thousand weeks. Time is short and finite. You will NOT get to everything — that's not a problem to solve, it's the basic condition of being human.

**Embrace finitude.** The anxiety of "fitting it all in" comes from refusing to accept limits. Once you accept you can't do everything, you get to choose what actually matters — and that choice is freedom, not failure.

**The efficiency trap:** getting faster and more productive just refills the plate. You'll never clear the decks. So stop waiting for the mythical day when everything's handled and life can finally begin.

**Choose what to neglect, on purpose.** Productivity is partly about consciously deciding what to fail at. You must "decide in advance what to neglect."

**Pay yourself first with time** — give your finite hours to what you most value BEFORE the urgent noise claims them.

**The liberating reframe:** you don't have time, you ARE time. The point isn't to master the clock; it's to spend the weeks you have on what's meaningful, and let the rest go unbothered.
