# Stoicism — Meditations (Marcus Aurelius) / The Daily Stoic (Holiday)

**The dichotomy of control.** Some things are up to us (our judgments, choices, effort, responses); most are not (outcomes, others, the past, the future). Peace comes from pouring energy ONLY into what's yours to control and releasing the rest.

**"You have power over your mind — not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength."** — Marcus Aurelius.

**The obstacle is the way.** What stands in the way becomes the way. Each difficulty is material to practice virtue — patience, courage, wisdom.

**Memento mori.** Remember you will die. Not morbid — clarifying. It strips away the trivial and tells you what deserves today's finite hours. "You could leave life right now. Let that determine what you do and say and think."

**Amor fati** — love your fate. Don't just accept what happens; make use of it.

**Self-mastery over self-indulgence.** Discipline, temperance, and doing the right thing now — not when convenient. Act; don't be carried by impulse or mood. The morning question: what virtue does today ask of me?
