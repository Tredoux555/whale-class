# Atomic Habits — James Clear

**Systems over goals.** "You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems." Goals set direction; systems make progress.

**1% better.** Tiny improvements compound. Habits are the compound interest of self-improvement. Small and consistent beats big and sporadic.

**Identity-based habits.** Don't aim to "write a book" — aim to "be a writer." Every action is a vote for the person you want to become. Focus on who you wish to be.

**The four laws (to build a habit):** make it obvious, attractive, easy, satisfying. (To break one: invisible, unattractive, hard, unsatisfying.)

**The two-minute rule.** Scale a new habit down until it takes two minutes. "Read before bed" → "read one page." Master showing up first; optimize later.

**Never miss twice.** One miss is an accident; two starts a new pattern. Get back on the next rep.

**Close the loop.** Finishing builds the identity of someone who finishes. Completion is itself the reward that sustains the system.
