_A brain component for **growing @lyf.coach on TikTok** — the distribution and growth
layer: the algorithm, posting strategy, paid amplification, and turning views into trials.
Reference knowledge for the assistant when advising Tredoux on growth. NOT user-facing
coaching content. For the SCRIPT itself (hooks, story, CTA wording) use topic
`video_scripts`; this module is everything around the script — how to get it seen and
how to convert the attention._

_Confidence labels appear throughout: **[confirmed]** = TikTok's own docs · **[consensus]**
= agreed across credible analysts (Buffer, Hootsuite, Sprout, Metricool) · **[lore]** =
widely repeated, unverified — a mental model, never a target. TikTok publishes signal
*categories* but never exact weights, so most numbers are directional. Re-verify any

## 0. HOW TO USE THIS — the reasoning hierarchy

When advising Tredoux on growth, reason in this order:

1. **Retention first, everything else second.** The two master levers are **completion
   rate + rewatch** and **early velocity** (first 30–60 min). If a video underperformed,
   diagnose those before touching strategy. Most "the algorithm hates me" problems are a
   weak first 3 seconds.
2. **Honesty is the constraint, not a tradeoff** (same firewall as `video_scripts` — never
   fake scarcity, proof, or authority; never a clinical claim). Tredoux's authenticity is
   the product AND the highest-converting mode.
3. **Small budget changes the whole paid playbook.** He's a solo, bootstrapped founder.
   Never advise cold conversion campaigns he can't feed (§4C). Lead with Spark-boosting
   proven organic.
4. **Label confidence; promise small.** No single tactic "goes viral." Stack honest edges,
   give ONE clear next action (his working style: small confirmed sequential wins).

## DECIDED POSITIONS (Lyf Coach) — hard-won from live results; these OVERRIDE the general tactics below

_Settled from real @lyf.coach data. Treat as decided unless new evidence overturns them._

**1. SELL WITHOUT SELLING — story-only videos. The product NEVER appears in the video.**
The instant a viewer smells a pitch, they swipe (observed: a clean drop the moment "Lyf Coach"
was spoken). So the video's ONLY job is to make someone **feel seen** — no product name, no
"app," no "link in bio," no URL spoken or on screen. End on the emotional beat and stop. The
**pitch lives in the CAPTION** ("How? Link in bio.") and the **bio link is the quiet door they
choose to open** — intrigue, not a sell. This overrides "say + show the URL" in §4D and the
`video_scripts` CTA section: for cold/story videos the URL is caption-and-bio only. The model:
> "I lost $4,000 on a flight. Wrong airport. My fault. Holiday — gone.
> You know what didn't happen? Nobody cried." — full stop. That's the whole video.
(The "Grounded" script was ~80% there; the fix was cutting the last 20% — the pitch — entirely.)

**2. VOLUME BEATS A COLD ALGORITHM — outpace it.**
A new account with no following gets a small test batch; if a video doesn't explode TikTok stops
pushing it — but it doesn't die. Real case: a video kept creeping past 100 views on **organic**
traffic (real people *choosing* to watch) after the algorithm went quiet. Read it right: the
**content is working; the audience isn't there yet.** That's a **distribution** problem, not a
**quality** problem — and distribution is solved by VOLUME. Every video is another lottery ticket;
with one in the draw you can't win. So when a decent video plateaus, the move is NOT "it failed,
change the formula" — it's **post the next one, same day if possible.** You beat a cold algorithm
by outpacing it.

**3. THE COVER / FIRST FRAME is a separate lever from the hook line.**
Drop-off at **0:01** (before the script even starts) = the cover/first frame killed it, not the
words. Treat the opening *frame* as its own deliverable: visually arresting, readable, a face or
motion — never a logo, title card, or slow build.

## 1. THE MASTER MENTAL MODEL (read before any tactic)

TikTok in 2026 runs a **filtration model**, not a distribution model **[consensus]**. It does
NOT push content broadly to find hits. It tests every video in a tiny cohort first and only
scales what proves early retention. "Good content" alone does not earn reach — content that
*holds attention in its first small test* earns reach.

**The two master levers — everything else is downstream:**

1. **Completion rate + rewatch.** The heaviest signal. The platform optimises total session
   watch-time, so a video people finish (or loop) gets pushed; one they swipe off dies.
   **~50% average completion = the floor where distribution accelerates; ~70%+ = "viral-grade"
   [lore, directionally solid].** One person watching 3× beats three single views — engineer
   the rewatch (loop the end into the start; hide a second-pass payoff).
2. **Early velocity** — how *fast* engagement accumulates in roughly the **first 30–60 minutes**
   **[consensus]**. The decisive window compressed from the old 24–48h slow burn. Rate of
   accumulation is weighted *above* total views and *above* follower count.

**Follower count is explicitly NOT a ranking factor [confirmed].** A 200-follower account and a
2M account face the same initial test. A tiny account can pop; buying followers does nothing.

**The cold-start ladder (mental model, not a target):** new video → **~200–500-person test pool**
[TikTok docs reference a ~500 "Basic Traffic Pool"] → strong retention/velocity → 1K–10K →
10K–100K (saves + shares dominate) → 100K+ (must hold across *different* segments, not just the
niche). Each step is a **fresh audition judged on the NEW audience**, not cumulative stats.

**The first real gate is the 5-second "Qualified View" [Sprout, credible]:** a view only counts
past ~5 seconds. Failing to clear enough 5-second views is the real cause of "200/300 view jail" —
which is *why* the 3-second hook is non-negotiable.

**The 2026 engagement shift — depth over volume [consensus]: Shares > Saves > meaningful
Comments > Likes.** Likes are deprioritised as a quality signal (a like costs half a second; a
save signals intent to return; a share is the highest-effort endorsement). **Build every video
and CTA around saves and shares, not likes.**

## 2. THE FIRST-3-SECONDS LAW + reach-killers

- **Hook in 3 seconds, in three channels at once:** say it (spoken, with the keyword), show it
  (a strong first *frame*, not a warm-up), put it on screen (≤6-word overlay). 30%+ watch on
  mute, so the text overlay carries the hook sound-off. (Hook craft itself → `video_scripts` §C.)
- **Cut everything before the hook.** "Hey guys, welcome back…" is the single biggest retention
  killer. The hook IS the opening.
- **The cover / first FRAME is its own lever (Decided Position 3).** A drop at 0:01 = the opening
  frame failed before the script started — fix the frame, not the line.
- **Never upload watermarked / cross-platform content.** Content carrying a CapCut, Instagram,
  Reels, or Shorts watermark is **officially ineligible for the For You feed [confirmed]** — the
  #1 confirmed reach-killer. Always export a clean master.
- **Other confirmed/strong reach-killers:** engagement bait ("like for part 2," "comment X for
  the link") [confirmed]; reposting a deleted video; bought followers/bot engagement; 5 posts in
  20 minutes; aggressively driving off-platform early. *Genuine* prompts ("what's your 3am
  thought?") are fine and encouraged — the line is manufactured/incentivised vs. organic.
- **Captions, on-screen text, and spoken words are SEO [confirmed].** TikTok transcribes audio
  and reads on-screen text to categorise and to rank in Search (TikTok is now a real search
  engine). Put the keyword in all three places.

## 3. OPERATING PRINCIPLES (what I advise by)

1. **Founder-led, face-led.** In a niche drowning in faceless quote/AI-slideshow accounts, a named
   human who is a *teacher* is the moat. Tredoux is the face.
2. **Show the real product.** Screen-record the actual chat (with a small face-cam reaction). For
   an AI chat app, letting people *see the quality of the responses* is the biggest trust-and-
   conversion lever there is.
3. **Optimise for shares + saves.** "Send this to someone who…", "save this for your 3am brain."
4. **Hashtags = SEO now, not tag-stuffing.** 3–5 *niche* tags beat 20 broad ones [consensus].
   `#fyp`/`#viral` do almost nothing. Use search-intent tags (`#overthinking`, `#shadowwork`).
5. **Consistency beats volume; volume only buys lottery tickets.** Going from ~1×/week to **2–5×/
   week** is the meaningful jump [Buffer, 11.4M posts]. More posting barely moves the *median* —
   it raises the *ceiling* (more chances at a breakout). Don't out-post the quality.
6. **Engineer the rewatch and the re-hook** (loop the ending; plant an open loop early, pay it off
   late; add a mid-video re-hook at the 40–60% mark).
7. **Diagnose before despairing** (§5). A flop is usually one video scored poorly in its seed pool —
   not a "shadowban."
8. **Never make clinical claims** (§6 — hard rule).

## 4. PLAYBOOKS

### 4A. Cold-start / newish account
- **Signal the niche before/while posting.** 15–30 min/day as a *consumer*: watch-to-end, like,
  save, comment on self-improvement/mindset content. This trains TikTok's topic model so it knows
  whom to test your videos on [consensus; "warming as a shadowban cure" is vendor lore — niche-
  signalling is the legit kernel].
- **Space posts 3–4 hours apart** so each clears its own test phase (no self-cannibalisation).
- **Bio-link rule [confirmed]:** personal accounts need **1,000 followers** for a clickable bio
  link; a **Business account** unlocks the link immediately but loses the full trending-sound
  library (can dampen reach). Trade-off, not a free win — for voiceover/talking-head coaching
  content (which leans on original audio) Business is often fine.

### 4B. Content craft (the engine)
- **Hook families that actually outperform [consensus]:** Identity Call ("If you're [ultra-specific
  identity]…"), Contrarian Strike ("Stop [common behaviour]"), Open Loop ("nobody's talking
  about…"), Confession ("I wasted [time] before I learned…"). The mechanic under all: **specificity**
  — "your 3am brain" beats "anxiety."
- **Retention editing:** change something on screen every 3–5s; jump-cut out breaths; fast hook →
  slow on the insight → fast close; seamless loop the ending into the open.
- **Length [consensus]:** ~11–18s for max completion; 21–38s for a full story; 60s+ for depth *only
  if completion holds*. Longer videos now get more distribution **only when completion stays high**.
- **Sounds:** ride a *rising-but-not-peaked* trending sound early for a spike; use **original audio**
  for durable, ownable reach. (Business accounts: confirm the sound is licensed for commercial use.)
- **Series:** 3-part max (retention tanks after ~3). End each on a cliffhanger. **Reply to comments
  with a video** — turns one question into a new post shown to everyone who engaged the original.
- **Cadence:** batch-film, drip-publish 2–5×/week. **Pull his own best times** (TikTok Studio →
  Analytics → Followers → most-active) and post slightly *before* the peak — the published "best
  time" charts flatly disagree, so they're only a starting hypothesis.

### 4C. Paid — amplify, don't gamble (small-budget reality)
**The hard truth:** a clean install/subscribe-optimised campaign needs ~**50 conversion events per
ad group per week** to stabilise — often **$100–$200/day per ad group** [consensus]. On a tiny
budget, do NOT run cold conversion campaigns. Win this way instead:
1. **Get conversion tracking right first** — TikTok **Pixel + Events API** (server-side), because
   browser pixels miss up to ~35% of events in 2026. Match event names exactly or you double-count.
2. **Spark Ads on proven organic winners.** Spark Ads promote a real organic post, so the likes/
   comments/shares stay on the post and keep the social proof; ~30% higher engagement, ~57% lower
   CPA vs standard in-feed [vendor-reported]. **Only boost posts that already beat your average on
   completion + shares** — let free organic be the A/B test. Authorise: post → ⋯ → Ad settings →
   toggle Ad authorisation → Generate code (pick a **365-day** window so it can't expire mid-flight).
3. **Harvest warm audiences for ~free** — 25/50/75/100% video-viewers, profile visitors, engagers.
   Organic viewers are usable immediately, no spend history required.
4. **Retarget warm pools with the free-trial offer** — where small budgets get their best cost per
   acquisition.
5. **Only then** test **App Promotion optimised for trial-start** (not raw install — avoid junk
   installs) or a **Smart+ App** campaign, once he can fund ~50 events/week and has 5–10 creatives.
6. **Creative is the new targeting:** 3+ hooks on the same body, run together; judge on **hook rate**
   (% past 3s) first. Ads fatigue in ~72h — refresh constantly. UGC beats polished (~20–40% lower
   cost-per-install).

**Spend order on $10–50/day:** tracking live → Spark-boost winners (Reach/Traffic) → harvest viewers
→ retarget with trial offer → (later) App Promotion/Smart+ → lookalikes off subscribers. **Concentrate
budget into 1–3 well-funded ad groups; never spread thin.**

### 4D. Conversion — viewer → trial → paid
- **DECIDED — story-only: the product NEVER appears in the video** (Decided Position 1). The pitch
  lives in the CAPTION ("How? Link in bio.") and the bio link; the video ends on the emotional beat.
  (General TikTok advice says "say + show the URL" — *overridden here*: for this trust-first product,
  naming the product mid-video makes them swipe. Caption + bio + landing carry the CTA, not the video.)
- **US comment-to-DM auto-linking is NOT available in 2026.** Use "DM me [WORD]" + a ManyChat flow
  (user DMs first; no clickable links inside TikTok DMs — move them to the site fast). Still ask for
  comments for the algorithm; reply manually.
- **Pre-sell by showing the product:** one micro-transformation per video — pain (3am spiral) →
  action (type it into Lyf Coach) → relief (the coach's reframe). They feel the value before they
  download.
- **Optimise for "start trial," not purchase** (iOS purchase signal is late/noisy). One CTA per
  landing page. Lower friction usually wins for a trust-sensitive wellness product.

## 5. DIAGNOSTICS (when a video flops / "am I shadowbanned?")
- **First, completion.** Low average watch = a hook/retention problem, not a ban. Re-cut the first
  3 seconds and the pacing.
- **TikTok Studio → Analytics:** if traffic is almost all "Following/Personal Profile" and ~0%
  "For You," it isn't being recommended.
- **Settings → Account → Account status → Posts:** any post tagged **"Not eligible for the For You
  feed"** names the exact rule + offers an appeal. This is the *only* reliable shadowban check —
  ignore third-party "shadowban checker" tools.
- **Plateau after a test batch is NOT failure (Decided Position 2).** If a video keeps gaining
  *organic* views after the algorithm stops pushing, the content works and the gap is distribution —
  answer with VOLUME (post the next one, same day), not a formula change.
- **Recover from suppression:** delete/private the flagged post (or appeal), pause posting 24–72h,
  return with clean original content + licensed in-app audio, stop any automation/bought engagement.
- **When to boost (Spark Ad):** only a post already beating your average on completion AND shares,
  ideally with purchase-intent comments ("is this an app?"). Don't pay to amplify unproven creative.

## 6. MENTAL-HEALTH COMPLIANCE FIREWALL (hard rules — never cross)
TikTok's Mental & Behavioural Health guidelines (updated Aug 2025) make this a real reach-AND-policy
risk surface. Same status as the `video_scripts` honesty firewall:
- **Never claim the app prevents, treats, cures, or diagnoses** anything. The single biggest landmine.
- **Say "coach," "reflection," "mindset," "support," "in your corner." Never "therapy,"
  "therapist," "treatment," "diagnosis."** The "things my coach told me" framing works *because*
  it's coaching, not clinical.
- **Frame outcomes as behavioural/experiential** — "clearer mornings," "fewer 3am spirals," "a place
  to think out loud" — never clinical reduction claims.
- **Never use crisis content as a marketing surface** (self-harm/suicide content is banned and can
  trigger escalation). Don't build hooks around crisis.
- **Pin a soft disclaimer** (bio + pinned comment): *"Lyf Coach is a personal coaching companion,
  not therapy or a substitute for professional mental-health care. If you're in crisis, contact a
  local helpline."* It protects you AND builds trust.
- **Handle the "isn't AI therapy dangerous?" objection head-on** — it's documented and the comments
  *will* come. Position as **complement, not replacement**: "not a replacement for a therapist —
  it's for the 11pm moments when no one else is awake." Owning the objection out-converts ignoring it.

## 7. WHAT WORKED FOR COMPARABLE APPS (proof, use as the roadmap)
- **Cal AI** (~$30M+/yr): the founder posted **281 videos / 2.1M views on his own account** before
  real revenue — **founder content was the cold-start engine.** Then sequenced influencer → paid →
  affiliate (never all at once). Scaled 150+ micro-creators on small **retainers** (~4 posts/mo each
  — frequency manufactures social proof). Influencer content earns attention; *ad* content (bold
  text, fast walkthrough, explicit CTA) converts — different jobs, don't just re-run influencer clips.
- **Finch** (~$30M ARR, no VC): engine was **paid UGC at volume** (scaled to 400–700 creatives/month,
  talking-head testimonials + download end-card), and it positioned as a friendly companion, **not a
  "mental health app"** — sidestepping both the wellness-claim rules and the "is this therapy?"
  skepticism. Lesson: the non-clinical "coach in your corner" frame spreads more freely.
- **Phase plan for Lyf Coach:** Phase 1 (now) = founder-led daily organic (free, proven cold-start) →
  Phase 2 = Spark-boost the best organic → Phase 3 = micro-creator UGC on small retainers → Phase 4 =
  affiliate/referral once trial→paid economics are known.

  retention and trial→paid, not cheapest install. At ~$15/mo, keep blended CAC well under subscriber
  LTV after app-store fees. **[vendor ranges]**
