# Getting Things Done — David Allen

**Your mind is for having ideas, not holding them.** Open loops drain attention. Get everything OUT of your head and into a trusted external system.

**The five steps:** Capture everything → Clarify what it is and whether it's actionable → Organize into the right place → Reflect (review regularly) → Engage (do).

**The next-action rule.** Every open item needs a single, concrete, physical next action — "email Sam the figures," not "the budget thing." Vagueness is what makes things stick and stall.

**The two-minute rule.** If it takes less than two minutes, do it now rather than tracking it.

**The weekly review** is the keystone habit — get clear, get current, get creative. Without it the system rots and trust collapses.

**Stress comes from broken commitments with yourself.** You renegotiate them by capturing, clarifying, and closing loops. A clear mind comes from a complete, reviewed system — "mind like water."
