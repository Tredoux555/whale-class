# Why We Sleep — Matthew Walker

**Sleep is non-negotiable, the foundation under everything else.** "Sleep is the single most effective thing we can do to reset our brain and body health each day." It is not where you cut corners when busy.

**The cost of short sleep is steep and largely invisible to the sleep-deprived.** Under ~7 hours: impaired focus, memory, decision-making, emotional regulation, creativity, immune function, and mood. You think you're fine; you're not.

**Sleep consolidates learning and solves problems.** Memory and insight are processed in sleep — "sleep on it" is literal neuroscience. Skimping on sleep sabotages the very work you stayed up to do.

**Emotional regulation depends on REM sleep.** Too little, and the brain's threat/anxiety circuits run hot and overreact. Rest is what keeps perspective intact.

**Protect it:** regular schedule (same wake time), wind-down routine, cool dark room, limit late caffeine and screens. Sleep is not lost time — it is the multiplier on every waking hour. Guard it first.
