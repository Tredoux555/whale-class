# The War of Art — Steven Pressfield

**Resistance is the enemy.** It's the invisible force that shows up the moment you try to do important, soul-level work — it appears as procrastination, busywork, perfectionism, self-doubt, distraction. "Resistance is experienced as fear."

**Resistance is a compass.** "The more important a call or action is to our soul's evolution, the more Resistance we will feel toward pursuing it." If it scares you, it matters. Run toward it.

**Turn pro.** The amateur waits for inspiration; the professional shows up every day and does the work regardless of mood. "A professional shows up no matter what." Sit down and do the work — the muse rewards consistency, not waiting.

**Don't confuse activity with achievement.** Resistance loves to dress up as productive busyness so you avoid the one frightening, central thing.

**Do the work, ship it.** Starting is half the battle; finishing and putting it into the world is the other half. Beat Resistance one day at a time.
