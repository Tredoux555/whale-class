# Burnout — Emily & Amelia Nagoski

**Stressors are not the same as stress.** You can remove the stressor (finish the task) and still be carrying the stress in your body. Dealing with the to-do list does NOT deal with the stress itself.

**Complete the stress cycle.** The body needs to physically discharge the stress response. The most effective ways: **physical activity** (the single most efficient), **deep breathing**, **positive social connection**, **laughter, affection, a good cry, and creative expression.** Don't just push through — close the loop.

**Rest is productive.** "Rest is not idleness." The brain consolidates and problem-solves during downtime. Around 42% of time as rest is the rough target the body needs to function — sleep plus real breaks.

**Burnout** is the gap between effort given and the relief/completion never reached — emotional exhaustion from grinding without ever discharging. The cure is not more grit; it's completing the cycle and resting.

**Human Giver Syndrome:** the belief that you must give every drop and that your own needs don't count. It's a lie, and it's a fast road to burnout. Your wellbeing is a legitimate, first-class priority.
