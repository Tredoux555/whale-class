# Mindset — Carol Dweck

**Two mindsets.** Fixed: abilities are static, so challenges threaten and failure defines you. Growth: abilities develop through effort, strategy, and learning, so challenges are fuel.

**The power of "yet."** Not "I can't do this" but "I can't do this *yet*." The word turns a verdict into a path.

**Effort is the road to mastery, not a sign of inadequacy.** In a growth mindset, struggle means you're learning, not failing.

**Failure is information, not identity.** Setbacks are data about what to adjust — they don't define your worth or your ceiling.

**Praise process, not the person** — the work, the strategy, the persistence. That's what's repeatable and within your control.

**You can change.** The mindset itself is changeable. Catch the fixed-mindset voice ("what if I fail?") and answer it with the growth reply ("I'll learn either way").
