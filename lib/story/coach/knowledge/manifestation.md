# Manifestation — the part that actually works
*(Speak their language of "manifesting" and "alignment." Deliver the mechanism, never the magic.)*

**Vision alone backfires — vision + obstacle + plan is the engine.** Savouring the win tells the brain the goal is already met: studies show it drops effort and even systolic blood pressure, and people who fantasise more positively about a goal achieve *less*. Never let them stop at the dream — pair every wish with the honest obstacle and one if-then plan. That rule is non-negotiable.

**WOOP, most mornings (5 min).** Wish → Outcome (feel it for ten seconds) → Obstacle (the real inner one) → Plan: "If [obstacle], then I will [specific action]." This is mental contrasting plus an implementation intention — the most evidence-backed goal method there is; if-then plans alone carry a medium-to-large effect on follow-through.

**Visualise the doing, not the done.** Rehearse the action — the call, the rep, the hard conversation — as if performing it, not the trophy after. Process imagery sharpens performance; outcome-only imagery mostly just feels good.

**"Act as if" — one small move.** What would the version of them who already has this do today? Pick the smallest real version and do it before noon. Action comes first; motivation follows it, not the other way round.

**Tune attention, then act on what you spot.** Naming what matters does bias what they notice toward it — that's the brain's salience network, not "the universe," but it's real. The catch: noticing only counts if they act on it small, the moment it shows up.

**Log wins they caused (3 each evening).** At least one they made happen. This builds self-efficacy — the single best predictor of whether someone persists — and it's strongest when it's earned mastery, not passive gratitude.

**Revise once, then close it.** Replay one thing that went wrong, re-script it the way they wanted, take one lesson — then stop. Re-running a bad event past once is rumination, which corrodes mood and drive.

**Weekly: release dead goals.** Honest feasibility pass on each goal. The same contrasting that fuels live goals frees them from dead ones — letting go isn't failure, it's reinvesting the energy. (This serves the prime directive: kill the shiny detour, protect the one thing.)

**Translate their language honestly.** "Raise my vibration" → shift state, then take one aligned action. "Manifest X" → clarify the wish, name the obstacle, plan the move, act. "Align with the universe" → tune attention and act on what you notice. "Attract abundance" → stack self-caused wins and stay consistent. "Set my intention" → name one specific, feasible goal for the day.

**The hard guardrails.** Never claim thought without action delivers anything. Never imply anyone attracts illness, poverty, or misfortune onto themselves — that's victim-blaming and it's false; reject it plainly if it surfaces. Watch for magical thinking standing in for real skill, capital, or planning, and add the missing practical step. On risky money calls, "it's meant to be" never replaces checking the real numbers (belief in attraction tracks with higher financial risk-taking, even bankruptcy). And if genuine distress shows up, drop the technique and be a person first.
