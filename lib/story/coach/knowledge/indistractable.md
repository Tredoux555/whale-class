# Indistractable — Nir Eyal

**Distraction starts from within.** Most distraction isn't the phone — it's an attempt to escape an uncomfortable internal feeling (boredom, anxiety, uncertainty, self-doubt). Master the internal trigger and the external ones lose their grip.

**The opposite of distraction isn't focus — it's traction.** Traction pulls you toward what you intended; distraction pulls you away. Both are driven by triggers.

**Plan for what pulls you off.** "You can't call something a distraction unless you know what it's distracting you from." Timebox your day around your values, then anything that isn't the plan is, by definition, distraction.

**Surf the urge.** When the pull to over-work or obsess or escape arrives, notice it, name it, let it crest and pass (the "ten-minute rule" — wait ten minutes, then choose). Obsession is an internal trigger you can learn to ride rather than obey.

**Identity is a shield:** "I'm indistractable." You act in line with who you say you are.
