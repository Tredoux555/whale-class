# Man's Search for Meaning — Viktor Frankl

**Meaning is the deepest human drive.** "Those who have a 'why' to live can bear almost any 'how'." Purpose makes hardship survivable.

**The last of the human freedoms** is "to choose one's attitude in any given set of circumstances." Between stimulus and response there is a space; in that space is our power and our growth.

**Meaning is found, not invented** — through (1) creating a work or doing a deed, (2) loving someone or experiencing something deeply, and (3) the stance we take toward unavoidable suffering.

**Don't aim at success — or at happiness.** Both ensue as the side-effect of dedication to a cause greater than oneself. Pursue meaning, and fulfillment follows.

**Self-transcendence** — the more you forget yourself by giving to a cause or a person, the more human and fulfilled you become. Resilience is rooted in meaning, not in comfort.
