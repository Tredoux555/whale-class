# Caption Writing Mastery

## The 3-Part Formula (Works on All Platforms)

### 1. HOOK (First Line)
**Goal:** Stop the scroll in 2 seconds

**Hook Types:**
- **Question:** "Ever spend 2 hours on progress reports?"
- **Bold Statement:** "Montessori teachers are quitting. Here's why."
- **Relatable Pain:** "Your classroom has 20 kids. Your to-do list has 200 items."
- **Number/List:** "3 things I wish I knew before teaching Montessori"
- **Story Start:** "A parent asked me why her child wasn't reading yet..."

**Hook Formula for Montree:**
```
Problem → Solution tease
"Teachers spend X hours on admin. I built an app to fix that..."
```

### 2. VALUE (Middle Section)
**Goal:** Deliver on the hook promise

**Keep it:**
- **Scannable:** Short sentences, line breaks
- **Specific:** "Cut report writing from 2 hours to 15 minutes" (not "save time")
- **Actionable:** What can they DO with this info?

**Structure options:**
- **Story:** Beginning → Middle → End
- **List:** Bullet points (but write naturally, not forced)
- **Before/After:** Old way vs New way
- **How-To:** Step 1, Step 2, Step 3

### 3. CALL-TO-ACTION (Last Line)
**Goal:** Tell them exactly what to do next

**CTA Types:**
- **Engagement:** "Tag a teacher who needs this 👇"
- **Save:** "Save this for later 📌"
- **Comment:** "Drop a ✋ if you've dealt with this"
- **Link:** "Try it free at montree.xyz"
- **Share:** "Share this with your teaching community"

**Pro tip:** Only ONE CTA per caption. Multiple CTAs = confused audience = no action.

## Platform-Specific Caption Strategies

### Instagram
- **Length:** 125-150 words ideal (longer is fine if valuable)
- **Line breaks:** Every 2-3 sentences for readability
- **Emojis:** 3-5 per caption (not excessive)
- **First line visible:** Make it count (cuts off at ~125 chars)

**Example:**
```
Montessori teachers spend hours on admin. I built Montree to fix that.

Track progress across all 5 areas in seconds. Generate parent reports with photos. Plan your curriculum visually.

All free. Built by a teacher, for teachers.

Try it → montree.xyz

📚 Montessori education
⚡️ Teacher tools
👨‍👩‍👧 Parent communication
```

### TikTok
- **Length:** 100-150 chars (keep SHORT)
- **Hashtags:** 3-5, integrated naturally
- **Voice:** Conversational, like texting a friend
- **Emojis:** Use sparingly

**Example:**
```
POV: You're a Montessori teacher who just found a free classroom management app 😍

Game changer for progress tracking → link in bio

#montessoriteacher #teacherlife #teachertok
```

### Facebook (Groups)
- **Length:** NO LIMIT (longer = better for groups)
- **Story format:** People read full stories in groups
- **Value-first:** Less salesy, more helpful
- **Natural link:** Don't use "link in bio" (paste actual URL)

**Example:**
```
I'm a Montessori teacher and I built Montree, a free classroom management app for tracking progress across all 5 areas, generating parent reports with photos, and planning curriculum. Free for schools to use.

[Explain the problem you solved, share your story]

Try it at montree.xyz

Follow us: Instagram @montreexyz | TikTok @montreexyz | facebook.com/montreexyz
```

### LinkedIn
- **Length:** 150-300 words (professionals read more)
- **Professional tone:** Less casual than Instagram
- **Data/Results:** Share metrics, case studies
- **Thought leadership:** What did you learn building this?

**Example:**
```
After 5 years teaching Montessori, I noticed a pattern:

Teachers spending 40% of their time on admin instead of teaching.

So I built Montree - a free classroom management app that automates:
→ Progress tracking across all 5 Montessori areas
→ Photo-based parent reports
→ Visual curriculum planning

Early results: Teachers cutting admin time by 60%.

For educators who want to spend more time teaching, less time on paperwork.

Try it: montree.xyz
```

## Caption Tone Guide

### Montree Brand Voice
- **Friendly:** Like a colleague, not a salesperson
- **Empathetic:** We KNOW teacher struggles (because we lived them)
- **Clear:** No jargon, no fluff
- **Inspiring:** Celebrate teachers, uplift the profession

**Avoid:**
- Corporate speak ("leveraging synergies")
- Overpromising ("revolutionize education!")
- Guilt-tripping ("If you don't use this, you're failing")

## Advanced Caption Techniques

### Pattern Interrupts
Break expected patterns to grab attention:
```
Normal: "Our app helps teachers save time"
Pattern interrupt: "I deleted 47 hours of work. Here's why."
```

### The Curiosity Gap
Tease the value without giving it all away:
```
"The one Montessori material every 3-6 classroom needs (but most teachers forget)"
```

### Social Proof Integration
Weave in testimonials naturally:
```
"A teacher from Texas messaged me: 'This cut my report writing from 3 hours to 20 minutes.' Here's how..."
```

### The "Scroll-Stopper" Test
Read your first line out loud. Would YOU stop scrolling? If not, rewrite.

---

## Caption Writing Workflow

1. **Draft fast** (5 minutes) - Don't overthink it
2. **Strengthen the hook** (2 minutes) - Make first line irresistible
3. **Cut fluff** (2 minutes) - Delete filler words, tighten sentences
4. **Add CTA** (1 minute) - What do you want them to do?
5. **Read aloud** (1 minute) - Does it sound natural?

**Total time:** 10-15 minutes per caption
