# PART A — For WebClaude: hashtags + descriptions (per platform)

## How to use
For each video, WebClaude should: write a **hook-first caption** (the first line is the scroll-stopper), pick one description angle below, fill the [brackets], and attach the platform's hashtag set. Keep the brand voice warm, practical, and educator-to-educator (not salesy).

**Brand hashtags (use on every post):** `#Montree` `#MeetAstra` `#MontessoriAI`
**Call-to-action options:** "Link in bio" · "Comment **MONTREE** and I'll send it" · "DM us 'demo'".

### Description angles (pick one per video, swap in the video's specifics)
1. **Problem → relief:** "[Hours spent on observations / parent reports / chasing teachers] — gone. Here's how [school name/teacher] uses Montree to [outcome]."
2. **Feature spotlight:** "Meet Astra, the AI chief-of-staff inside Montree. Ask it about any child, any classroom, any parent conversation — and it answers from your real classroom records."
3. **Day-in-the-life / time saved:** "What a Montessori principal's Monday looks like with Montree: [3 quick beats]. ~[X] hours back every week."
4. **Outcome / proof:** "From '[pain]' to '[result]' in one term. This is what Montree does for a [N]-classroom school."

Each caption: **1 hook line → 1–2 value lines → 1 CTA → hashtags.**

# PART B — Best posting times (Beijing time) + what triggers the algorithm

## The two golden Beijing windows (US-primary audience)
| Beijing window | Hits US audience at | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **06:00 – 11:00** (early morning) | US **evening** the night before (~6–11pm ET) | **TikTok + Reels** (evening scroll peaks) |
| **21:00 – 01:00** (late evening) | US **morning–midday** (~9am–1pm ET) | **Facebook + Instagram feed**, general best window |

> Anchored to **US Eastern**. West Coast runs ~3h later, so these windows comfortably cover ET→CT and bleed into PT.

## Per-platform specifics (best **days** + Beijing **times**)
| Platform | Best days | Beijing time window | (US local equivalent) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Instagram / Reels** | **Tue–Thu** (avoid Sat/Sun) | **06:00–10:00** & **22:00–00:00** | Reels evening 6–11pm ET; feed Tue 1–7pm / Wed 12–9pm ET |
| **TikTok** | **Saturday** (then Mon/Sun) — post **Fri night/Sat AM Beijing** to land US Saturday | **07:00–11:00** & **18:00–22:00** | US evening 7–11pm ET; mid-day 10–11am ET |
| **Facebook** | **Wed–Thu** (weekends quiet) | **21:00–00:00** | Wed/Thu 9am–12pm ET |

Rule of thumb if you only remember one thing: **post Tue–Thu around Beijing 9–11pm, and Sat around Beijing 8–10am.**

## What actually triggers reach (2026 signals)
**TikTok (where virality is most winnable):**
- **Completion rate is king (~40–50% of the algorithm).** You now need roughly **70%+ watch-through** to go viral (up from ~50% in 2024). → Keep videos **tight (15–30s)**, front-load the payoff, and make the ending **loop** back to the start.
- **Hook in the first 1–3 seconds** — on-screen text + a question or bold claim. Most drop-off happens here.
- **Shares & saves > likes.** Explicitly prompt them: *"Save this for your next staff meeting"* / *"Send this to your director."* A high share-to-view ratio outperforms raw views.
- **Followers-first testing (new in 2026):** your video is shown to a slice of *your followers* first; if they engage, it goes wider. → Build a real follower base and post for *them*, not just strangers.
- **First-hour engagement matters more than ever** — hence the timing windows above. Reply to every comment in the first hour to spark more.
- **Niche consistency:** posting across 3+ unrelated topics = ~45% lower reach. Stay tightly on **Montessori / school-ops / edtech.**
- **Original audio + production quality** now factor in; trending sounds still help discovery.

**Instagram (Reels):**
- Ranks on **interest, recency, relationship, and how often the user opens IG.** **Likes are down-weighted**; **saves, shares/sends, and DMs** are the strong signals — design posts people *send to a colleague*.
- Use **trending audio**, on-screen captions, a strong **cover frame**, and keep Reels **loopable**.

**Facebook:**
- Feed reach is weak for cold pages — **distribute through Groups** and encourage **shares/comments**. Native video (uploaded, not a link) gets far more reach than a link post.

**Across all three:**
- **Consistency beats virality** — 3–5 posts/week, same niche, compounds.
- **Captions/subtitles always** (sound-off viewing).
- First **comment-bait** line ("What's your biggest admin time-sink?") to seed early comments.
- Post **native** (don't cross-post with another platform's watermark — both TikTok and Reels suppress watermarked re-uploads).
