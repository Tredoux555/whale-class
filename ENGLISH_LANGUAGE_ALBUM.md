# INTRODUCTION & PHILOSOPHY {#introduction}

## The Montessori Approach to Language

Maria Montessori discovered that children have a natural, sensitive period for language acquisition between ages 2-6. During this time, children absorb language effortlessly when given the right environment and materials.

### Key Principles

**Writing Before Reading:** Montessori observed that children naturally want to write before they read. Writing is analysis (breaking sounds into symbols), while reading is synthesis (combining symbols into meaning). Children find analysis easier.

**Phonetic Foundation:** We teach letter SOUNDS, not names. A child cannot read "cat" by saying "see-ay-tee" but CAN read it by blending /k/-/a/-/t/.

**Multi-Sensory Learning:** Every material engages multiple senses:
- **Visual:** Seeing the letter shape
- **Auditory:** Hearing the sound
- **Tactile:** Feeling the sandpaper texture
- **Kinesthetic:** Moving the hand in the letter pattern

**Isolation of Difficulty:** Each material focuses on ONE skill. Sandpaper letters teach sound-symbol correspondence. Metal insets teach pencil control. We don't combine difficulties.

### Presentation

**Setup:**
1. Select 5-6 real objects from one category (e.g., fruits)
2. Place objects in an attractive basket
3. Have a soft work rug ready

**Three-Period Lesson:**

*Period 1 - Introduction (This is...)*
1. Invite the child: "I have some beautiful objects to show you."
2. Place rug on floor, sit beside child
3. Take out one object at a time
4. "This is an apple." (Place on rug)
5. "This is a banana." (Place next to apple)
6. "This is an orange." (Place in row)
7. Pause. Let the child absorb.

*Period 2 - Recognition (Show me...)*
1. "Can you show me the apple?"
2. "Put the banana in my hand."
3. "Point to the orange."
4. Vary requests: "Give the apple to me slowly/quickly"
5. If child makes error, don't correct. Simply continue.

*Period 3 - Recall (What is this?)*
1. Point to an object: "What is this?"
2. Child names the object
3. If child cannot name it, return to Period 2

**Extensions:**
- Blindfold game: Identify objects by touch
- Memory game: Remove one object, child names missing item
- Sorting: Group by color, size, or category

| Small rug | BUY | `è’™æ°å·¥ä½œæ¯¯ å°å·` | Define workspace |

### Presentation

**Stage 1: Beginning Sounds**

*Setup:*
- Place 3-5 small objects on a tray
- Objects should have clearly different beginning sounds
- Example: apple, ball, cat, dog

*Presentation:*
1. "Let's play I Spy!"
2. "I spy with my little eye something that begins with /a/..."
3. Wait. Give child time to scan objects.
4. If child picks correct object: "Yes! Apple begins with /a/. /a/-/a/-apple."
5. If incorrect: "Let's listen... /b/-/b/-ball. Ball begins with /b/. Try again. /a/..."

**Stage 2: Ending Sounds**
1. "I spy something that ENDS with /t/..."
2. "Cat! Yes, ca-/t/. Cat ends with /t/."

**Stage 3: Middle Sounds**
1. "I spy something with /a/ in the MIDDLE..."
2. "Cat! C-/a/-t. Cat has /a/ in the middle."

### Critical Notes
- Say the SOUND not the letter NAME
- /m/ not "em", /s/ not "ess"
- For consonants, avoid adding "uh" sound
- /b/ not "buh", /t/ not "tuh"

### Presentation

**Rhyming Picture Match:**
1. Spread 6-8 picture cards face up
2. "These pictures have friends that sound the same at the end."

### Presentation â­ THE THREE-PERIOD LESSON

**Preparation:**
- Child should have played "I Spy" successfully with beginning sounds
- Select THREE letters to introduce:
  - Two consonants + one vowel
  - Letters that look AND sound different
  - Start with useful letters: s, a, t, m, c, r, i, p

**Period 1: Introduction ("This is...")**

1. Sit at a table with child on your dominant side
2. Stack three letters face-down in upper right
3. Turn over first letter, place in front of child

4. *Tracing:* 
   - Use index and middle fingers together
   - Trace the letter slowly, exactly as you would write it
   - Say the SOUND as you complete the trace: "mmmmm"

5. *Repeat twice more:* 
   - Trace... "mmmmm"
   - Trace... "mmmmm"

6. *Invite child:*
   - "Would you like to trace it?"
   - Child traces and says sound
   - If incorrect sound, simply model again

7. *Connect to words:*
   - "Mmmmm... what words start with mmmmm?"
   - "Moon! Mmmmm-moon!"
   - "Monkey! Mmmmm-monkey!"

8. Move letter to upper left. Repeat with next two letters.

**Period 2: Recognition ("Show me...")**

1. Place all three letters randomly on table
2. "Show me the letter that says /m/."
3. Child points to or touches the letter
4. "Trace it and say its sound."

5. Vary requests:
   - "Put the /s/ on your head."
   - "Give me the /a/."
   - "Hide the /m/ under your hands."

6. If child chooses incorrectly:
   - DO NOT say "No" or "Wrong"
   - Simply continue: "This one says /m/. Trace it with me."
   - Return to Period 1 if needed

**Period 3: Recall ("What is this?")**

1. Point to a letter
2. "What sound does this make?"
3. Child says the sound
4. "Yes! And can you trace it?"

5. If child cannot recall:
   - Return to Period 2
   - Come back to Period 3 another day

### Presentation

**First Presentation: "Making Words"**

3. Take a small object (e.g., toy cat):
   - "This is a cat. Let's make the word 'cat'."
   - "What sound do you hear at the beginning of cat? /k/-/k/-cat."

4. "What sound comes next? C-/a/-t. /a/!"
   - Child finds 'a', places next to 'c'

5. "What sound at the end? Ca-/t/. /t/!"
   - Child finds 't', places to complete word

6. "You made 'cat'! /k/-/a/-/t/... cat!"

7. Run finger under word left to right as you blend sounds
